Getting Started

What to Put on Your Business Website

5 min read

Get a Professional Getting Started Website in 60 Minutes →

Let's get real for a moment: your business website is like the front door to your shop. You want it to be inviting, informative, and easy to navigate. But what exactly should you put on your website to make it truly shine? The answer lies in understanding your audience and delivering exactly what they need. Whether you're running a cozy café in the heart of downtown or a boutique in a vibrant neighborhood, your website should reflect your brand's personality and connect with your community. Let's dive into the essential elements that will help your website stand out.

Essential Pages for Your Business Website

Your website should serve as a hub for all the information potential customers need. Here’s what you absolutely must include:

  • Home Page: This is your digital storefront. Make sure it clearly conveys who you are and what you do. Use engaging visuals and a catchy tagline that captures the essence of your brand. Consider adding a brief introductory video or a slideshow showcasing your best products or services.
  • About Us: Share your story, mission, and values. This helps build trust and connect with your audience. Include a personal touch, like the journey that led you to start your business, or highlight the team behind the scenes. Sharing photos of your team at work or participating in community events can add warmth and authenticity.
  • Services or Products: Provide detailed descriptions of what you offer, including pricing and any special deals. Use high-quality images and consider categorizing your offerings for easier navigation. If applicable, include an online booking system or e-commerce functionality to streamline the purchasing process.
  • Contact Information: Make it easy for visitors to reach out. Include a phone number, email address, and a contact form. You might also add a live chat feature for instant communication. Don't forget to include your physical address and a map if you have a storefront, as this can help customers find you more easily.
  • Testimonials: Showcase reviews from satisfied customers to build credibility and attract new clients. Use real names, and, if possible, photos of the customers to make testimonials more relatable. You can also create case studies from particularly successful projects to highlight your capabilities.

Additional Features to Enhance User Experience

Once you have the basics down, consider adding these features to improve user experience:

  • Blog: Share valuable content related to your industry. This can help with SEO and keeps visitors coming back. Write about tips, trends, or local happenings that resonate with your audience. Engaging content can establish your expertise and keep your website fresh.
  • Gallery: If your business is visual, include a portfolio or gallery of your work. This is especially important for photographers, artists, and designers. Use high-resolution images and categorize them for easy browsing. You might even consider adding a 'behind-the-scenes' section to give customers insight into your creative process.
  • FAQ Section: Anticipate questions your customers might have and provide clear answers to save time for both sides. This could cover topics like service procedures, return policies, or even tips on choosing the right product. Not only does this improve user experience, but it can also reduce the number of inquiries you receive.
  • Newsletter Sign-Up: Encourage visitors to subscribe to your updates. This helps you build a loyal customer base. Offer incentives like discounts or exclusive content for signing up. Regular newsletters can keep your audience informed about new products, events, or promotions, fostering a sense of community.
  • Social Media Links: Connect your website with your social media profiles to enhance engagement. Make it easy for visitors to follow you on platforms like Instagram, Facebook, or Twitter. Regularly update these channels with content that showcases your brand's personality and connects with your audience.

Local Flavor: Make Your Website Relevant to Your Community

If you’re running a business in a specific neighborhood or city, don’t forget to tailor your content to reflect local culture. Here’s how:

  • Local SEO: Use local keywords that potential customers might search for, like "best coffee in [Your Neighborhood]" or "top florist in [City]." Consider writing blog posts about local events or attractions that relate to your business. This will not only help with search engine rankings but also strengthen your connection to the community.
  • Community Involvement: Highlight any local events or charity work you participate in. This shows you care about your community. Feature upcoming local events on your website or share stories of past events where your business made a difference. Collaborating with local organizations can also enhance your visibility.
  • Local Imagery: Use photos of your business and team that reflect your surroundings and resonate with local customers. Showcase local landmarks or popular spots in your imagery to create a familiar environment for visitors. This can make your website feel more relatable and rooted in the local culture.
