Getting Started

What to Do After Your Website Launches

5 min read

Get a Professional Getting Started Website in 60 Minutes →

Imagine the moment you hit 'publish' on your website; it feels like a marathon finally coming to a close. But hold on—this is just the beginning of a new journey. After your website launches, there are essential steps to ensure it not only survives but thrives. From refining your content to attracting your target audience, every action you take post-launch is crucial for your website's success. Let's dive into what you should focus on after taking that exciting leap.

Celebrate Your Launch

First things first, take a moment to appreciate your hard work! Celebrate your achievement with your team or family. This is a milestone worth recognizing, so consider hosting a small gathering or virtual party. After the celebrations, it’s time to spread the word:

  • Share the launch on social media: Craft a compelling message and share it across platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn to inform your audience and generate excitement.
  • Send a newsletter: Reach out to your existing contacts with an enthusiastic email announcement detailing what your website offers. Include a direct link to your site, encouraging them to visit and provide feedback.
  • Engage with early visitors: As your first visitors arrive, actively ask for their opinions through quick polls or feedback forms. Their insights can help you identify areas for improvement and make your site more user-friendly.

Test Everything

Now that your website is live, it’s time to ensure everything is functioning as it should. Testing is crucial to providing a positive user experience, and it’s an ongoing process:

  • Check that all links are working properly: Go through your site and click every link to ensure they lead to the correct pages. Broken links can frustrate visitors and harm your SEO.
  • Verify that forms are submitting correctly: Test all forms, whether they are for contact, subscriptions, or purchases. Make sure you receive the information as intended and that users receive confirmation.
  • Ensure your website displays well on various devices and browsers: Use tools like BrowserStack to see how your site appears on different devices. A mobile-friendly design is essential, as many users will access your site via smartphones or tablets.

Monitor Performance

Setting up analytics is your next step. Understanding how visitors interact with your website will guide your future decisions and help you make informed adjustments:

  • Install Google Analytics or another tracking tool: This allows you to access valuable data about your audience, including demographic information and how they found your site.
  • Monitor metrics: Keep an eye on key performance indicators such as page views, bounce rates, and user behavior. For example, if you notice a high bounce rate on a particular page, it might indicate that the content isn’t engaging enough.
  • Adjust your content based on the data received: Use insights from your analytics to refine your content strategy. If certain topics attract more visitors, consider creating more related content to maintain that interest.

Promote Your Website

Your website needs visitors! Here are some effective strategies to promote it and increase your reach:

  • Consider running targeted ads: Set up campaigns on social media platforms like Facebook and Instagram, focusing on audiences that align with your niche. Use eye-catching visuals and clear calls-to-action to drive clicks.
  • Optimize your site for SEO: Conduct keyword research to ensure your content aligns with what potential visitors are searching for. Use tools like SEMrush or Ahrefs to refine your strategy and improve your site's visibility.
  • Engage with local communities: If your business has a local focus, participate in local events or sponsor community activities. Building relationships with local businesses and organizations can enhance your word-of-mouth referrals.

Plan for Continuous Improvement

Your website isn't a static entity; it should evolve over time. Here’s how to keep it fresh and relevant:

  • Regularly update content: Schedule a routine for content updates—whether it's blog posts, product listings, or service descriptions—to reflect current offerings and insights. This not only helps with SEO but also keeps your audience engaged.
  • Solicit and implement feedback from users: Encourage visitors to share their thoughts, and consider integrating their suggestions where feasible. This builds a sense of community and trust.
  • Stay informed about industry trends: Subscribe to relevant newsletters, attend webinars, or join online forums to keep your knowledge fresh. Incorporate new features or design elements that align with industry advancements to keep your site modern and appealing.

Launching your website is just the first step in a long journey. With the right strategy in place, you can ensure it grows and evolves. Remember, this is an ongoing process that requires your attention and creativity.
