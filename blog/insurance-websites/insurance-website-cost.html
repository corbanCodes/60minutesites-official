How Much Does an Insurance Website Cost in 2026

7 min read

Insurance agents researching website options quickly discover that pricing varies dramatically. You can find options ranging from free DIY builders to custom development projects costing tens of thousands of dollars. Understanding what you get at each price point helps you make an informed decision for your agency.

This guide breaks down the realistic costs of insurance agency websites in 2026, including both upfront expenses and ongoing costs that are easy to overlook.

Insurance Website Cost Overview

Before diving into specific options, here is a quick overview of what insurance agents typically spend on websites:

  • DIY Website Builders: $0-30/month
  • Template-Based Professional Sites: $40-150/month
  • Custom Website Development: $3,000-15,000+ upfront plus ongoing costs
  • Agency Management System Websites: Often included or $50-200/month additional

The right choice depends on your budget, technical comfort level, time availability, and growth goals. Let us examine each option in detail.

DIY Website Builders

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and GoDaddy Website Builder allow anyone to create a basic website without coding knowledge. These are the most affordable options for getting online.

Typical Costs

  • Free tiers available with platform branding and limitations
  • Basic paid plans: $12-20/month
  • Business plans with custom domain and more features: $20-35/month
  • Domain registration: $10-15/year

What You Get

DIY builders provide templates, drag-and-drop editing, hosting, and basic features like contact forms. Most include SSL certificates and mobile responsiveness. You handle all the setup, design decisions, content creation, and ongoing maintenance.

Hidden Costs to Consider

The biggest hidden cost is your time. Building a professional-looking website takes hours, even with easy-to-use tools. If you value your time at $100/hour and spend 20 hours building your site, that is $2,000 in opportunity cost not reflected in the monthly fee.

Additional costs may include premium templates, stock photos, third-party form tools, and plugins for features the basic platform lacks.

Best For

DIY builders work for agents who have time to invest, enjoy learning new tools, and have a limited budget. They are also suitable for agents just starting out who want to establish a basic online presence quickly.

Template-Based Professional Sites

Template-based services provide professionally designed websites built for specific industries. You get a polished site without starting from scratch, often with setup assistance or done-for-you implementation.

Typical Costs

  • Monthly plans: $40-150/month
  • Usually includes hosting, updates, and basic support
  • Setup fees may apply: $0-500
  • Domain registration may be included or separate

What You Get

Template-based services provide insurance-specific designs that already include the pages, forms, and features agencies need. The heavy lifting of design and development is done for you. Many services include content suggestions or starter text tailored to insurance.

Hidden Costs to Consider

Some services charge extra for customization beyond the template. Adding features, changing layouts, or integrating with other tools may incur additional fees. Review what is included in the base price versus what costs extra.

Best For

Template-based solutions work for agents who want a professional site without the time investment of DIY or the cost of custom development. They are ideal for small to mid-size agencies that need to get online quickly with a proven format.

Custom Website Development

Custom development means hiring a designer and developer to build a website specifically for your agency. Every element is created to your specifications.

Typical Costs

  • Basic custom site: $3,000-5,000
  • Mid-range custom site: $5,000-10,000
  • Advanced custom site with integrations: $10,000-25,000+
  • Ongoing maintenance: $100-500/month
  • Hosting: $20-100/month

What You Get

Custom development provides a completely unique website designed around your specific needs, branding, and workflow. You can include custom features, integrations with agency management systems, and functionality that template solutions cannot provide.

Hidden Costs to Consider

Custom sites require ongoing maintenance, security updates, and periodic redesigns. Developer hourly rates for changes and updates add up over time. If your original developer becomes unavailable, finding someone to maintain custom code can be expensive.

The project management time on your end is also significant. Custom development requires your input throughout the process, often taking weeks or months to complete.

Best For

Custom development makes sense for large agencies with substantial budgets, unique requirements that templates cannot accommodate, or specific integration needs. It is also appropriate when your website is a major competitive differentiator.

Agency Management System Websites

Some agency management systems include website functionality or partner with website providers. These solutions may integrate directly with your client data and workflows.

Typical Costs

  • Sometimes included in AMS subscription
  • Add-on pricing: $50-200/month
  • May require specific AMS tier or package

What You Get

AMS-integrated websites may offer features like client portals, certificate requests, or payment processing that connect directly to your management system. The integration can streamline operations by reducing duplicate data entry.

Hidden Costs to Consider

AMS websites may have limited design flexibility compared to standalone options. If you change management systems, you may need a new website. Features and quality vary significantly between AMS providers.

Best For

AMS websites work for agencies that prioritize operational integration over design flexibility. They make sense when client self-service features are important and you plan to stay with your current management system long-term.

Total Cost of Ownership

When comparing options, calculate the total cost over three to five years, not just the initial expense. Here is an example comparison:

DIY Builder (5-year cost)

$25/month x 60 months = $1,500, plus 20+ hours of your time for setup and ongoing maintenance.

Template-Based Service (5-year cost)

$75/month x 60 months = $4,500, minimal time investment after initial setup.

Custom Development (5-year cost)

$7,500 initial build + $200/month maintenance x 60 = $19,500, plus project management time and a likely redesign around year 4.

The cheapest option is not always the best value when you factor in time, results, and long-term costs.

What Should You Spend?

There is no universal answer, but here are guidelines based on agency size and goals:

New or small agencies: $40-100/month for a professional template-based site that does not consume your time. Focus on building your book of business, not your website.

Established agencies: $75-150/month for a quality template solution, or $5,000-10,000 for custom development if you have specific needs templates cannot meet.

Large agencies: Custom development often makes sense when budget allows and you have unique requirements. Budget $10,000+ for initial development plus ongoing maintenance.

Making Your Decision

Consider these questions when choosing your website investment level:

  • How much is your time worth? Building and maintaining a DIY site takes hours.
  • How important is your website to lead generation? A better site that generates more quotes may pay for itself quickly.
  • Do you have unique needs that templates cannot accommodate?
  • What is your comfort level with technology and design decisions?
  • How quickly do you need to be online?

For most insurance agencies, a template-based professional site offers the best balance of cost, quality, and time investment. You get a polished result without the expense of custom development or the time sink of DIY building.
