How Much Does a Barber Website Cost in 2026

7 min read

The cost of a barber website varies dramatically depending on how you build it. You can spend nothing with a free DIY builder, a few hundred dollars per year with a professional template, or several thousand on custom development. Understanding these options helps you make the right investment for your shop.

This guide breaks down realistic costs for each approach, explains what affects pricing, and helps you decide which option makes sense for your situation.

Barber Website Cost Breakdown

Option 1: Free DIY Website Builders

Initial cost: $0

Annual cost: $0

Platforms like Wix, Weebly, and Google Sites offer free tiers that technically allow you to create a website at no cost. However, "free" comes with significant limitations.

Free websites typically include the platform's branding and ads, use the platform's subdomain rather than your own domain name, have limited storage and bandwidth, and lack essential features like custom forms or booking integration.

For a barber shop trying to establish credibility, a website with "yourshop.wix.com" as the address and third-party ads scattered throughout sends the wrong message. Most barbers outgrow free options quickly.

Option 2: DIY Website Builders (Paid Plans)

Initial cost: $0-150 (for domain and setup)

Monthly cost: $12-40/month

Annual cost: $150-500

Paid plans on platforms like Squarespace, Wix, or GoDaddy remove most limitations of free tiers. You get your own domain name, no third-party ads, more storage, and access to better templates and features.

The catch is time. Building a professional website yourself, even with a drag-and-drop builder, takes hours of learning the platform, choosing and customizing a template, writing content, and troubleshooting issues. For barbers already working long hours, this time investment can be significant.

Results also vary based on design skills. A barber with an eye for design might create something impressive. Someone without that background might end up with a site that looks homemade.

Option 3: Professional Templates with Setup

Initial cost: $0-200 (setup fees vary)

Monthly cost: $40-100/month

Annual cost: $500-1,200

Template-based solutions designed specifically for barber shops offer a middle ground. You get a professionally designed website built for your industry, usually with setup assistance and ongoing support.

These solutions typically include hosting, a domain name, mobile-responsive design, booking integration, and templates designed specifically for barber shops. The monthly cost is higher than DIY, but you save significant time and get a more polished result.

This approach works well for barbers who want a professional online presence without the time investment of building from scratch or the cost of custom development.

Option 4: Custom Website Development

Initial cost: $2,000-10,000+

Monthly cost: $50-200/month (hosting and maintenance)

Annual cost: $600-2,400 (after initial build)

A custom-built website gives you complete control over design and functionality. A web developer creates something unique to your shop, tailored to your specific needs and brand.

Custom development makes sense for established shops with significant budgets, unique requirements that templates cannot accommodate, or those wanting to stand out with completely original design. For most barber shops, especially those just starting their online presence, custom development is overkill.

Additional Costs to Consider

Domain Name

Cost: $10-50/year

Your domain name (like yourshopname.com) requires annual renewal. Common extensions like .com typically cost $10-15 per year. Some premium or short domains cost more. Many website services include a domain with their plans.

Online Booking Software

Cost: $0-50/month

If your website solution does not include booking, you will need separate software. Options like Square Appointments offer free tiers for solo barbers, while platforms like Booksy or Vagaro charge monthly fees for additional features.

Professional Photography

Cost: $200-800

Professional photos of your shop and work make a significant difference. This is a one-time investment that pays off across your website, social media, and marketing materials. Many barbers start with smartphone photos and invest in professional photography later.

Logo Design

Cost: $50-500

If you do not have a professional logo, you will need one for your website. Options range from budget logo generators to professional designers. A quality logo is used across all your branding, making it a worthwhile investment.

What Affects Barber Website Cost

Number of Pages

A simple five-page site costs less than a complex site with individual service pages, team bios, a blog, and multiple galleries. More pages mean more content creation, design work, and maintenance.

Custom Features

Standard features like contact forms and booking buttons are included in most solutions. Custom features like loyalty programs, product sales, or complex scheduling systems add cost.

Design Complexity

A clean, template-based design costs far less than custom graphics, animations, and unique layouts. For most barber shops, professional templates provide all the design sophistication needed.

Ongoing Support

Some solutions include ongoing support and updates. Others leave you to handle everything yourself after launch. Support has value, especially if you are not technically inclined.

Calculating Your Return on Investment

The real question is not what a website costs, but what it returns. Consider: if a website helps you book just one additional client per week at an average ticket of $35, that is $1,820 in additional annual revenue.

A website that costs $500-1,000 per year pays for itself many times over with modest increases in bookings. Factor in reduced no-shows from online booking reminders, time saved not answering routine phone calls, and the ability to attract clients searching online, and the return becomes even clearer.

The barber shops struggling most are not those that invested in their online presence. They are the ones that ignored it while competitors captured the clients searching online.

Choosing the Right Investment Level

For a new barber shop or one just establishing an online presence, a professional template solution in the $500-1,200 annual range offers the best balance of quality, cost, and time savings.

For established shops with strong revenue, custom development might make sense if you have specific needs templates cannot meet. But for most barber shops, a well-designed template does everything needed at a fraction of the cost.

Avoid free or ultra-cheap options if you can afford better. The damage to your professional image from a subpar website often exceeds whatever money you save.
