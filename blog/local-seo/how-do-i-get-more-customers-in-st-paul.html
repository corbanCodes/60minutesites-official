How Do I Get More Customers in St. Paul?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Getting customers in St. Paul can feel like a tough gig, especially with all the competition. However, with the right strategies in place, you can stand out and grow your local service business effectively in this vibrant urban landscape.

Build a Professional Website

Your website is the foundation of your online presence. It needs to look sharp and function smoothly. Here’s what actually works:

  • Make sure your website is mobile-friendly; most people in St. Paul search on their phones, especially when on the go.
  • Optimize for local SEO by using keywords like 'St. Paul [your service]' to help locals find you easily.
  • Include clear calls-to-action so visitors know what to do next (like booking or contacting you).
  • Add a blog to share your expertise and improve your search rankings over time; consider writing about local events or community interests to engage your audience.

Leverage Google My Business

Google My Business is a game-changer for local visibility. Here’s how to maximize it:

  • Claim and verify your listing to show up in local search results, especially in areas like the bustling District del Sol or the historic Cathedral Hill.
  • Keep your business information up to date (hours, location, services) to avoid confusing potential customers.
  • Encourage happy customers to leave reviews; positive reviews boost your ranking, particularly in competitive neighborhoods.
  • Post updates, special offers, and events to keep your listing fresh and relevant to local happenings.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Word-of-mouth is still one of the best marketing tools. Here’s how you can tap into it:

  • Ask satisfied customers for reviews on Google and Yelp; these can sway potential buyers looking for services in vibrant areas like Lowertown.
  • Create a referral program that rewards customers for bringing in new clients; consider local incentives that resonate with the St. Paul community.
  • Share testimonials on your website and social media to build trust; showcasing local projects can also enhance credibility.

Engage on Social Media

Social media is vital for connecting with the local community. Here’s what I’ve seen work:

  • Use platforms like Facebook and Instagram to showcase your services and engage with followers, particularly during local events such as the St. Paul Winter Carnival.
  • Share behind-the-scenes content to create a personal connection, highlighting your involvement in local charities or events.
  • Run local ads targeting St. Paul residents to reach potential customers; consider promoting your services during popular community events.
  • Collaborate with local influencers to extend your reach; this can be particularly effective in neighborhoods known for their arts scene, like the West 7th.

Network within the Community

Networking is crucial for growth. Here’s how to get involved:

  • Attend local business events and trade shows in St. Paul, such as the St. Paul Business Expo, to meet potential clients and partners.
  • Join local business organizations or chambers of commerce, like the St. Paul Area Chamber of Commerce, to expand your connections.
  • Partner with other local businesses for cross-promotion; collaborating with businesses near the popular Grand Avenue can yield mutual benefits.

The hustle of running a local business isn't easy, but with these strategies in place, you're setting yourself up for success in the heart of St. Paul. Stay consistent, keep connecting with your community, and watch your customer base grow in this supportive and dynamic environment.
