What Content Should Be on My Business Website?

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Picture this: your website is like a welcoming storefront that invites passersby to step inside. You want it to reflect who you are and what you offer, but figuring out what content to include can feel overwhelming. Let's break it down into manageable website content tips that will help your business shine online and truly resonate with your audience.

Craft a Compelling Homepage

Your homepage is your first impression. It should be engaging and informative, serving as the gateway to your business. Consider including:

  • A clear business name and logo: Your logo should be prominently displayed to reinforce brand recognition. Use a catchy tagline that encapsulates what you do.
  • A brief introduction to your services or products: This should be a succinct overview—think of it as your elevator pitch. Highlight what sets you apart within the first few sentences.
  • High-quality images that represent your brand: Invest in professional photography or use high-resolution images that showcase your products or services. Consider including images of your team in action or happy customers using your products.
  • Easy navigation to other pages: Your menu should be intuitive. Include links to important pages like Services, About Us, and Contact, ensuring visitors can find what they need without hassle.
  • A call to action (like 'Contact Us' or 'Shop Now'): Make it clear what you want visitors to do next. Use contrasting colors and clear language to draw attention to buttons or links.

About Us: Share Your Story

Your 'About Us' page should tell the story behind your business. It's your chance to connect with visitors on a personal level and build trust. Here’s what to include:

  • A brief history of your company: Share how you started, what motivated you, and the journey that led to your current offerings. Personal anecdotes can make this section more relatable.
  • Your mission and values: Clearly articulate what your business stands for. This not only helps potential customers understand your ethos but also attracts those who share your values.
  • Team member bios with photos: Introduce the faces behind your business. Include fun facts about each team member and their roles. This humanizes your brand and fosters connection.
  • Your unique selling points: Highlight what differentiates you from the competition. This might include unique methods, sourcing practices, or exceptional customer service protocols.

Services or Products: Be Clear and Detailed

This section should lay out exactly what you offer in an organized manner. Here’s how to make it effective:

  • Detailed descriptions of each service or product: Use clear, concise language that describes what each offering entails. Avoid jargon, and focus on how it benefits the customer.
  • Pricing information, if applicable: If you can, provide pricing or at least a price range. This sets expectations and reduces the chances of losing potential customers due to confusion.
  • Benefits of choosing your offerings: Don’t just list features; explain how your products or services solve problems or fulfill needs. Use bullet points for easy reading.
  • Customer testimonials or reviews: Showcase positive feedback prominently. Real-life experiences build credibility and encourage new customers to trust your business.

Contact Information: Make It Easy to Reach You

Don’t hide your contact details! Make it easy for customers to get in touch and feel supported. Include:

  • A contact form for quick inquiries: Ensure this form is easy to find. Keep fields to a minimum to encourage completion.
  • Your email address and phone number: Include multiple ways to reach you, catering to different preferences. Consider setting business hours so customers know when to expect a response.
  • Your business address, especially if you have a physical location: If you have a storefront or office, include a map link or directions to help new customers find you easily. Mention nearby landmarks for local flavor.
  • Links to your social media accounts: Encourage visitors to follow you on social media for updates and engagement. This also provides another avenue for customer interaction and feedback.

Blog: Share Valuable Content

A blog can establish you as an authority in your field and keep your website fresh and engaging. Consider:

  • Writing posts that answer common customer questions: Think about the queries you receive most often and address them in detail. This positions you as a helpful resource.
  • Sharing industry news or trends: Keeping your audience informed about what's happening in your sector builds credibility and keeps them coming back for more.
  • Providing tips related to your services or products: Create how-to guides or advice pieces that showcase your expertise while subtly promoting your offerings.
  • Highlighting local events, especially if they relate to your business: If your business is rooted in a community, posting about local events or partnerships can strengthen ties and attract local customers.

Filling your website with the right content is essential to engaging your visitors and converting them into customers.
