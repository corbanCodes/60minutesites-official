How Do I Get More Customers in Scottsdale?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

If you're running a small business in Scottsdale, Arizona, you know how competitive it can be to attract customers. With a bustling local economy and a vibrant community, it's essential to leverage both online and offline strategies to stand out from the crowd. Scottsdale, known for its upscale resorts, golf courses, and art galleries, offers a unique backdrop for businesses looking to thrive in a city that values quality and community engagement.

Build a Professional Website

The truth is, your website is the foundation of getting customers online. It’s your digital storefront, and it needs to be inviting. Here's how to make it work for you:

  • Ensure your website is mobile-friendly; many people search for services on their phones, especially tourists exploring Scottsdale.
  • Include clear calls to action (CTA) that guide visitors on what to do next, like booking an appointment or contacting you.
  • Optimize your website for local SEO to show up in searches specific to Scottsdale, such as 'best restaurants in Scottsdale' or 'top-rated spas in Scottsdale.'
  • Use high-quality images and engaging content that reflects your brand and the local culture, featuring iconic Scottsdale attractions like Camelback Mountain or the Desert Botanical Garden.
  • Make your contact information easy to find, including your location which is especially relevant for those visiting the area.

Leverage Google My Business

Setting up a Google My Business profile is crucial for local visibility. Here's what actually works:

  • Fill out your profile completely with accurate information about your business, including location details that can attract both locals and visitors.
  • Regularly update your business hours, services, and photos to reflect any seasonal offerings, especially during Scottsdale's busy tourist seasons.
  • Encourage customers to leave reviews; this builds trust and boosts your rankings, making your business more visible to those searching for services in Scottsdale.
  • Post updates and offers; this keeps your profile active and engaging, especially during events like the Scottsdale Arts Festival or the Scottsdale Culinary Festival.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Word-of-mouth is still one of the best ways to get customers, especially in a close-knit community like Scottsdale. To maximize this:

  • Ask satisfied customers to leave reviews on Google and Yelp, particularly targeting those who have experienced your services during popular events like the Scottsdale International Film Festival.
  • Create a referral program that rewards customers for bringing in new business, which can be particularly effective in a community that values local connections.
  • Share customer testimonials on your website and social media; this builds credibility and showcases the positive experiences of both locals and tourists.

Engage on Social Media

Social media is a powerful tool for reaching potential customers. Here’s how to use it effectively:

  • Choose platforms that resonate with your target audience; Instagram and Facebook are particularly popular in Scottsdale, especially for showcasing the city's vibrant lifestyle and events.
  • Post regularly and engage with your followers; respond to comments and messages promptly to build a loyal online community.
  • Share local events or collaborations with other businesses to strengthen community ties, such as participating in the Scottsdale Art Walk or partnering with local festivals.
  • Use targeted ads to reach specific demographics in the area, particularly during peak tourist seasons when foot traffic in popular areas like Old Town Scottsdale increases.

Network Locally

Networking can open doors you didn’t even know existed. Consider these strategies:

  • Attend local business events and trade shows; Scottsdale hosts plenty of them, including the Scottsdale Chamber of Commerce events that connect local entrepreneurs.
  • Join local business organizations or chambers of commerce that can provide resources and support for your business growth.
  • Collaborate with other local businesses for joint promotions; it’s a win-win for everyone involved, especially when teaming up for holiday events or seasonal festivals that attract tourists.
