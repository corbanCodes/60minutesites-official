How to Rank on Google Maps in Memphis

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Here's the reality: if you're a business in Memphis, not showing up on Google Maps is like being invisible to your customers. With vibrant neighborhoods like Midtown, the eclectic art scene, and the hustle of Beale Street, your potential clients are just a search away. Whether you run a cozy eatery in Cooper-Young or a boutique shop in Downtown, mastering Google Maps is essential for connecting with your audience. Let’s dive into how you can get your business noticed on Google Maps in Memphis, ensuring that you attract both locals and visitors alike.

Claim and Optimize Your Google My Business Listing

The first step to ranking on Google Maps is to claim your Google My Business (GMB) listing. This is your digital storefront, so make it shine. Here’s how to do it effectively:

  • Visit the Google My Business website and sign in or create an account. Make sure you use your business email for consistency.
  • Claim your business by entering your business name and address. Be cautious about selecting the right business from the suggestions provided by Google.
  • Complete all relevant information, including hours, services, and high-quality photos. A well-chosen cover photo and a few captivating images of your products or services can make a huge difference.
  • Choose the correct categories that best describe your business. For instance, if you own a barbecue restaurant, use terms like 'Barbecue Restaurant' and 'Catering Service' to help Google understand what you offer.

Don't forget to regularly update your listing with any changes, promotions, or events happening in your business. If you’re offering a special for the Memphis in May festival or hosting a live music event, make sure it’s reflected in your GMB profile.

Gather and Respond to Customer Reviews

Reviews are crucial for your Google Maps ranking and your reputation, especially in a community-oriented city like Memphis. Here’s how to leverage them:

  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews on your GMB listing. Consider placing a reminder on receipts or sending a follow-up email thanking them for their business and inviting feedback.
  • Respond to all reviews, both positive and negative, to show that you value customer feedback. This engagement can foster loyalty; for example, thanking a customer for a positive review can encourage them to return, while addressing a negative review with professionalism can turn a dissatisfied customer into a loyal one.
  • Implement a follow-up email or message after a purchase to request a review. Personalizing the message can make customers feel more appreciated and likely to leave a review.

Every single review counts and helps improve your visibility in local searches. Plus, showcasing your responsiveness can attract new customers who value excellent customer service.

Optimize Your Website for Local SEO

Your website needs to work hand-in-hand with your GMB listing. Here are steps to optimize it:

  • Include your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) clearly on your website. Make sure this information is also visible in the footer of every page.
  • Utilize local keywords, like ‘best BBQ in Memphis’ or ‘Memphis artisanal coffee,’ in your content to attract local searches. Consider writing blog posts about local events or even food reviews of nearby attractions to further enhance your local SEO.
  • Create location-specific pages or blog posts that highlight local events, news, or partnerships with other local businesses. For example, if you're hosting an event in conjunction with the Memphis in May festival, create a dedicated page with all relevant details.

This not only boosts your Google Maps ranking but also enhances your overall online presence, giving potential customers a reason to choose you over competitors.

Use Local Citations and Backlinks

Building local citations can significantly enhance your credibility and visibility. Here’s what to do:

  • List your business on local directories such as Yelp, Yellow Pages, and Memphis-specific directories like Memphis.com. Make sure your information is accurate and consistent across all platforms.
  • Ensure your NAP is consistent across all platforms. This consistency helps Google trust your business and improves your ranking.
  • Seek backlinks from local news sites or blogs by contributing guest posts or newsworthy events. For instance, if you’re hosting a charity event, reach out to local news outlets to cover it, which could lead to valuable backlinks.

These local links help Google see that your business is reputable and relevant to Memphis residents. Plus, the more you connect with the community, the more people will talk about you online.

Engage with the Community and Share on Social Media

Being active in your community can lead to organic growth on Google Maps. Here’s how:

  • Participate in local events, sponsor community activities, or collaborate with other local businesses. For example, co-host a food festival or a charity run that brings attention to your business.
  • Share your involvement on social media platforms like Facebook and Instagram, tagging local hotspots and using relevant hashtags. Engaging with local influencers can also help broaden your reach.
  • Post images and updates consistently to keep your audience engaged. Sharing behind-the-scenes looks at your business or customer stories can humanize your brand and foster community connection.

This not only builds relationships but also improves your visibility as a local business. When community members see your presence, they’re more likely to support you.
