Why You Need a Google Business Profile

A Google Business Profile is crucial for any local business in University Place. It acts as your digital storefront, showcasing your services and making it easy for locals to find you online. Here are a few key benefits that illustrate why you should prioritize this:

  • Enhances your visibility in local searches: When someone searches for a service like ‘best pizza in University Place,’ your business can appear in the local pack, increasing your chances of being chosen. The more complete your profile, the higher your chances of ranking well.
  • Offers crucial information like hours, location, and contact details: Customers can easily access your business hours, location near well-known landmarks like the University Place Library, and your contact number. This information is vital, especially for those who are on-the-go.
  • Allows you to engage with customers through reviews and Q&A: Responding to reviews, whether positive or negative, shows potential customers that you value feedback. Engaging in the Q&A section allows you to address common queries, making your business appear more approachable.
  • Provides insights into how customers find your business: Google Insights can show you how many people are finding your profile through searches, what actions they take, and even how they interact with your posts. This information is invaluable for tailoring your marketing strategies.

Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Getting started with your Google Business Profile is straightforward, but there are specific steps to ensure you create an effective and appealing profile that resonates with your audience. Follow these steps:

  • Visit the Google Business Profile website: Start by signing in with your Google account. If you don’t have one, creating an account is simple and free.
  • Enter your business name and address: Accuracy is key. If your business is located in a specific area, like the vibrant 27th Street corridor, make sure to include that detail to guide customers accurately.
  • Select the appropriate category for your business: This helps Google show your profile to relevant searches. For example, if you run a coffee shop, select categories like ‘Coffee Shop’ or ‘Café’ to capture the right audience.
  • Fill in your business information: Include your phone number, website, and hours of operation. Make sure your website is mobile-friendly, as many locals will be searching for businesses while they are out and about.
  • Upload high-quality photos: Eye-catching images of your business, products, or services can significantly enhance your listing. Show off your inviting interior, delicious menu items, or any special events happening at your location.
  • Verify your business: Follow the verification method provided by Google, which could be via a postcard, phone call, or email. This step is crucial as it confirms your ownership and makes your profile more credible.

  • Exploring nearby options: When using maps or local listings, customers prefer seeing businesses that are conveniently located. Highlight your proximity to popular spots like the Chambers Bay Golf Course or the Marlene’s Market to enhance your appeal.

Make sure your Google Business Profile reflects this behavior by optimizing it with relevant keywords and up-to-date information. Consider posting regularly to keep your audience engaged and informed about special offers or events.
