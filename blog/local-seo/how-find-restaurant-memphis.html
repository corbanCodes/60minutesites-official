How to Find a Restaurant in Memphis

5 min read

Finding a great restaurant in Memphis can feel like searching for treasure in a city rich with flavors and cultures. With so many options, it’s all about knowing where to look and how to narrow down your choices. From smoky barbecue to exquisite fine dining, Memphis offers a culinary adventure waiting to be explored.

Tap Into Local Recommendations

One of the best ways to find a restaurant in Memphis is by asking locals for their favorites. They often know hidden gems that guidebooks overlook. Here are some tips to help you uncover those culinary treasures:

  • Join local Facebook groups or forums: Platforms like Facebook have community groups where residents share their go-to dining spots. Search for groups focused on Memphis foodies, and don’t hesitate to ask for suggestions. Locals love to share their insights!
  • Check out food blogs and social media accounts: Memphis has a vibrant food blogging scene. Look for bloggers who specialize in Memphis cuisine; they often provide detailed reviews and mouth-watering photos. Instagram is particularly useful for visual inspiration—search hashtags like #MemphisFood or #MemphisEats.
  • Visit local markets or events: Spend a Saturday morning at the Cooper-Young Community Farmers Market or the Memphis Farmers Market. While you’re there, chat with vendors and fellow shoppers about their favorite dining spots. You may discover a cozy restaurant that’s not on any map.

Explore Memphis Neighborhoods

Each neighborhood in Memphis has its own character and culinary scene. Exploring different areas can yield fantastic dining experiences that reflect the local culture. Consider these popular spots:

  • Beale Street: Known for its live music and vibrant nightlife, Beale Street is also a haven for Southern comfort food. Try the iconic Gus's World Famous Fried Chicken or indulge in some ribs at A&R Bar-B-Que while enjoying the sounds of blues music.
  • Cooper-Young: This trendy neighborhood is home to a mix of eclectic eateries and craft breweries. Grab a bite at The Beauty Shop, a converted salon turned restaurant serving innovative American dishes, or head to Young Avenue Deli for a casual meal with a local twist.
  • Overton Square: With its rich history and artistic vibe, Overton Square offers diverse dining options. From the upscale dining experience at The Second Line to the casual, delicious offerings at Boscos Squared, there’s something for every palate.

Utilize Online Resources and Apps

Embrace technology to simplify your search for a restaurant in Memphis. Here’s how to make the most of online resources:

  • Use apps like Yelp or TripAdvisor: These platforms allow you to search for restaurants based on ratings, type of cuisine, or distance from your location. You can filter results to find exactly what you’re craving, whether it’s barbecue, sushi, or vegan fare.
  • Read reviews and look at photos: Take the time to read reviews from previous diners. Look for comments about the atmosphere, service, and standout dishes. Photos can give you a visual sense of what to expect, especially helpful if you’re unsure about a restaurant's menu.
  • Check out Google Maps: Use Google Maps to find nearby options and view their hours of operation. The “Explore” feature can suggest popular spots based on your current location, making it easy to discover something new.

Attend Food Festivals and Events

Memphis hosts numerous food festivals throughout the year where you can sample a variety of cuisines. These events are perfect for discovering new restaurants and enjoying the local food culture. Look for:

  • The World Championship Barbecue Cooking Contest: Held annually in May, this festival is a must-visit for barbecue lovers. Not only can you taste some of the best BBQ in the world, but you’ll also have the chance to meet the chefs behind the magic.
  • The Memphis Food and Wine Festival: This event showcases a selection of top local chefs and wineries, allowing you to experience culinary excellence right in the heart of Memphis. It’s a fantastic way to sample dishes and drinks while mingling with fellow food enthusiasts.
  • Local farmers’ markets: These markets often feature local eateries as vendors, giving you a chance to taste what the city has to offer. Try some homemade snacks, artisanal cheeses, or farm-fresh dishes while supporting local businesses.

Finding the perfect restaurant in Memphis doesn't have to be overwhelming. With a little research and some local insights, you can discover incredible dining experiences that will leave your taste buds dancing. Whether you're in the mood for BBQ, soul food, or something more eclectic, Memphis has it all.
