Why You Need a Google Business Profile

Think of your Google Business Profile as your digital storefront. It’s often the first impression potential customers have of your business. Here are several compelling reasons to prioritize this setup:

  • Improves your visibility in local searches: When someone searches for services or products you offer, a well-optimized profile can help you appear at the top of local search results. This is particularly important in a bustling town like New Iberia, where local searches are commonplace.
  • Provides crucial information: Customers can easily find essential details such as your business hours, location, and contact information, which are vital for attracting foot traffic. Make sure to include a map pin that accurately reflects your physical location, especially if you're situated near popular landmarks like the Shadows-on-the-Teche or the Bayou Teche.
  • Allows customers to leave reviews: Reviews are a powerful way to build your reputation. Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive feedback on your profile, as this can significantly influence potential customers' decisions. Responding to reviews—both positive and negative—demonstrates your commitment to customer service.
  • Gives you insights: Google provides analytics on how customers interact with your profile. You can see how many people viewed your profile, requested directions, or called your business, enabling you to make informed decisions about your marketing strategy.

How to Set Up Your Google Business Profile

Getting started with your Google Business Profile is a simple process. Just follow these detailed steps to set up your profile:

  • Go to the Google Business Profile website: Begin by visiting the Google Business Profile website and click on 'Manage now' to start the setup process.
  • Sign in with your Google account: If you already have a Google account, log in. If not, take a moment to create one. This account will be crucial for managing your business profile.
  • Enter your business name and address: Input your business name exactly as it appears in the real world, and ensure your address is accurate, as this affects local search results.
  • Select the appropriate category: Choose a category that best reflects your business. For example, if you own a local restaurant, select 'Restaurant' or a more specific category like 'Seafood Restaurant' to help customers find you easily.
  • Add your phone number and website URL: If you have a website, link it to your profile. This allows customers to navigate directly to your online presence for additional information.
  • Verify your business: Verification is typically done through a postcard sent by Google to your business address. This step is crucial, as it confirms that your business is legitimate.
  • Complete your profile: Enhance your profile by adding high-quality photos that showcase your products or services, detailing your business hours, and crafting a compelling business description that highlights what makes you unique.

  • Checking social media: Many locals turn to platforms like Facebook and Instagram for recommendations and to learn about local events. Engaging with your community on social media can complement your Google Business Profile and enhance your overall online presence.
