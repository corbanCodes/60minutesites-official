How Do I Get More Customers in Charlotte?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Charlotte, North Carolina, is a vibrant and rapidly growing city that boasts a diverse economy, making it an excellent location for small businesses to flourish. With its dynamic landscape, entrepreneurs must employ strategic online and offline tactics to attract customers amid healthy competition.

Building Your Online Presence

The foundation of getting customers online is a professional website. Here's what actually works:

  • Create a user-friendly website that clearly outlines your services and contact information, emphasizing features like your physical location in popular neighborhoods such as NoDa or South End.
  • Optimize your site for local SEO by incorporating Charlotte-specific keywords, such as ‘Charlotte small business’ or ‘business in Uptown Charlotte.’
  • Set up a Google My Business profile, complete with your location near landmarks like the Bank of America Stadium or the NASCAR Hall of Fame, to improve your visibility in local searches.
  • Regularly update your website with fresh content, like blogs or service updates, to keep it engaging and relevant to Charlotte residents.
  • Ensure your website is mobile-friendly since many customers will be browsing on their phones, especially during events like the Charlotte International Arts Festival.

Harnessing the Power of Reviews

The truth is, online reviews can make or break your business. Here’s how to leverage them:

  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews on Google and Yelp, particularly focusing on local attractions like the U.S. National Whitewater Center to attract adventure seekers.
  • Respond to all reviews, both positive and negative, to show you value customer feedback and to establish trust in a close-knit community.
  • Consider creating a referral program that rewards customers for bringing in new business, perhaps offering discounts that can be redeemed at local events like the Taste of Charlotte.

Maximizing Social Media Engagement

Social media is a powerful tool for connecting with your community. Here’s what you need to do:

  • Choose the right platforms; Facebook and Instagram are popular in Charlotte for local businesses, especially for sharing updates about events at venues like the Charlotte Convention Center.
  • Post regularly about your services, promotions, and community involvement, such as partnerships with local charities or participation in the Charlotte Pride Festival.
  • Engage with your audience; respond to comments and messages promptly to foster a loyal customer base.
  • Share user-generated content to build trust and community around your brand, especially during community events like the annual Charlotte Film Festival.

Networking and Community Involvement

Building relationships in Charlotte can lead to valuable referrals. Here’s how to network effectively:

  • Attend local business events, like Chamber of Commerce meetings or trade shows, which are often held at venues such as the Charlotte Marriott City Center.
  • Join local business associations, such as the Charlotte Regional Business Alliance, to connect with other entrepreneurs and potential collaborators.
  • Partner with complementary local businesses for joint promotions or events, leveraging the popularity of local markets like the Charlotte Farmers Market.
  • Volunteer for community events, such as the annual Queen City Unity Festival, to raise your business profile and show your commitment to local causes.

Remember, building a customer base takes time and persistence. Stay committed to your strategies, engage with your community, and watch your business grow in the beautiful city of Charlotte, where opportunities abound for those willing to invest in their local presence.
