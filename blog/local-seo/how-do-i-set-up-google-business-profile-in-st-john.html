Why Set Up a Google Business Profile?

Creating a Google Business Profile is essential for visibility and engagement with your local community. It helps you connect with customers searching for services nearby. Here’s why it’s important:

  • Visibility in Local Searches: When potential customers search for businesses like yours, having a Google Business Profile increases the chances that they will find you in their local search results. For example, if someone types 'best coffee shop in St. John', your business could appear at the top of the results if you have a profile.
  • Showcase Key Information: Your profile allows you to highlight essential details such as your hours of operation, exact location, and the services you provide. This information is critical for customers who want to visit or contact you. Include special features that might attract locals, like outdoor seating for your café or delivery options for your store.
  • Build Trust Through Reviews: Customer reviews are a cornerstone of your online reputation. Encouraging satisfied customers to leave positive feedback can enhance your credibility. In a close-knit community like St. John, word-of-mouth is powerful, and positive online reviews can lead to more foot traffic.

Step-by-Step Guide to Setting Up Your Profile

Getting started with your Google Business Profile is straightforward. Follow these steps:

  • Navigate to Google Business Profile: Go to the Google Business Profile page and click on 'Manage now.' You’ll need to be signed into your Google account.
  • Enter Your Business Name and Address: Make sure it’s accurate—locals rely on it to find you! If your business is located in a popular area, like near the St. John Town Center, mention that for better visibility.
  • Select Your Business Category: Choose a category that best fits your business. For instance, if you own a bakery, select 'Bakery' or 'Dessert Shop.' This helps Google show your profile to the right audience.
  • Add Contact Information: Input your business phone number and website link if you have one. A well-designed website can complement your profile and provide more information about your offerings.
  • Verify Your Business: Follow Google’s verification process, which may involve receiving a postcard at your business location. Verification is crucial as it confirms your legitimacy.
  • Optimize Your Profile: Once verified, enhance your profile with high-quality photos of your business, updates about upcoming events, and any special offers. For example, if you're hosting a local art show, post about it to attract more visitors!

How Locals in St. John Search for Businesses

Understanding how St. John residents look for businesses can help you attract more customers:

  • Local Search Phrases: Many locals search using phrases like 'best pizza in St. John' or 'plumbers near me.' Tailoring your business description and services to include these keywords can help improve your search rankings.
  • Utilization of Google Maps: St. John residents often rely on Google Maps to find nearby options quickly. Ensure your location is marked accurately, and encourage customers to use Maps to navigate to your business.
  • The Importance of Reviews: Reviews play a significant role in local searches; residents tend to trust businesses with positive feedback. Regularly monitor and respond to reviews to show that you value customer input.
  • Engagement in Community Events: Participating in local festivals or markets can significantly increase your visibility. St. John hosts several community events throughout the year, such as the St. John Summer Festival. Consider setting up a booth or sponsorship to engage with the local community directly.
