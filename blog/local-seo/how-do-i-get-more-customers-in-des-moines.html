How Do I Get More Customers in Des Moines?

9 min read

Landing new customers in Des Moines can feel like a challenge, but with the right strategies, you can stand out. This vibrant city, known for its friendly residents and supportive business community, has much to offer to entrepreneurs. Let's dive into what actually works in Des Moines.

Build a Professional Website

The truth is, your website is the cornerstone of your online presence. It’s where potential customers will first interact with your business. Here’s what you need to focus on:

  • Clear, easy navigation to enhance user experience
  • Mobile-friendly design, as many Des Moines residents access the internet via smartphones
  • Strong calls to action that encourage engagement
  • Contact information that’s easy to find, including local phone numbers and addresses
  • SEO optimization to rank higher on Google, particularly for local searches that include phrases like 'best coffee in Des Moines' or 'Des Moines landscaping services'

Optimize for Google My Business

Your Google My Business profile is crucial for local visibility. Here’s how to make it shine:

  • Keep your business info up to date, especially hours of operation, as many Des Moines locals appreciate transparency
  • Use high-quality images that showcase your products or services, as visuals can resonate well in community-oriented settings
  • Regularly post updates or offers to stay relevant in the minds of potential customers
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews, particularly those who frequent popular spots like the East Village or Historic Valley Junction
  • Use relevant keywords in your business description that reflect local interests

Leverage Customer Reviews

Positive reviews can be a game-changer. Here’s how to gather and utilize them:

  • Ask for reviews after services or purchases, especially after attending local events like the Iowa State Fair
  • Make it easy to leave reviews by providing direct links on your website and social media
  • Respond to all reviews, whether positive or negative, to show you value customer feedback
  • Showcase testimonials on your website and social media to build trust among potential clients

Harness the Power of Referrals

Referrals are pure gold in local business. Here’s what works:

  • Create a referral program with incentives that appeal to locals, such as discounts at nearby attractions or restaurants
  • Network with complementary businesses for cross-referrals, especially during community events in areas like the Historic Court District
  • Always ask satisfied customers to recommend you to friends and family, leveraging Des Moines’ close-knit community feel

Engage on Social Media

Social media can be a powerful tool for connection. Here’s how to make it work for you:

  • Choose the platforms where your audience hangs out (Facebook, Instagram, etc.), as Des Moines has a vibrant social media landscape
  • Post regularly and engage with followers, especially highlighting local culture and events like the Des Moines Arts Festival
  • Share behind-the-scenes content to create a personal connection with your audience
  • Run local ads to target specific demographics, capitalizing on the city's diverse population

Network Locally

Building relationships in the community can lead to new customers. Here’s how to network effectively:

  • Attend local business events and trade shows, such as the Greater Des Moines Partnership events
  • Join local chambers of commerce or business groups to foster connections
  • Collaborate with local nonprofits for community events, demonstrating your commitment to the Des Moines area
  • Don’t underestimate the power of face-to-face interactions, especially in places like the Pappajohn Sculpture Park or local coffee shops

Getting customers in Des Moines takes effort, but by leveraging these strategies, you can build a solid customer base. Stay persistent, keep refining your approach, and remember that every interaction counts! Embrace the local culture and community spirit, and success will follow.
