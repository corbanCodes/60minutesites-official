Why You Need a Google Business Profile

Establishing a Google Business Profile is essential for any business wanting to be found by local customers. Here’s why you should set it up:

  • Increased visibility on Google Search and Maps: When potential customers search for services or products you offer, a well-optimized profile ensures that your business appears prominently on search results and Google Maps. This can mean the difference between a sale and a missed opportunity, especially in a community where local preferences heavily influence buying decisions.
  • Access to customer insights and analytics: Google provides valuable data about how customers find your business and what actions they take—such as calling your business or visiting your website. This information can help you refine your marketing strategy to cater to local demands.
  • Ability to respond to reviews and engage with customers: Engaging with customer feedback not only shows that you value their opinions, but it also builds trust and credibility. This is particularly important in close-knit communities like East Chicago, where word-of-mouth can significantly impact your reputation.
  • Showcase your products, services, and special offers: Your profile allows you to highlight what makes your business unique—whether it’s the artisanal bread from your bakery or the special discounts you offer on services. Use high-quality images and engaging descriptions to draw in customers.
  • Build credibility with verified business information: Having accurate and up-to-date information boosts your credibility. Customers are more likely to choose a business that appears authoritative and reliable.

Step-by-Step Guide to Setting Up Your Profile

Getting started with your Google Business Profile is easier than you might think. Follow these steps to create an engaging profile that reflects your business:

  • Go to the Google Business Profile website: Begin by visiting the Google Business Profile website and clicking 'Start now.' This initiates the simple process of setting up your profile.
  • Enter your business name: Make sure to input the exact name of your business. Choose the right category to help customers find you. For example, if you run a coffee shop near the East Chicago Public Library, select 'Coffee Shop' to ensure you attract coffee lovers in the area.
  • Provide your business address: Enter your physical location accurately. If your business operates in multiple areas or provides services, specify these locations to widen your reach. This is especially important for service-based businesses in East Chicago that cater to different neighborhoods.
  • Choose a contact method: Select how customers can reach you—either through a phone number or a website URL. This is where 60 Minute Sites can help you build a stunning, user-friendly website that complements your profile and enhances your online presence.
  • Verify your business: Google will provide options for verifying your business, which may involve receiving a postcard at your business address, a phone call, or an email. Verification is a crucial step in ensuring that you have control over your business information.
  • Add comprehensive details: Once verified, enrich your profile with detailed information. Include your hours of operation, high-quality photos of your products, and any special promotions you may have. For instance, if you run a local diner that offers a special brunch on weekends, highlight this to attract more visitors.

How Locals in East Chicago Search for Businesses

Understanding how locals search for businesses can give you an edge. Many East Chicago residents turn to their phones or computers to find nearby shops, restaurants, and services. Here’s how they typically look:

  • Using Google Search: Locals often type in queries like 'best pizza in East Chicago' or 'local plumbers near me.' Make sure your profile is optimized with relevant keywords to boost your chances of appearing in these searches.
  • Checking Google Maps: Many users will open Google Maps to find directions and read reviews while on the go. Ensure your location is accurately pinned to avoid any confusion for potential customers trying to find you.
  • Looking for specific services on social media: Platforms like Facebook and Instagram serve as visual search engines for many users. Share your Google Business Profile link on these platforms to attract a wider audience.
  • Reading customer reviews: Reviews play a significant role in decision-making. Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews, as this can greatly influence new customers who are on the fence about visiting your establishment.
  • Asking for recommendations: Residents often ask friends and family for business recommendations and follow that up with online searches. Engaging with your community can help you become a go-to option in these conversations.
