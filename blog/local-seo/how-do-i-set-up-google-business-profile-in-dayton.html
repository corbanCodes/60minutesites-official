Creating a Great Google Business Profile

Here's the secret to getting noticed: a well-optimized Google Business Profile. Follow these steps to create one that shines:

  • Claim your business listing on Google.
  • Fill out all the essential information: business name, address, phone number, and website. Include your location within Dayton, whether it’s the bustling Oregon District, the historic neighborhoods of Walnut Hills, or near the Dayton Art Institute.
  • Choose the right categories to help customers find you, such as 'restaurants,' 'boutiques,' or 'service providers' specific to Dayton's diverse offerings.
  • Add high-quality photos that showcase your business and products, such as images of your storefront, your team, or your best-selling items.
  • Write a compelling business description that highlights what makes you unique, especially local elements like your involvement in community events or partnerships with other Dayton businesses.
  • Regularly update your profile with posts, special offers, and events, such as local festivals, farmer's markets, or special promotions during the Dayton Air Show.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I verify my Google Business Profile?

You can verify your profile by receiving a postcard from Google with a verification code, or in some cases, by phone or email. Make sure to complete this step to enhance your credibility and visibility in Dayton.

Q: Can I update my business hours?

Yes! You can easily update your hours on your Google Business Profile. Make sure to keep them current, especially during holidays or local events like the Dayton Celtic Festival.

Q: What if my business has multiple locations?

You can create separate profiles for each location, ensuring each one has its unique details and customer engagement. This is particularly useful if you operate in different neighborhoods within Dayton.

Q: How do I respond to customer reviews?

You can respond directly through your Google Business Profile dashboard. Engaging with reviews shows you care about customer feedback, whether it's positive or negative. This is important in a community-oriented city like Dayton, where relationships matter.
