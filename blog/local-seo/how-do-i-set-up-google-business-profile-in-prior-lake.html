Why Google Business Profile Matters

Your Google Business Profile is your digital storefront, and it’s essential for connecting with local customers. Here’s why you should set it up:

  • Boosts your visibility in local searches: When potential customers search for services or products you offer, your business can appear right in their search results, increasing the chances they'll choose you over competitors.
  • Provides essential information like hours, location, and services: Customers can easily find out when you're open, where you're located, and what offerings you have, all from a quick glance at your profile.
  • Builds trust with customer reviews and photos: Positive reviews from happy customers can significantly enhance your credibility. Encourage satisfied clients to leave feedback and share their experiences to attract new customers.
  • Allows you to share updates and special offers directly: Use your profile to promote seasonal specials, new menu items, or upcoming events. This keeps your audience engaged and informed.

Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Let’s walk through the steps to get your Google Business Profile set up:

  • Create Your Listing: Start by visiting the Google Business Profile website and select 'Start now.' Make sure you're logged into your Google account.
  • Enter Your Business Name: Ensure it matches your actual business name. Consistency helps with branding and makes it easier for customers to find you.
  • Select Your Business Category: Choose a category that best describes your business, such as “Café,” “Plumber,” or “Retail Store.” This decision helps Google show your business to the right audience.
  • Add Your Location: If you have a physical storefront, input your address. If you provide services in specific areas, such as nearby neighborhoods like Lakefront or the Prior Lake area, specify that to help local customers find you.
  • Provide Contact Information: Include a phone number and website URL so customers can reach you easily. Make sure these are up-to-date to avoid any confusion.
  • Verify Your Business: Google will guide you through the verification process, which may involve receiving a postcard at your physical address with a verification code. This step is crucial to ensure your business is legitimate.
  • Optimize Your Profile: Upload high-quality images of your products, services, and storefront. A captivating business description can draw customers in, so make it engaging and informative. Don’t forget to keep your hours of operation updated, especially around holidays or special events.
