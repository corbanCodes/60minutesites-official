How Do I Get More Customers in Scranton?

5 min read

What if the secret to growing your small business in Scranton lies in the very neighborhood where you set up shop? As the landscape of customer engagement evolves, local businesses have unique opportunities to attract more customers, both online and offline. Let's dive into effective strategies that resonate with the Scranton community and help you expand your customer base.

Online Strategies for Attracting Customers

Embracing the digital realm is essential for any small business looking to thrive in Scranton. Here are some effective online strategies tailored for the local market:

  • Create a user-friendly website: Your website serves as the digital storefront for your business. Ensure it is visually appealing and easy to navigate. Include high-quality images of your products or services, clear descriptions, and customer testimonials. A blog featuring local events, tips, or industry insights can also engage visitors and improve your SEO.
  • Utilize social media platforms: Platforms like Facebook and Instagram are excellent for connecting with your audience. Share stories about your business, showcase behind-the-scenes content, and engage with followers through polls or questions. Consider featuring local influencers to expand your reach. Regularly posting about community events or partnerships can also strengthen your local ties.
  • Invest in local SEO: Optimize your website for local search results by including keywords specific to Scranton. For example, phrases like “best coffee shop in Scranton” or “Scranton boutique gifts” can help. Ensure your Google My Business profile is complete and accurate, including your location, hours, and customer reviews, to enhance visibility.
  • Engage with email marketing: Build an email list to communicate directly with your customers. Send out newsletters featuring upcoming events, special promotions, and local news. Personalize emails to make customers feel valued, perhaps by celebrating their birthdays with a special offer.
  • Encourage customer reviews: Online reviews on platforms like Google and Yelp can significantly influence potential customers. Encourage satisfied customers to share their experiences. Consider creating a review card that offers a small discount on their next purchase for leaving a review.

Offline Strategies for Attracting Customers

While online efforts are important, don’t underestimate the power of traditional methods. Here are some offline strategies that resonate particularly well in Scranton:

  • Host local events or workshops: Organizing events like art shows, cooking classes, or DIY workshops can draw in local residents. For instance, collaborating with a local artist for a gallery night can attract art enthusiasts and encourage community engagement.
  • Network with other local businesses: Forming partnerships with neighboring businesses can lead to cross-promotions. For example, a local bakery might team up with a nearby coffee shop for a “coffee and pastry” combo deal, benefiting both establishments.
  • Utilize flyers and posters: Design eye-catching flyers or posters to distribute in local hotspots such as the Steamtown National Historic Site, the Marketplace at Steamtown, or even popular coffee shops. Highlight special promotions or events to attract foot traffic.
  • Participate in Scranton's festivals and farmers' markets: Events like the Scranton Jazz Festival or the Scranton Farmers Market offer great opportunities for visibility. Setting up a booth can help you interact directly with customers and showcase your offerings.
  • Offer exclusive in-store promotions: Create special offers that are available only in-store to encourage foot traffic. This could be anything from a “buy one, get one free” deal to a limited-time discount for first-time customers.

The Scranton Business Community

Scranton has a rich tapestry of small businesses that contribute to its unique charm. Being part of this tight-knit community can open doors for collaboration and shared success. Here’s how to leverage the local business community effectively:

  • Join local chambers of commerce or business associations: Becoming a member helps you stay informed about local business news and events while providing networking opportunities with fellow entrepreneurs.
  • Attend networking events: Participating in local meet-ups or business mixers can foster valuable connections. Consider attending events hosted by the Greater Scranton Chamber of Commerce to meet potential collaborators and customers.
  • Collaborate on joint marketing efforts: During local events, consider teaming up with other businesses to co-sponsor activities. A group promotion can extend your reach and attract more participants, such as a holiday market or a summer block party.
  • Support and promote other small businesses: Use your social media platforms to spotlight other local businesses. This not only builds goodwill but can also encourage reciprocal promotion, helping both parties grow.
  • Engage in community service projects: Volunteering for community service projects or sponsoring local sports teams can enhance your visibility and foster goodwill within the community. It's a powerful way to connect and demonstrate your commitment to Scranton.

Conclusion

By combining both online and offline strategies, your business in Scranton can thrive and attract more customers. The local community values relationships and authenticity, so engaging with them thoughtfully is key.
