How Do I Get More Customers in State College?

5 min read

Here's the thing: attracting more customers as a small business in State College isn't just about having a good product; it's about connecting with your community both online and offline. With a vibrant mix of students, locals, and visitors, there are countless opportunities to grow your customer base if you know where to look. Whether you're nestled in the bustling downtown area or in the quieter outskirts, creating a strong community presence can make all the difference in your success.

Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Building your presence online is crucial. Here are some effective strategies that can help you stand out in the digital landscape:

  • Create a user-friendly website: Your website is often the first impression potential customers will have of your business. Ensure it is visually appealing, easy to navigate, and mobile-friendly. Highlight your unique offerings and include high-quality images of your products or services. Incorporate an FAQ section to address common customer inquiries and establish trust.
  • Optimize for local SEO: Local search engine optimization is essential for ensuring your business appears at the top of search results when potential customers look for services in State College. Use keywords that are specific to your area, such as “best coffee shop in State College” or “affordable catering near Penn State.” Register your business with Google My Business and keep your information up to date.
  • Utilize social media platforms: Platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter are invaluable for engaging with your audience. Share behind-the-scenes content, customer testimonials, and promotions to create a dialogue with your followers. Use hashtags relevant to your community, like #StateCollegeEats or #HappyValley, to increase your visibility.
  • Encourage customer reviews: Positive reviews on platforms like Google and Yelp can significantly influence potential customers. Consider sending a follow-up email after a purchase thanking customers and inviting them to leave a review. You could also create a small incentive for reviews, such as a discount on their next purchase.
  • Run targeted online ads: Consider using Facebook Ads or Google Ads to run targeted campaigns aimed at specific demographics, such as college students or families in the area. Tailor your messaging to resonate with the interests and needs of these groups. For example, if you own a bookstore, you might promote special student discounts during back-to-school season.

Offline Strategies to Draw in Customers

Don't underestimate the power of face-to-face interactions. Here are some offline strategies that can effectively draw in customers:

  • Participate in local events: State College hosts numerous events throughout the year, including the Central Pennsylvania Festival of the Arts and the annual Arts Fest. Set up a booth or sponsor an event to showcase your products and connect with the community. Hand out samples, promotional materials, and engage in conversations to create lasting impressions.
  • Network with other local businesses: Building relationships with fellow entrepreneurs can lead to mutually beneficial partnerships. Consider arranging joint promotions, such as a discount when customers shop at both your store and a neighboring business. This can help you tap into each other’s customer bases and drive foot traffic.
  • Offer loyalty programs: Encourage repeat visits by implementing a loyalty program that rewards customers for their patronage. For example, a coffee shop might offer a free drink after ten purchases. You could also provide exclusive discounts for local residents or students, making them feel valued and appreciated.
  • Host workshops or classes: Engaging the community through workshops or classes not only showcases your expertise but also builds a sense of community around your brand. If you own a craft store, consider hosting DIY workshops. If you run a fitness studio, offer free community classes in the park to attract new clientele.
  • Utilize flyers and local newspapers: Don't shy away from traditional advertising methods. Create eye-catching flyers and place them in community centers, libraries, and local cafes. Advertise special promotions or new products in local newspapers or magazines, which can still reach a broad audience within State College.

The State College Business Community

State College has a rich business community, largely supported by the presence of Penn State University. This environment fosters collaboration among businesses, making it easier to connect with local entrepreneurs. Joining groups such as the Chamber of Business and Industry of Centre County can open doors to networking opportunities and resources. Here are some key points to consider:

  • Engage with local chambers or business associations: Being part of local business networks not only provides visibility but also offers support and resources. Look for networking events and workshops that can help you learn from other successful business owners and gain insight into best practices.
  • Participate in community initiatives: Getting involved in community initiatives that promote local shopping, like Small Business Saturday, can help increase your visibility and attract customers who are passionate about supporting local businesses.
  • Build relationships with surrounding businesses: Collaborate with neighboring businesses to create packages or joint events. For instance, a local restaurant could partner with a nearby brewery for a special tasting event. These collaborations can draw larger crowds and create a buzz around both establishments.

Conclusion

Attracting more customers in State College is about building connections and being proactive. By implementing a combination of these online and offline strategies, you can effectively increase your visibility and draw in new customers.
