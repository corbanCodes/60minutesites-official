How Do I Get More Customers in New Bedford?

5 min read

Real talk: small businesses in New Bedford have a unique charm that can draw customers in, but sometimes it takes a little strategy to really shine. With a blend of online and offline tactics, you can effectively boost your customer base and foster community support. New Bedford is rich in culture and history, and leveraging these attributes can truly set your business apart from the competition. Let’s dive into how you can strategically attract more customers and create lasting relationships within this vibrant community.

Online Strategies to Attract More Customers

Building a strong online presence is essential for capturing the attention of potential customers. Here are some practical strategies that can make a significant difference:

  • Optimize your website for local searches: Make sure your website is not just visually appealing but also functional. Incorporate keywords like 'New Bedford coffee shop' or 'unique gifts in New Bedford' throughout your site’s content. Use local landmarks and neighborhoods, such as mentioning your proximity to the New Bedford Whaling Museum or the Seaport Cultural District, to enhance your relevance in local searches.
  • Engage with customers on social media: Platforms like Facebook and Instagram are powerful tools for connecting with your community. Post regularly about your products, share behind-the-scenes content, and highlight your involvement in local events. Use local hashtags, such as #NewBedfordEats or #ShopLocalNewBedford, to reach a broader audience.
  • Utilize Google My Business: This free tool is a game changer for small businesses. Ensure your profile is complete with your address, phone number, business hours, and engaging photos of your products or services. Regularly update your listing with promotions or new offerings to keep your audience informed and engaged.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews: Word of mouth is powerful, especially in a close-knit community like New Bedford. Ask your happy customers to share their experiences on Google, Yelp, or Facebook. Consider offering a small incentive, like a discount on their next purchase, for leaving a review.
  • Run targeted online ads: Use platforms like Facebook Ads or Google Ads to reach specific demographics in the New Bedford area. Consider targeting ads based on interests or behaviors that align with your products, ensuring your message reaches those most likely to become customers.

Offline Strategies to Engage Locally

While online tactics are vital, don’t underestimate the power of face-to-face interactions. Here are some effective offline strategies to engage with the local community:

  • Participate in local events and festivals: Events like the New Bedford Folk Festival or the annual Whaling City Festival provide excellent opportunities to showcase your products. Set up a booth, offer samples, or host a demonstration to attract foot traffic and build connections.
  • Collaborate with other local businesses: Form partnerships with fellow entrepreneurs for cross-promotions. For example, if you own a bakery, consider teaming up with a local coffee shop to offer a 'coffee and pastry' deal that benefits both businesses.
  • Host workshops or community gatherings: Create a welcoming space for locals by organizing workshops or events that reflect your business's mission. Whether it’s a crafting night, a cooking class, or a book reading, these gatherings help foster community and loyalty.
  • Network with local organizations: Join the New Bedford Chamber of Commerce or other local organizations to build relationships and gain referrals. Attend their meetings and events to meet other business owners and share insights.
  • Consider traditional advertising: Don’t shy away from local newspapers or community bulletins. Advertisements in publications like The Standard-Times or local magazines can help you reach an audience that appreciates traditional forms of communication.

The New Bedford Business Community

The New Bedford business community is vibrant and supportive, filled with entrepreneurs eager to collaborate. Here’s how you can tap into this network to cultivate your business:

  • Join local business associations: Becoming a member of local associations can provide you with invaluable networking opportunities and resources. Many organizations host workshops, seminars, and social events that can connect you to potential customers and collaborators.
  • Engage in community service or sponsorships: Supporting local charities or events not only boosts your visibility but also enhances your reputation. Sponsor a local sports team or participate in charity events to showcase your commitment to the community.
  • Attend workshops and seminars: Stay informed by participating in local educational events. Learning from fellow business owners and industry experts can give you fresh perspectives and ideas to implement in your own business.
  • Stay active in local forums or online groups: Online platforms like Facebook or Nextdoor have local groups where community members discuss businesses, share recommendations, and seek advice. Being active in these forums can increase your visibility and credibility among local consumers.
  • Be a part of local initiatives: Engage in campaigns like “Shop Small” during the holidays, which encourages consumers to support small businesses. Participating in such initiatives can help you gain recognition and connect with like-minded business owners.

Building your customer base in New Bedford doesn’t have to be overwhelming. By using a mix of online and offline strategies, you can attract more customers and strengthen community ties.
