Why Your Google Business Profile Matters

Creating a Google Business Profile is essential for enhancing your visibility in both local and online searches. This free tool allows you to manage how your business appears in local search results. Here’s what you can do:

  • Showcase Your Business Information: Clearly display your business name, address, and phone number (NAP). Make sure this information is accurate to ensure customers can easily reach you.
  • Add Engaging Photos: Upload high-quality photos that reflect your business's personality. If you own a café, for example, include images of your delicious menu items and cozy seating areas. This visual appeal can entice potential customers.
  • Engage with Customer Reviews: Respond promptly to customer reviews, whether they are positive or negative. This interaction builds trust and shows potential customers that you care about their experience.
  • Post Updates and Offers: Regularly post updates about your services, special events, or promotions. For instance, if you’re hosting a workshop at your bookstore, make sure to promote it on your profile.
  • Track Insights: Utilize the insights feature to understand how customers are finding your business. This data can inform your marketing strategies and help you reach more people.

Steps to Set Up Your Google Business Profile

Getting started with your Google Business Profile is straightforward. Follow these steps to ensure your business is well represented online:

  • Visit the Google Business Profile Website: Go to the Google Business Profile website and sign in with your Google account. If you don’t have one, creating an account is quick and easy.
  • Click on ‘Manage Now’: Enter your business name carefully to avoid any typos, which could affect searchability.
  • Fill in Your Address and Service Area: Specify your business address and the areas you serve. If you operate a delivery service, for example, include the neighborhoods you cover.
  • Select the Right Business Category: Choosing the correct category helps Google understand what your business offers. For instance, if you run a bakery, select “Bakery” instead of a more general category.
  • Verify Your Business: Verification is crucial to establishing your legitimacy. Google typically sends a postcard with a verification code to your business address, but you may also have options for phone or email verification.

Once verified, you can start customizing your profile with additional details like your website link, hours of operation, and more.

How Locals in Ann Arbor Search for Businesses

Understanding how Ann Arbor residents look for businesses can help you tailor your profile effectively. Here’s what you should know:

  • Specific Search Terms: Many locals search for businesses using detailed phrases, such as ‘best coffee in Kerrytown’ or ‘top-rated pizza near the University of Michigan.’ Incorporating these keywords into your business description can improve your visibility.
  • Importance of Reviews: Consumers often gravitate toward businesses with solid reviews. Encourage satisfied customers to leave feedback and consider responding to reviews to create a dialogue.
  • Utilizing Local Landmarks: Mentioning local landmarks in your business description can significantly aid in searches. For instance, if you’re a boutique a short walk from the Ann Arbor Farmers Market, highlight that in your profile. This not only aids in SEO but also connects you to the community.
  • Keep Hours Updated: Many customers check for hours of operation before visiting. Make it a practice to keep this information current, especially during holidays or special events.
