How Do I Update My Old Website?

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Let me be direct with you: having an outdated website can feel like wearing last season's fashion. It’s time to refresh your online presence. Updating your old website doesn’t have to be a daunting task; with the right steps and a bit of creativity, you can breathe new life into it and captivate your audience. Whether you're a small business owner in a bustling city like Chicago or a freelancer in a quiet town, a modern website can help you stand out and connect with your audience.

Assess Your Current Website

Before diving into updates, take a moment to evaluate what you currently have. Examine your site’s layout, content, and overall functionality. Consider:

  • Is the design visually appealing? Look at your site as a visitor would. Does it reflect your brand? Are the images high-quality?
  • Is the content up to date and relevant? Review your text for outdated information, and ensure your services or products are accurately described.
  • Does your site load quickly on all devices? Use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights to check loading times. A fast-loading site keeps visitors engaged.
  • Are you using modern web standards? Check if your site is mobile-friendly, meaning it should adjust seamlessly to various screen sizes.

Taking the time to thoroughly assess your current website will provide clarity on what needs updating and what can stay.

Choose a Modern Design

The look and feel of your website are crucial. If your site design feels outdated, consider selecting a fresh template or redesigning it entirely. You can:

  • Look for responsive designs that work well on mobile and desktop. A responsive design ensures that your website looks great no matter what device your visitors are using.
  • Incorporate current design trends like minimalism, bold typography, and ample white space to create a clean and modern aesthetic. Websites should feel inviting, not cluttered.
  • Ensure your color scheme reflects your brand. Use tools like Adobe Color to create a palette that resonates with your target audience and enhances your brand identity.

Remember, a well-designed website builds trust and encourages visitors to explore further.

Update Your Content

Content is king, and keeping it fresh is essential. Review your pages and ask yourself:

  • Are there any outdated statistics or information? Replace old data with current research or trends that are relevant to your industry.
  • Does your content engage visitors and encourage them to take action? Use compelling calls-to-action (CTAs) like 'Get a Free Quote' or 'Subscribe for Updates' to guide users.
  • Are you targeting the right keywords to attract your audience? Use keyword research tools like Google Keyword Planner to find terms that your potential customers are searching for.

Make sure to add new blog posts, service descriptions, or client testimonials to keep your site dynamic. Highlighting recent projects or sharing customer success stories can create a personal connection with your audience.

Optimize for SEO

Search engine optimization is vital for visibility. Updating your old website should also include optimization techniques:

  • Check and update your meta titles and descriptions. These elements help search engines understand your content and can influence whether someone clicks on your link.
  • Use alt text for images to improve accessibility and SEO. Descriptive alt text helps search engines index your images and makes your site more user-friendly.
  • Ensure internal and external links are functioning well. Broken links can harm your search rankings and frustrate visitors, so use tools like Screaming Frog to identify any issues.

This will help your website rank higher in search results, making it easier for new customers to find you.

Make It User-Friendly

User experience can make or break a website. During your update, focus on:

  • Improving navigation so visitors can find what they need easily. Consider adding a search bar or simplifying your menu structure.
  • Adding clear calls-to-action to guide users. Make sure buttons stand out with contrasting colors and persuasive text.
  • Ensuring your website is accessible to everyone, including those with disabilities. Follow WCAG guidelines to make your site more inclusive, such as providing text alternatives for non-text content.

Creating a user-friendly experience not only helps retain visitors but also encourages them to return.

Choose the Right Tools for Updating

Finally, having the right tools can make updating your old website much easier. Consider using:

  • Website builders like 60 Minute Sites for an intuitive way to create and manage your site. With user-friendly interfaces, you can make updates quickly without needing extensive technical skills.
  • Content management systems (CMS) that let you update without coding. Platforms like WordPress or Wix provide flexible solutions for content management.
  • Analytics tools to track your website's performance post-update. Google Analytics can help you understand visitor behavior and measure the success of your updates.

By leveraging the right tools, you can streamline your process and focus on creating great content.
