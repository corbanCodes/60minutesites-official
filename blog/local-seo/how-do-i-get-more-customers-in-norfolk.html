How Do I Get More Customers in Norfolk?

9 min read

Getting customers in Norfolk, Virginia can feel like a daunting task, but with the right strategies, you can stand out in this bustling community. The local market is ripe with opportunities for service businesses willing to hustle and connect with their audience. Known for its rich maritime history and vibrant arts scene, Norfolk offers a unique backdrop for businesses to thrive, especially along the scenic waterfront and in the historic Ghent district.

Your Website is the Foundation

The truth is, you need a professional website. It’s your digital storefront and the first impression many customers will have of your business. Here’s what actually works:

  • Make sure it’s mobile-friendly; a lot of folks will search on their phones as they explore the city’s attractions or look for services nearby.
  • Optimize for local SEO so you show up when someone searches for your services in Norfolk, especially for keywords like 'best coffee in Ghent' or 'plumbing services near Waterside District.'
  • Include clear calls-to-action to encourage visitors to contact you or book services, making it easy for them to engage with your business.

Leverage Google My Business

This is a game changer for local visibility. If you’re not on Google My Business, you’re missing out. Here’s what you should do:

  • Claim and verify your listing to ensure your business appears in local searches, particularly for those looking for services while exploring attractions like the Norfolk Botanical Garden.
  • Keep your business hours up to date, especially during local events like the Norfolk Harborfest or holiday seasons when foot traffic increases.
  • Post updates and offers to keep customers engaged and informed about your business.
  • Encourage customers to leave reviews to build credibility, which is crucial in a community that values local recommendations.

Harness the Power of Reviews

Reviews can make or break a small business. Here’s how to use them to your advantage:

  • Ask satisfied customers to leave reviews on Google and Yelp, particularly after they’ve enjoyed a visit to local landmarks like the Chrysler Museum of Art or the Nauticus.
  • Respond to all reviews, positive or negative, to show you care about customer feedback and are engaged with the community.
  • Feature glowing reviews on your website and social media as testimonials to attract new clients.

Networking and Referrals

Never underestimate the power of word-of-mouth. Here’s how to cultivate referrals:

  • Attend local networking events and business meet-ups in Norfolk, such as those hosted by the Norfolk Chamber of Commerce or the Downtown Norfolk Council.
  • Join local business groups or chambers of commerce to connect with other entrepreneurs and share resources.
  • Offer referral discounts to customers who send new clients your way, which can be especially effective in a close-knit community that values personal connections.

Get Social (Media, That Is)

Social media is a powerful tool for small businesses. Here’s what you should focus on:

  • Choose platforms where your target audience hangs out, such as Facebook or Instagram, particularly to engage with the young professionals and families that populate areas like the Neon District.
  • Share engaging content, such as behind-the-scenes looks at your business or customer testimonials, to create a sense of community.
  • Run targeted ads to reach local customers in Norfolk, especially during seasonal events or festivals that draw crowds to the city.
