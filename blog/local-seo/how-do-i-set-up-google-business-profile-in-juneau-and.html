Why Set Up a Google Business Profile?

A well-optimized Google Business Profile can be a game-changer for your visibility and customer engagement. Here’s what it offers:

  • Enhanced Local Search Presence: With a Google Business Profile, your business will appear in local searches and on Google Maps, making it easier for both locals and tourists to find you. For example, when someone searches for "restaurants near Mendenhall Glacier," your business can show up if you are listed correctly.
  • Customer Reviews and Ratings: Reviews play a crucial role in attracting new customers. A strong collection of positive reviews can significantly boost your credibility. Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews and respond to them promptly to show you value their feedback.
  • Accurate Business Information: Ensure your business details—such as address, phone number, and operating hours—are accurate. This information is crucial for helping potential customers find you easily, especially during peak tourist seasons.
  • Better Engagement Through Posts and Updates: Use your profile to post updates about special offers, events, or new products. Sharing local stories or insights can create a deeper connection with your audience. For instance, highlight a local fishing trip or a partnership with nearby artisans.
  • Insights into Customer Interactions: Google provides analytics that help you understand how customers find and interact with your profile. Utilize this data to refine your marketing strategies and improve customer service.

Steps to Set Up Your Google Business Profile

Getting your Google Business Profile set up is straightforward and can significantly boost your online presence. Follow these actionable steps:

  • Go to the Google Business Profile website: Start by visiting the Google Business Profile homepage to begin the setup process.
  • Click on 'Manage now' and enter your business name: If your business has multiple locations, make sure to select the correct one to avoid confusion.
  • Add your business address: Ensure that your address is accurate and formatted correctly for Juneau. This is essential for local SEO. If you don't have a physical storefront, you can specify the areas you serve.
  • Choose the right category: Selecting the appropriate category is crucial for visibility. For example, if you run a coffee shop, choose "Coffee Shop" instead of a generic term like "Cafe." This specificity helps Google direct the right customers to you.
  • Enter your contact information: Include a local phone number and a link to your website. If you don’t have a website, consider utilizing services like 60 Minute Sites to create one quickly and efficiently.
  • Verify your business: Google usually sends a verification postcard to your business address. Follow the instructions provided to complete this process, as verification enhances trust and visibility.
  • Once verified, enhance your profile: Upload high-quality images of your business, products, and services. Include your business hours and any special hours for holidays or events to keep customers informed.

How Locals in Juneau Search for Businesses

Understanding how locals search can help tailor your Google Business Profile and attract more customers. Here are some common strategies:

  • Utilizing local keywords: Use phrases that locals might search for, such as "best seafood in Juneau" or "outdoor gear rental near Auke Bay." Incorporating these keywords into your profile can drive relevant traffic to your business.
  • Searching on Google Maps: Many locals turn to Google Maps to find businesses nearby. Ensure that your profile is fully optimized so that it appears prominently on the map when users search for services.
  • Reading reviews: Customers often read reviews before making a decision. Strive to maintain a positive reputation by encouraging reviews from happy customers and addressing any negative feedback thoughtfully.
  • Asking for recommendations: Locals frequently seek advice within community groups on social media or during local events. Engage with these platforms to promote your services and encourage word-of-mouth referrals. Consider collaborating with local events or sponsoring community initiatives to build relationships.
