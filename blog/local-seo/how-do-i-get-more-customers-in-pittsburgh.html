How Do I Get More Customers in Pittsburgh?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Getting customers in Pittsburgh can be a rewarding challenge. This city, known for its rich history in steel production and its transformation into a hub for technology and healthcare, has a vibrant local economy. However, you need the right strategies to stand out amidst the competition.

Establish Your Online Presence

Your website is the foundation of reaching customers online. A professional website not only acts as your digital storefront but also helps build credibility. Consider these steps:

  • Create a clean, user-friendly website that reflects your brand.
  • Optimize your site for local SEO to show up in Pittsburgh searches, utilizing keywords like 'Pittsburgh services' or 'local Pittsburgh businesses'.
  • Use clear calls-to-action to guide visitors on what to do next.
  • Ensure your site is mobile-friendly, as many customers search on their phones.
  • Include a blog to share insights about Pittsburgh's local culture and events, establishing your expertise.

Leverage Google My Business

I've seen this work time and time again in Pittsburgh. Google My Business is crucial for local visibility. Here’s what you should do:

  • Claim and verify your business listing.
  • Keep your information up-to-date (hours, location near landmarks like Point State Park or the Andy Warhol Museum, and contact).
  • Post updates and offers directly to your listing, especially during local events like the Three Rivers Arts Festival.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews, as they significantly boost your ranking.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Word of mouth is gold in Pittsburgh. Here’s how to build your reputation:

  • Ask happy customers for reviews on Google and Yelp, especially after serving clients in bustling neighborhoods like Shadyside or South Side.
  • Provide exceptional service that encourages referrals.
  • Consider creating a referral program to incentivize word-of-mouth marketing.
  • Share customer testimonials on your website and social media, showcasing your connection to the local community.

Be Active on Social Media

The hustle of running a local business means you need to be seen. Social media can help you connect with potential customers:

  • Choose platforms where your audience hangs out (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter).
  • Post engaging content such as behind-the-scenes glimpses of your business, customer stories, and promotions tailored to local events or Pittsburgh sports teams.
  • Use local hashtags like #PittsburghProud or #PittsburghEats to reach a Pittsburgh audience.
  • Engage with followers by responding to comments and messages promptly.

Network and Collaborate Locally

Pittsburgh's business community is supportive and collaborative. Here’s how to tap into it:

  • Attend local networking events like those hosted by the Pittsburgh Technology Council to meet other business owners.
  • Join local chambers of commerce or business associations to stay informed about opportunities.
  • Partner with other businesses for cross-promotions or events, particularly those held in popular areas like Station Square.
  • Participate in community events such as the Pittsburgh Vintage Grand Prix to raise awareness of your brand.

With the right strategies tailored to the unique character of Pittsburgh, you can carve out your niche in the local market. Stay persistent, keep engaging with your community, and watch your customer base grow.
