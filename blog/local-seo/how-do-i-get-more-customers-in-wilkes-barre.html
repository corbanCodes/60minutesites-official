How Do I Get More Customers in Wilkes-Barre?

5 min read

Wilkes-Barre is a city steeped in history, known for its warm community spirit and a thriving small business scene. If you're looking to attract more customers, it's essential to embrace both online and offline strategies that resonate with locals. Whether you're a quaint café on South Main Street or a boutique in the bustling River Street corridor, there are numerous ways to elevate your presence and draw in more patrons.

Online Strategies to Attract More Customers

Establishing a strong online presence is essential for any business in Wilkes-Barre. Here are some actionable strategies to consider:

  • Optimize Your Website for Local Searches: Ensure your website is search engine optimized (SEO) for keywords that potential customers might use, such as 'best coffee shop in Wilkes-Barre' or 'affordable auto repair near me.' Incorporate location-based keywords into your content, meta descriptions, and titles. Using tools like Google Keyword Planner can help identify effective terms.
  • Engage on Social Media: Platforms like Facebook and Instagram are perfect for showcasing your offerings. Post high-quality images of your products or services, share stories about your business journey, and highlight customer testimonials. Consider creating a dedicated hashtag like #WilkesBarreEats to encourage customer engagement and sharing.
  • Utilize Google My Business: Claim and optimize your Google My Business listing. This tool allows you to manage how your business appears on Google Search and Maps, making it easier for locals to find you. Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews, as these will enhance your visibility and credibility.
  • Run Targeted Online Ads: Platforms like Facebook Ads or Google Ads allow you to target specific demographics based on location, interests, and behaviors. For instance, if you run a family-friendly restaurant, target ads to parents in nearby neighborhoods. This precision can help you reach your ideal customers more effectively.
  • Start an Email Newsletter: Collect emails through your website and in-store, and send out regular newsletters. Share updates about new products, exclusive promotions, and upcoming events. This keeps your business top-of-mind for customers and encourages repeat visits.

Offline Strategies to Draw in Local Shoppers

Engaging with the local community can be just as crucial as your online efforts. Here are some effective offline strategies:

  • Participate in Local Events: Events like the Wilkes-Barre Cherry Blossom Festival or the Luzerne County Fair are excellent opportunities to showcase your business. Set up a booth, offer samples, or run a contest to attract visitors and create memorable interactions.
  • Collaborate with Other Local Businesses: Form partnerships with other businesses in your area. For example, if you own a bakery, collaborate with a local coffee shop to create a special combo offer. This not only draws in new customers but also strengthens the local business network.
  • Host Workshops or Classes: Organize workshops that relate to your business and invite the community to participate. For instance, if you run a craft store, host a DIY class that allows attendees to create their own projects. This positions you as an expert and builds a loyal customer base.
  • Distribute Flyers and Postcards: Create visually appealing flyers or postcards that highlight your services or promotions, and distribute them in local neighborhoods or at community centers. Consider targeting areas with high foot traffic or those near your business.
  • Join Local Chambers of Commerce: Membership in the Greater Wilkes-Barre Chamber of Commerce can provide networking opportunities and access to resources that benefit your business. Attend their events, participate in workshops, and connect with other local entrepreneurs.

The Wilkes-Barre Business Community

The business community in Wilkes-Barre is vibrant and diverse, offering a unique blend of opportunities for collaboration and growth. Here are some insights:

  • Embrace Collaboration: The local business scene thrives on collaboration. By supporting fellow entrepreneurs, whether through joint promotions or community clean-up events, you create a network of support that benefits everyone.
  • Attend Networking Events: Regular networking events, hosted by local organizations, allow you to connect with other business owners. These gatherings are excellent for sharing ideas, discussing challenges, and discovering potential partnerships.
  • Leverage Local Resources: The Greater Wilkes-Barre Chamber of Commerce offers a wealth of resources, including workshops that cover everything from marketing strategies to financial management. Take advantage of these educational opportunities to enhance your business acumen.
  • Get Involved in Community Initiatives: Participate in local charity events, sponsor youth sports teams, or contribute to community projects. This involvement not only builds goodwill but also enhances your visibility in the community.
  • Highlight Local Culture: Wilkes-Barre boasts a rich history and cultural diversity. Incorporate local themes into your marketing efforts, whether through special promotions during local festivals or collaborations with artists from the area. This resonates well with residents who take pride in their community.
