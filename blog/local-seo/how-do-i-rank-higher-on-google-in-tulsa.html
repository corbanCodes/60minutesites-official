Hey there, Tulsa business owners! If you're looking to boost your local SEO game and get more customers through your doors, you're in the right spot. Optimizing Your Google My Business Listing

Your Google My Business (GMB) listing is crucial for local SEO in Tulsa. Here's how to make it shine:

  • Claim and verify your listing, ensuring you appear on Google Maps and local search results.
  • Fill out all your business information completely – hours, address, phone number, website, and categories relevant to your business.
  • Use high-quality images of your business and products; consider including images of your location in relation to local landmarks like the Golden Driller or the Philbrook Museum of Art.
  • Encourage happy customers to leave positive reviews and respond to them promptly, showcasing your commitment to customer service.
  • Post updates, offers, or events to keep your listing active and engage with the community.

Using Local Keywords Effectively

Keywords are the backbone of search engine optimization. To rank well in Tulsa searches, you need to use them wisely:

  • Research keywords relevant to your business and incorporate local terms like 'Tulsa' and neighborhood names, such as 'Brookside', 'Midtown', and 'South Tulsa'.
  • Use these keywords in your website content, titles, meta descriptions, and headers to improve visibility.
  • Consider phrases that locals might search for, like 'best coffee in Brookside' or 'affordable plumbing in Midtown', and tailor your content accordingly.

Creating Local Content

Content is king, especially when it comes to local SEO. Here's how to create content that resonates:

  • Write blog posts about local events, news, or community happenings, including annual events like the Tulsa State Fair or the Tulsa Tough cycling event.
  • Share stories that relate to Tulsa's culture and vibe – maybe a behind-the-scenes look at your business's involvement in local art exhibitions or concerts at the BOK Center.
  • Create guides or lists that highlight other local businesses, fostering community connections and backlinks, such as 'Top 10 Places to Eat in the Brady Arts District'.

Building Local Backlinks

Backlinks from local sites can boost your authority. Here's how to earn them:

  • Partner with local organizations or events, like the Tulsa Arts District, to get featured on their sites and increase your visibility.
  • Offer to guest blog for local publications, bringing awareness to your business and establishing yourself as a thought leader in your field.
  • Participate in local forums or community boards, such as those that discuss upcoming events or local issues, to build your presence and network with potential customers.

What Makes Tulsa Special

Tulsa is a gem with its unique blend of history, culture, and community spirit. From the iconic Gathering Place, a sprawling riverfront park, to the bustling Cherry Street District known for its eclectic shops and eateries, there's a sense of pride in supporting local businesses here. The weather might have its ups and downs, but the warmth of the people and the vibrant arts scene make it a fantastic place to run a business. Plus, with events like the Tulsa State Fair, the Blue Dome Arts Festival, and the Tulsa International Mayfest, there's always an opportunity to connect with potential customers!
