How Do I Get More Customers in Asheville?

5 min read

Look, I get it. Running a small business in Asheville can feel like a wild ride, with the stunning Blue Ridge Mountain views and a bustling community to navigate. As a small business owner, your goal is to stand out and attract more customers, but it can be daunting to know where to even begin. Let’s explore some effective strategies—both online and offline—that can help your business thrive in this vibrant town filled with art, culture, and a supportive community spirit.

Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Creating a strong online presence is essential in attracting new customers in Asheville's bustling market. Here are some actionable steps you can take to elevate your business:

  • Create a user-friendly website: Ensure your website is easy to navigate, visually appealing, and mobile-friendly. Showcase your products or services with high-quality images and clear descriptions. Include an 'About Us' section that shares your story, which can resonate with locals who appreciate authenticity.
  • Leverage social media: Platforms like Instagram and Facebook are powerful tools for engaging with your audience. Share behind-the-scenes content, local stories, and promote upcoming events or specials. Use local hashtags to increase your visibility and connect with community members, such as #AshevilleEats or #AshevilleArt.
  • Optimize your Google My Business listing: Make sure all your information is up to date—your address, phone number, hours of operation, and website link. Regularly post updates and photos to your listing to improve your visibility in local searches, making it easier for potential customers to find you.
  • Encourage customer reviews: Actively ask satisfied customers to leave reviews on platforms like Google, Yelp, and Facebook. Positive reviews build trust and credibility, making new customers more likely to choose your business over competitors.
  • Run targeted online ads: Consider using Facebook or Google Ads to specifically target potential customers in Asheville. Tailor your ads to promote any unique offerings or events you have, ensuring they resonate with the local audience.

Offline Strategies to Connect with the Local Community

While online strategies are vital, don’t overlook the immense value of face-to-face interactions in Asheville. Here’s how you can make a real impact in the community:

  • Network with other local businesses: Reach out to nearby business owners to collaborate on events, promotions, or even co-hosting a local market. This not only increases your visibility but also fosters a sense of community.
  • Participate in community events: Join local festivals, farmers' markets, or art walks to showcase your offerings. Events like the Asheville Art Festival or the Downtown Asheville Art District's First Friday can be great venues to connect with potential customers.
  • Host open houses or workshops: Create opportunities for people to visit your space, whether it’s through an open house or a hands-on workshop. This personal touch helps build relationships and gives potential customers a taste of what you offer.
  • Partner with local influencers: Collaborate with local bloggers or social media influencers who share your values. They can help amplify your story to their followers, creating authentic connections with a wider audience.
  • Offer special promotions: Design exclusive deals for local residents to encourage loyalty. This could be a discount for first-time customers or a loyalty program that rewards repeat visits, making locals feel valued.

Embracing the Asheville Business Community

The Asheville business community is a unique blend of creativity and camaraderie. Here’s how to tap into that spirit and strengthen your presence:

  • Join local business associations: Become a member of organizations like the Asheville Area Chamber of Commerce. This not only helps you stay informed about local events and opportunities but also connects you with other business owners who can offer support and collaboration.
  • Attend local meetups and networking events: Engage in community networking events to share ideas and foster relationships. Events hosted by organizations like the Asheville Downtown Association can be great opportunities to meet fellow business owners.
  • Support fellow local businesses: Promote and collaborate with your neighbors. For example, if you run a café, consider sourcing pastries from a nearby bakery. This not only supports the local economy but also attracts their customer base to your business.
  • Engage with local art and music scenes: Align your business with Asheville's vibrant culture by hosting local artists or musicians. This can attract their audience to your space and create a lively atmosphere that draws in foot traffic.
