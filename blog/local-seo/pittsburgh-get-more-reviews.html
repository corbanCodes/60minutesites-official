How to Get More Google Reviews in Pittsburgh

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Let's get real for a moment: your business might offer the best products and services in Pittsburgh, but if no one is talking about it online, it can feel like you're invisible. Getting more Google reviews can change that, boosting your visibility and credibility. Here’s how to encourage happy customers to share their experiences, so that your business shines in the bustling Pittsburgh marketplace.

Make It Easy for Customers to Leave Reviews

The simpler you make the process, the more likely your customers are to leave a review. Consider implementing the following actionable steps:

  • Share a direct link: Use the Google Maps link to your business to make accessing your review page a breeze. You can find this link by searching for your business on Google Maps, clicking on the 'Write a Review' button, and copying the URL. Share this link in emails, social media posts, and on your website.
  • Add review requests to emails: After a purchase, send a friendly follow-up email thanking customers for their business and gently requesting a review. Personalize your message by mentioning the specific product or service they purchased, which can prompt them to reflect on their positive experience.
  • QR codes: Print a QR code that links directly to your Google review page and place it in your store, on receipts, or even on business cards. This provides a quick and easy way for customers to leave feedback while their experience is still fresh in their minds.

Ask at the Right Moment

The timing of your request can make all the difference. Here are some moments to seize for maximum impact:

  • After a great interaction: If you notice a customer is particularly happy—perhaps they’re smiling or expressing satisfaction—don’t hesitate to ask them right then and there. A personal touch can make your request feel genuine and heartfelt.
  • During special occasions: Consider asking for reviews during events like customer appreciation days, local festivals, or after successful service completion. For example, if your business is involved in the Three Rivers Arts Festival, leverage that occasion to ask attendees to share their experiences.
  • At checkout: Train your staff to ask for reviews when customers are in high spirits as they complete their purchase. A simple, enthusiastic request can significantly increase your chances of receiving a review.

Respond to Existing Reviews

Engaging with your current reviews can encourage more customers to share their thoughts. Here’s how to effectively respond:

  • Thank reviewers: A simple "thank you" goes a long way and shows that you value customer feedback. Consider mentioning specific details from their review to personalize your response.
  • Address concerns: If you receive negative reviews, respond professionally and offer to resolve the issue. This demonstrates your commitment to customer satisfaction and can turn a dissatisfied customer into a loyal one. For instance, if someone complains about a long wait time, acknowledge it and offer a discount on their next visit.
  • Showcase positive reviews: Highlight glowing reviews on your website or social media to inspire others to contribute their own. You could create a “Customer Spotlight” section on your website or feature reviews in your Instagram stories.

Leverage Social Media and Local Events

Your presence on social media and at local events can amplify your request for reviews. Consider these strategies:

  • Promote reviews: Use your social media platforms to encourage followers to leave reviews. You could even host a giveaway as an incentive—enter customers who leave a review into a raffle for a gift card or a product from your store.
  • Participate in community events: Get involved in local events, like those at Point State Park or the Strip District, where you can set up a booth or sponsor a local team. Ask attendees to leave reviews after engaging with your business. Having a physical presence can create a lasting impression that translates into online feedback.
  • Local partnerships: Collaborate with other local businesses to promote each other and share your review links together. For example, if you're a coffee shop, partner with a nearby bakery to offer a combined deal, and ask customers to share reviews about both businesses.

Building a strong collection of Google reviews can significantly enhance your business's online presence in Pittsburgh. By creating a seamless process for customers to leave feedback and engaging with them actively, you can foster a community of satisfied customers who are eager to share their experiences.
