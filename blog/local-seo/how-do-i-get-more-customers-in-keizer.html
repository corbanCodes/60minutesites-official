Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Establishing a robust online presence is critical for small businesses today. Here’s a detailed breakdown of effective strategies you can employ:

  • Engage on social media: Leverage platforms such as Facebook and Instagram not only to promote products but also to create a community. Offline Strategies to Boost Foot Traffic

Even in a digital world, offline strategies remain powerful tools to attract customers. Here are some actionable ideas to consider:

  • Host local events or workshops: Organize classes or events that align with your business. For instance, a bakery might host a cupcake decorating workshop, while a craft store could hold DIY sessions. Events like these not only draw potential customers into your space but also create a sense of community.
  • Collaborate with local businesses: Partner with nearby stores to cross-promote each other's offerings. For example, a coffee shop could team up with a local bookstore for a reading event, enticing customers of both businesses to explore new options.
  • Participate in community fairs and markets: Get involved in local events such as the Keizer Little League games or the Keizer Art Association’s events. Set up a booth to showcase your products, engage with the community, and distribute flyers or samples to entice future visits to your store.
  • Use flyers and local advertising: Design eye-catching flyers or posters and distribute them in community centers, libraries, and local cafes. Consider placing ads in local newspapers or sponsoring local events to increase your visibility among residents who may not be active online.

Engaging with the Keizer Business Community

The Keizer business community is thriving with opportunities for collaboration and growth. By connecting with local organizations, you can amplify your outreach efforts:

  • Join the Keizer Chamber of Commerce: This organization is a valuable resource for networking with other business owners, gaining insights, and accessing marketing resources. Attend monthly meetings or events to establish relationships and partnerships that could benefit your business.
  • Attend local networking events: Look for networking events hosted by community organizations, such as business mixers or entrepreneur meet-ups. These gatherings are perfect for exchanging ideas, sharing experiences, and finding potential collaborators.
  • Engage with local nonprofits: Partnering with local charities or nonprofits for fundraising events not only helps the community but also raises your business profile. Sponsor an event, donate a portion of sales, or organize a volunteer day with your team to foster goodwill and connect with local residents.
  • Participate in local initiatives: Get involved in Keizer’s initiatives aimed at promoting the city as a business-friendly environment. Whether it’s contributing to local clean-up days or supporting community beautification projects, being visible in these efforts showcases your commitment to the area.
