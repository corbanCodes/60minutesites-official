Calendly vs Acuity: Which Is Better?

5 min read

Finding the right scheduling tool can feel like searching for a needle in a haystack, especially when two heavyweights like Calendly and Acuity Scheduling stand out. Both platforms promise to simplify your life and boost your productivity, but how do you choose between them? Let’s take a deep dive into their differences, features, and what might work best for you.

User Experience and Interface

First impressions matter, and the user interface can make or break your scheduling experience. Both Calendly and Acuity offer intuitive designs, but they differ significantly in their navigation and usability. Here’s a closer look:

  • Calendly is famous for its straightforward, minimalist approach. The clean layout allows first-time users to dive in without a steep learning curve. You can easily create event types and share your availability with just a few clicks. The dashboard is uncluttered, making it easy to view your upcoming events at a glance.
  • Acuity provides a more comprehensive dashboard that offers detailed customization options. If you need control over your branding, client intake forms, and appointment types, this platform is perfect for you. Its interface may feel slightly more complex at first, but the depth of customization available is worth the initial learning curve.
  • Both platforms are mobile-friendly, allowing you to manage appointments on the go. Whether you’re in a coffee shop in downtown Seattle or at a client meeting in New York City, you can access your calendar anytime, anywhere.

Features and Customization

Next up, let’s dive into the features that make each tool unique. You want a solution that fits your specific needs, right? Here’s how they stack up:

  • Calendly shines with its simplicity, offering core features like time zone detection and automatic calendar syncing. It allows you to set your availability, and clients can book appointments without the back-and-forth emails. Calendly also supports one-on-one and group meetings, making it versatile for various scheduling needs.
  • Acuity takes it a step further with advanced features such as customizable intake forms, allowing you to gather essential information before your meetings. You can also create multiple appointment types, each with its settings, and even add custom branding options to make your scheduling experience reflect your business identity. This is especially beneficial for service-based businesses that require specific details from clients before appointments.
  • If you run a small business or offer services that require more tailored scheduling, Acuity’s extensive customization capabilities might be your best bet. For example, you can set buffer times between appointments, define cancellation policies, and automate reminders to clients, ensuring a smooth scheduling process.

Pricing Structures

Money matters, and understanding the pricing can help you make the best choice for your budget. Here’s how the two compare:

  • Calendly offers a free tier, which is great for basic users who just need to schedule a few meetings a month. Paid plans start at a reasonable monthly fee, offering additional features like advanced integrations and the ability to remove Calendly branding from your scheduling links.
  • Acuity also has a free option, but its paid plans provide more robust features, which can be more beneficial for service-based businesses. For instance, with the paid tiers, you can access features like text message reminders and the ability to sell packages or subscriptions directly through the platform.
  • Consider your long-term needs—will you benefit from the additional features in Acuity, or is Calendly’s simplicity all you need? Think about your usage patterns and the potential return on investment for your business.

Integrations and Compatibility

Integration with other tools can significantly enhance your workflow. Both Calendly and Acuity offer a variety of integrations:

  • Calendly integrates seamlessly with popular platforms like Zoom, Google Calendar, and Microsoft Teams, making it an excellent choice if you frequently conduct virtual meetings. The automatic syncing of events ensures that your schedule remains up-to-date across all platforms.
  • Acuity goes a step further, connecting with payment processors like PayPal and Stripe, as well as various marketing tools, including Mailchimp and Constant Contact. This is particularly useful for entrepreneurs who need to manage appointments while also marketing their services or collecting payments online.
  • If you're looking for a scheduling tool that can harmonize with your existing workflow, think about which integrations are vital for you. For example, if you run a yoga studio in Austin, Texas, having a scheduling tool that connects with your payment processing and email marketing systems can save you time and streamline your operations.

Ultimately, choosing between Calendly and Acuity boils down to your specific needs and preferences. If you're looking for straightforward, hassle-free scheduling, Calendly might be your go-to. On the other hand, if you need extensive customization and advanced features, Acuity could be the better fit.
