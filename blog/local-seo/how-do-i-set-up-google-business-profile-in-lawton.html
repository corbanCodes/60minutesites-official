Why Set Up a Google Business Profile?

Your Google Business Profile acts as a digital storefront, providing essential visibility and credibility to your business. Here are some compelling reasons to create one:

  • Increased Visibility: With so many people searching for local services, a Google Business Profile ensures that your business appears in local search results and on Google Maps, making it easier for potential customers in Lawton to find you.
  • Updates and Promotions: You can post updates about your business, such as new services, special offers, or events. For example, if you're a local restaurant, you could announce a happy hour or a weekend brunch special, enticing more customers to visit.
  • Customer Reviews: Positive reviews from satisfied customers boost your credibility. A strong collection of reviews can persuade newcomers to choose your business over others. Encourage patrons to leave feedback, as this social proof is invaluable in building trust.
  • Insights and Analytics: Google provides insights that show how customers find your business—whether through direct searches or discovery—along with their actions, such as calls or visits to your website. This information can guide your marketing strategies and help you cater to local preferences.

Step-by-Step Guide to Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Setting up your Google Business Profile is straightforward and can significantly enhance your online presence. Follow these steps to ensure everything is done correctly:

  • Visit the Google Business Profile website: Start by going to the Google Business Profile homepage and clicking on 'Manage now.' This is your gateway to creating your business listing.
  • Sign in or create an account: Use your existing Google account or create a new one if you don’t have it yet. It’s a good idea to use an account you regularly check, as Google may send important notifications and updates to this email.
  • Enter Your Business Details: Accurately input your business name and address. This information should match what’s listed on your website and any physical signage to maintain consistency. A well-structured address helps with local SEO and ensures customers can find you without confusion.
  • Select a Business Category: Choose the most relevant category for your business. For instance, if you own a bakery, select 'Bakery' to help Google categorize your business effectively. This selection can significantly impact how you appear in search results.
  • Add Contact Information: Include your business phone number and website URL. If you don’t have a website yet, consider using services like 60 Minute Sites, which can help you create a professional site quickly.
  • Verify Your Listing: Verification is crucial. You can often verify your listing by mail or phone. Google will send a postcard with a verification code to your business address, which you’ll need to input to gain control over your profile.
  • Complete Your Profile: Once verified, fill out your profile with as much relevant information as possible. Include your business hours, high-quality photos of your products or services, and a detailed description of what you offer. A well-rounded profile can help attract more customers.

How Locals in Lawton Search for Businesses

Understanding the local search habits in Lawton can give your business a significant advantage. Residents often turn to Google for nearby services and shops. Here’s how they typically search:

  • Localized Search Terms: Many residents use phrases like 'best coffee shop near me' or 'plumbers in Lawton.' When setting up your profile, incorporate these keywords into your business description to improve your visibility in search results.
  • Google Maps Usage: Locals frequently check Google Maps for directions, business hours, and contact information, especially when they're out and about. Ensure that your hours of operation are accurate and updated, as this can influence whether someone decides to visit.
  • Review Reading: Before making a decision, potential customers often read reviews and ratings. Positive feedback can be a deciding factor in choosing your business over a competitor. Consider responding to reviews to show engagement and appreciation for customer feedback.
  • Promotions and Events: Many locals look for special promotions or events that businesses post directly on their profiles. Whether you're hosting a community event or offering discounts during holidays, make sure to highlight these opportunities to attract more visitors.
