How to Find a Restaurant in St Louis

5 min read

Imagine walking down the cobblestone streets of St. Louis, the aroma of toasted ravioli wafting through the air, and you realize it’s time to find a restaurant that captures the heart of this vibrant city. With countless options available, how do you choose the perfect spot for your meal? This guide offers practical tips and local insights to help you navigate the culinary landscape of St. Louis and discover the best dining experiences it has to offer.

Explore Local Neighborhoods

Each neighborhood in St. Louis has its own unique flavor, influencing the restaurants you’ll encounter. To uncover hidden gems, consider exploring:

  • The Hill: This charming neighborhood is renowned for its authentic Italian cuisine. Here, you can indulge in classic dishes like spaghetti and meatballs or grab a plate of gnocchi at places like Noto's or Rigazzi's. Don't forget to stop by the iconic St. Louis Bread Company for a fresh loaf of Italian bread!
  • Forest Park: Home to one of the largest urban parks in the United States, this area offers a mix of casual eateries and fine dining. After a visit to the St. Louis Art Museum, enjoy a meal at The Boathouse with stunning views of the lake or opt for the upscale Eleven Eleven Mississippi for a chic dining experience.
  • Delmar Loop: Famous for its eclectic food scene and vibrant atmosphere, the Loop is a must-visit. Try Blueberry Hill, a classic diner that has hosted music legends, or sample unique dishes at Salt + Smoke, known for its mouthwatering barbecue.
  • South Grand: This diverse neighborhood showcases a variety of international options. From the delectable Vietnamese cuisine at Mai Lee to the authentic Mexican fare at La Vallesana, you’ll find something to satisfy every craving.

Use Local Guides and Apps

Utilizing local guides or restaurant apps can simplify your search significantly. Here are some popular tools that can help you discover the best spots:

  • Yelp: This platform offers reviews and ratings from locals, helping you gauge popular dishes and overall experiences. Search for restaurants by category or location, and filter by price range to find options that fit your budget.
  • TripAdvisor: Perfect for finding top-ranked spots, this app provides insights into popular attractions and dining experiences. Look for trending restaurants or read traveler reviews to choose the best dining options.
  • Google Maps: Not only does it help with navigation, but Google Maps also allows you to discover nearby options based on your current location. You can view photos, menus, and read reviews all in one place.
  • Social media platforms: Instagram is a goldmine for food lovers. Follow local eateries like Farmhaus or Pappy's Smokehouse to see what's trending, what dishes are being featured, and any specials they might be running.

Ask for Recommendations

Don’t hesitate to ask for recommendations! Sometimes, the best dining suggestions come from locals who know the city well. Here’s how to gather insights:

  • Chat with locals: Whether you’re at a coffee shop or a bar, strike up a conversation with St. Louis residents. They may point you to their favorite hidden gems that aren’t on tourist maps.
  • Join community groups: Engage with St. Louis community groups on social media platforms like Facebook. These groups often share personal recommendations and restaurant reviews, providing a wealth of local knowledge.
  • Seek tips from hotel staff: Hotel concierges are often a goldmine of information. They can provide you with their favorite spots, especially restaurants that might not be widely known but offer incredible food and ambiance.

Consider Your Cravings

To narrow down your options, take a moment to think about what type of cuisine you’re in the mood for. Some popular choices in St. Louis include:

  • Barbecue: Known for its rich flavors, St. Louis barbecue is a must-try. Places like Pappy's Smokehouse and Smokey O's are famous for their tender ribs and pulled pork, with Pappy's even having a unique dry rub that locals rave about.
  • Italian: Especially in The Hill, where tradition thrives. Restaurants like Charlie Gitto's serve up exquisite pasta dishes, while Fazio's is known for its homemade ravioli.
  • Craft beer and gastropubs: St. Louis boasts a vibrant craft beer scene. Visit 4 Hands Brewing Co. for a local brew and enjoy it alongside delicious bites from their food truck partners.
  • Vegan and vegetarian options: The city also caters to plant-based diners, with places like Alchemist offering innovative vegan dishes that even meat-eaters will love.

Look for Special Events

St. Louis frequently hosts food festivals and events that showcase local culinary talent. Here’s how to find them:

  • Check local event calendars: Websites like St. Louis Magazine and Explore St. Louis regularly post about food-related events. Keep an eye out for festivals like Feast in the Park that celebrate local chefs and their creations.
  • Follow local food blogs: Blogs such as St. Louis Eats and Sauce Magazine are great resources for updates on pop-up dining events and special tasting menus at various restaurants.
  • Visit farmers' markets: Check out local farmers' markets, such as the Tower Grove Farmers’ Market, where many local chefs showcase their creations, often offering samples of their food and promoting their restaurants.

Finding the right restaurant in St. Louis can be a delightful adventure filled with culinary exploration. With these tips, you’ll be well-equipped to sample the best this city has to offer. Whether you’re in the mood for savory barbecue, authentic Italian, or innovative vegan cuisine, St. Louis has something to satisfy every palate.
