How Do I Get More Customers in St. Louis?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

St. Louis, Missouri, known for its iconic Gateway Arch and rich cultural heritage, is a vibrant city with a thriving small business community. With a diverse population and a strong sense of local pride, there are numerous opportunities to attract more customers to your local service business here, particularly in sectors such as food and beverage, healthcare, and creative services.

Build a Professional Website

Your website is your online storefront, and it’s crucial for getting customers. Here's what actually works:

  • Make it mobile-friendly – A lot of your potential customers will be searching on their phones, especially during their commutes or while exploring neighborhoods like Soulard or The Loop.
  • Optimize for SEO – This means using the right keywords that your local customers are searching for, such as 'best coffee shop in St. Louis' or 'affordable plumbing services in North City'.
  • Include clear calls to action – Tell visitors exactly what you want them to do next, whether it’s scheduling a consultation or signing up for a newsletter.
  • Showcase customer testimonials – Social proof can be a game changer in decision-making, particularly in a community that values word-of-mouth recommendations.
  • Keep it updated – Regularly post new content or offers to keep visitors engaged, like promotions tied to local events such as the St. Louis Art Fair or the St. Louis Blues hockey season.

Leverage Google My Business

Google My Business is a must for any local business. The truth is, it's one of the easiest ways to get found online:

  • Claim and verify your listing – This puts your business on the map literally and figuratively, especially in popular neighborhoods like Clayton or the Central West End.
  • Fill out all the details – Address, hours, services, and photos are crucial. Include images of your storefront, products, or services in a local context.
  • Encourage reviews – Positive reviews can distinguish you from competitors and improve your ranking, particularly in a city where residents often seek local options first.
  • Post updates and offers – Keep your audience informed about what’s going on, such as special deals during St. Louis’ annual Mardi Gras festivities.

Harness the Power of Reviews

Reviews can make or break your business. Here’s how to maximize them:

  • Ask satisfied customers for reviews – A simple follow-up email can do wonders. Consider incentivizing reviews with discounts or small gifts.
  • Respond to all reviews – This shows you're engaged and care about customer feedback. Acknowledge specific details from customer experiences to personalize your responses.
  • Use reviews in marketing – Highlight positive testimonials on your website and social media, especially during local events like the Great Forest Park Balloon Race.

Network Locally

Networking is key in St. Louis. I've seen this work time and time again:

  • Join local business groups – Organizations like the St. Louis Regional Chamber can connect you with opportunities, including workshops and networking events.
  • Attend community events – Farmers markets, fairs, and trade shows, such as the St. Louis Art Fair, are great for visibility and community engagement.
  • Collaborate with other local businesses – Partnering for promotions can expand your reach. Consider teaming up with businesses in the historic Soulard area for combined marketing efforts.

Utilize Social Media Effectively

Social media is an invaluable tool if used right. Here’s what you should focus on:

  • Choose the right platforms – Facebook and Instagram are popular in St. Louis, particularly for showcasing local events and promotions.
  • Engage with your audience – Respond to comments, share local news, and connect on a personal level, especially regarding community issues or events.
  • Post consistently – Regular updates keep your business top-of-mind for customers, especially during peak seasons like summer festivals or holiday markets.
  • Use local hashtags – This can help you reach a targeted audience, such as #STLfood or #STLsmallbusiness.

Attracting customers in St. Louis is all about leveraging your local connections and making your online presence strong. Stay persistent, keep hustling, and remember that building your customer base takes time but is absolutely achievable. Embrace the local culture, participate in community events, and showcase what makes your business uniquely suited to serve the St. Louis community.
