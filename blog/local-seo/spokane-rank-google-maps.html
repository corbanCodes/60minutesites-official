How to Rank on Google Maps in Spokane

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

One aspect that many business owners often underestimate is the immense power of local search visibility. For those operating a business in Spokane, effectively ranking on Google Maps can dramatically enhance not just foot traffic but also your overall online presence. Whether your business is nestled in the bustling downtown area or located in one of Spokane's charming neighborhoods like Kendall Yards or the historic South Hill, local visibility is crucial. Let’s explore a series of actionable strategies that will help your business shine in Spokane’s vibrant marketplace.

Claim and Optimize Your Google My Business Listing

The first step to boosting your visibility on Google Maps is to claim your Google My Business (GMB) listing. This serves as the bedrock for your online presence and ranking. Here’s how to fully optimize it:

  • Accurate Business Information: Ensure your business name, address, and phone number are meticulously accurate. This is vital for both users and Google. Consider including your suite number if applicable.
  • Detailed Business Description: Write a compelling and detailed business description that includes relevant keywords. For instance, if you run a coffee shop in Spokane, mention your specialty brews, locally sourced ingredients, or the cozy atmosphere that distinguishes you from competitors.
  • Select Appropriate Categories: Carefully choose the GMB categories that best define your services. This can help Google understand what you offer. For example, if you offer catering services in addition to restaurant dining, make sure to include both categories.
  • High-Quality Images: Upload a variety of high-quality images of your business, products, or services. Pictures of your storefront, interior ambiance, and even happy customers enjoying your offerings can create a compelling visual narrative.
  • Encourage Customer Reviews: Actively encourage customers to leave reviews and make it easy for them to do so. Responding promptly to these reviews—both positive and negative—can foster a sense of community and show prospective customers that you value feedback.

Maintain Consistent NAP Information

Your NAP (Name, Address, Phone Number) must remain consistent across all online platforms to avoid confusing Google and potential customers. Here’s how to ensure consistency:

  • Check Local Directories: Regularly review local directories like Yelp, Yellow Pages, and Angie’s List to ensure your information is correct and up-to-date.
  • Update Your Website: Make sure your website’s contact page reflects the same NAP information you’ve submitted to Google. This consistency helps reinforce your credibility.
  • Monitor Platforms: Set aside time every few months to check all platforms for outdated or incorrect details. Tools like Moz Local can help streamline this process by providing an overview of your listings across various directories.

Leverage Local Keywords

Incorporating local keywords into your content is essential for helping Google understand your geographical relevance. Here are some practical tips:

  • Use Local Names: Include “Spokane” along with nearby landmarks or neighborhoods in your website content. For example, if you are a florist, mention your location near Riverfront Park or the Spokane Arena to attract local customers.
  • Create Localized Blog Posts: Write engaging blog posts about local events, festivals, or features that tie into your business. For example, if you're a bakery, consider writing about local farmers' markets and how you source your ingredients from them.
  • Integrate Keywords in GMB: Be sure to include local keywords in your GMB description and posts to increase the likelihood of appearing in local searches. For instance, if you offer home renovation services, use phrases like “Spokane home remodel” in your GMB profile.

Build Local Backlinks

Establishing backlinks from reputable local websites can significantly enhance your credibility. Here’s how to cultivate these valuable connections:

  • Local Partnerships: Partner with other local businesses for cross-promotions. For example, a local gym might collaborate with a nearby healthy cafe, promoting each other's services through links on your websites.
  • Sponsor Events: Get involved in local events or charities and ensure your business is mentioned on their websites. Not only does this build community goodwill, but it also creates valuable backlinks.
  • Guest Posting: Offer to write guest posts for local blogs or news sites. This not only positions you as an authority in your field but also provides an opportunity to include a link back to your website.

Encourage Customer Engagement

Active customer engagement signals to Google that your business is vibrant and valued. Here’s how you can enhance engagement:

  • Regular Updates: Regularly post updates and special offers on your GMB profile. This could include seasonal promotions or new product launches, helping keep your audience informed and engaged.
  • Review Responses: Make it a habit to respond to all reviews, whether positive or negative, to show that you value customer feedback and are dedicated to improving their experience.
  • Use Google Posts: Take advantage of Google Posts to share announcements, events, or promotions directly on your GMB listing. For example, if you’re hosting a local workshop or event, use this feature to spread the word and keep your community informed.

Achieving a good ranking on Google Maps in Spokane goes beyond simply filling out forms; it's about cultivating a dynamic online presence that resonates with your community. With consistent effort and the right strategies, you can significantly elevate your visibility.
