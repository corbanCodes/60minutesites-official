How Do I Get More Customers in Madison?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Madison is a vibrant city with a strong local economy, making it a fantastic place for small businesses to thrive. Known for its beautiful lakes, extensive parks, and a youthful energy driven by the University of Wisconsin-Madison, the city offers a robust customer base. If you're looking to attract more customers in this competitive market, I've got some solid strategies to share with you.

Build a Professional Website

The truth is, your website is the foundation of getting customers online. It’s your digital storefront and needs to be top-notch. Here's what actually works:

  • Ensure it's mobile-friendly; a lot of people are searching on their phones.
  • Optimize for local SEO so you show up in search results for your services in Madison, especially in neighborhoods like State-Langdon or Downtown.
  • Include clear calls to action; make it easy for visitors to contact you or book your services.
  • Showcase customer testimonials and case studies to build trust.
  • Keep your content fresh; regularly update your blog or news section with local events or news related to your business.

Leverage Google My Business

Claiming and optimizing your Google My Business profile can put you on the map—literally. I've seen this work time and time again:

  • Fill out every section completely, including hours, services, and photos that highlight your location’s charm, such as views of the State Capitol or local landmarks.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews; respond to them to show you care.
  • Use posts to share updates, special offers, or community involvement, such as participating in local events like the Art Fair on the Square.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Word-of-mouth is still one of the most powerful marketing tools. Here’s how to maximize it:

  • Ask your happy customers for referrals; they can be your best sales team.
  • Create a referral program with incentives for both the referrer and the new customer.
  • Highlight positive reviews on your website and social media to build credibility, especially during events like the Wisconsin State Fair where local support is strong.

Engage on Social Media

Social media is where community engagement happens. Here's the lowdown:

  • Choose platforms where your target audience hangs out—Facebook and Instagram are popular in Madison, particularly among the university demographic.
  • Share behind-the-scenes content to humanize your brand, perhaps showcasing local employees or your involvement in community service.
  • Run local promotions or contests to attract attention, especially during popular local events like the Great Taste of the Midwest.
  • Collaborate with local influencers to reach a wider audience, such as bloggers who focus on Madison’s food scene.

Network Like a Pro

Networking is crucial in a city like Madison, where community ties run deep. Consider these strategies:

  • Attend local business events and workshops, such as those hosted by the Greater Madison Chamber of Commerce, to meet potential customers and collaborators.
  • Join local chambers of commerce or industry associations to expand your network, including niche groups like the Madison Area Builders Association.
  • Partner with other businesses for cross-promotions; think coffee shops and bakeries working together, especially in vibrant areas like Willy Street.

Building a successful business in Madison takes hustle and smart strategies, but by leveraging online tools and engaging with the community, you can attract more customers. Stay persistent, adapt your approach, and watch your business grow!
