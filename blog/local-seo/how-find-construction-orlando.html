How to Find a Construction in Orlando

5 min read

Finding a construction project in Orlando can feel like searching for treasure in the sand. With the city’s dynamic growth and a constant influx of new developments, knowing where to look can make all the difference in getting the information you need. From residential neighborhoods to commercial hubs, there's a wealth of opportunities waiting to be uncovered. This guide will help you navigate the landscape of construction projects in Orlando, ensuring you’re always in the know.

Explore Local Resources

Start your search by diving into local resources that provide updates on construction projects. These resources are invaluable for anyone looking to get the latest information:

  • City of Orlando's official website: This site is a treasure trove for local news, featuring press releases and updates on public projects. Regularly check their Construction Projects page for detailed information on ongoing and future construction.
  • Local newspapers like the Orlando Sentinel: This publication often covers significant construction announcements and can provide insights into the latest residential and commercial projects. Don't forget to check their business section for the latest updates.
  • Community boards or neighborhood associations: These local groups often share updates on developments that directly affect your area. Get involved with associations in neighborhoods like College Park or Baldwin Park to stay informed about community projects.

Utilize Online Tools

There are several online platforms that can help you find construction information in Orlando. These digital tools are designed to simplify your search:

  • Websites such as BuildZoom or HomeAdvisor: These platforms provide detailed information about ongoing and upcoming projects, including permits and contractor ratings. You can search by zip code or project type, making it easy to find specific developments that interest you.
  • Social media groups: Platforms like Facebook host numerous groups where locals discuss construction updates. Join groups like "Orlando Construction News" to engage with community members who share tips and news about local projects.
  • Real estate platforms: Websites like Zillow and Realtor.com often list new developments in the area, including residential and commercial properties. Use their filters to find newly built homes or upcoming commercial spaces that might interest you.

Network with Local Professionals

Connecting with professionals in the construction industry can yield valuable insights and information:

  • Attend local networking events or trade shows: Events like the Orlando Home & Garden Show bring together industry professionals and provide opportunities to learn about new projects while networking with builders and contractors.
  • Reach out to architects, contractors, or real estate agents: These professionals often have inside information about upcoming projects. Schedule a coffee chat with a local architect to discover which neighborhoods are experiencing growth.
  • Join local business groups or chambers of commerce: Organizations like the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce often share leads on projects and can connect you with key players in the construction industry.

Keep an Eye on Popular Neighborhoods

Some areas in Orlando are buzzing with construction activity and are worth keeping on your radar:

  • Lake Nona: Known for its rapid development, this area is home to the Lake Nona Medical City, where numerous healthcare projects are underway. Additionally, the community is expanding with new residential neighborhoods, parks, and commercial spaces.
  • Downtown Orlando: Urban renewal is a constant effort in this vibrant area. Projects like the Creative Village aim to transform the skyline with mixed-use developments that include residential, retail, and office spaces.
  • Windermer: This charming neighborhood is seeing growth in both residential and commercial sectors, with new developments like the Windermer Resort enhancing the community's appeal.

Finding construction opportunities in Orlando doesn't have to be daunting. By leveraging local resources, online tools, and networking, you can stay informed about all the exciting developments.
