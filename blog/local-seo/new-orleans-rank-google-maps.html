How to Rank on Google Maps in New Orleans

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Imagine strolling through the vibrant streets of the French Quarter and stumbling upon your business at the top of Google Maps—sounds great, right? Getting your small business to rank on Google Maps in New Orleans can feel like a treasure hunt, but with the right tools and strategies, you can make it happen. Whether you're nestled in the eclectic Marigny or the bustling Warehouse District, proper optimization can truly enhance your visibility and attract foot traffic.

Claim Your Google Business Profile

To start ranking on Google Maps, you first need to claim your Google Business Profile. Here’s how:

  • Visit the Google Business Profile page and sign in using your Google account.
  • Search for your business name to see if it already exists in the Google ecosystem.
  • If it does, follow the prompts to claim it; if not, create a new listing by clicking on 'Add your business.' Make sure to choose the correct business category to help Google understand what you do.

By claiming your profile, you gain control over the information displayed about your business, which is crucial for potential customers searching for services in New Orleans.

Optimize Your Profile Information

Once you have claimed your profile, it’s time to optimize it. Make sure to include:

  • Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP): These should be exactly as they appear on your website. Consistency is key for local SEO.
  • A brief but engaging business description: This should highlight what makes your business unique, especially in the context of New Orleans' rich culture. For example, if you run a Cajun restaurant, mention your authentic recipes or locally sourced ingredients.
  • High-quality photos: Showcase your location and offerings with vibrant images that capture the essence of your business. Consider adding pictures of your storefront, interior, and popular menu items. A stunning photo of your beignets could entice visitors from all around.

Optimizing your profile not only helps with ranking but also makes a great first impression on potential customers.

Encourage Customer Reviews

Reviews play a crucial role in your ranking. Here’s how to gather them:

  • Ask satisfied customers: After providing great service, politely ask your customers to leave reviews on your Google Business Profile. Consider sending follow-up emails or messages thanking them for their visit and including a link to your review page.
  • Respond to reviews: Engage with your customers by responding to both positive and negative reviews. Thank them for their feedback and address any concerns they may have raised. This shows that you care about their experience.
  • Consider offering incentives: While you can't pay for reviews, you can encourage them by offering incentives like discounts on future purchases for customers who take the time to write a review. Just make sure to clarify that the review should be honest.

Building a robust review profile not only enhances your ranking but also builds trust with potential customers looking for their next great meal or service in New Orleans.

Use Local Keywords Effectively

Incorporating local keywords is key for ranking. To do this:

  • Include phrases: Use local phrases that resonate with New Orleans residents and visitors alike, such as "best coffee shop in the Garden District" or "authentic gumbo in Treme." These keywords should be woven into your business description and posts.
  • Focus on nearby attractions: Mention landmarks and events that tourists or locals may be searching for, like the Audubon Zoo or Mardi Gras festivities. This can help you capture searches from people looking to explore the area.
  • Update your keywords: Regularly check for trending keywords in your industry or neighborhood. Use tools like Google Trends or Ubersuggest to see what potential customers are searching for.

Using local keywords effectively can help you connect with your community and draw in clients who are looking for exactly what you offer.

Utilize Local Citations

Building local citations helps boost your visibility. Consider these steps:

  • List your business: Get your business listed on local directories such as Yelp, TripAdvisor, and Yellow Pages. Each listing should include your NAP details, website, and a brief description.
  • Ensure NAP consistency: Check that your business name, address, and phone number are consistent across all platforms. Inconsistencies can confuse Google and hurt your ranking.
  • Engage with local blogs and websites: Reach out to local bloggers or tourism websites that might feature your business. Offering them a complimentary meal or service in exchange for a review can be a great way to build relationships and gain exposure.

Local citations not only help improve your ranking but also lend credibility to your business in the eyes of potential customers.

With these strategies, you'll be well on your way to ranking higher on Google Maps in New Orleans, making your business more visible to locals and tourists alike.
