How Do I Get More Customers in Pasadena?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

If you're a small business owner in Pasadena, California, you're in a vibrant market with unique opportunities to attract more customers. The truth is, with the right strategies, you can stand out among the competition and thrive in this community-focused environment. Pasadena, known for its rich history, diverse culture, and iconic landmarks such as the Rose Bowl and the Norton Simon Museum, offers a unique backdrop for businesses looking to connect with a dynamic local population.

Build a Professional Website

A professional website is the foundation for getting customers online. It's often the first impression potential clients will have of your business. Here's what actually works:

  • Make sure your website is mobile-friendly; many users will be searching from their phones, especially during events like the Rose Parade.
  • Include clear calls to action (CTAs) that guide visitors on what to do next, such as visiting your store in Old Town Pasadena.
  • Optimize for local SEO by including Pasadena in your keywords to help you show up in search results, particularly for local searches like 'best coffee in Pasadena.'
  • Showcase your services, and don't forget to highlight any unique selling propositions (USPs) that set you apart from local competitors, such as locally sourced products or specialized services.

Leverage Google My Business

Google My Business is a must-have for local visibility. I've seen this work time and time again:

  • Claim and verify your listing to enhance your visibility in local searches, especially important during peak times like the Pasadena Chalk Festival.
  • Keep your business information up-to-date (hours, location, services) to avoid confusing potential customers.
  • Post regular updates, promotions, or news to keep your audience engaged, particularly during community events or festivals.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews, as these can greatly influence new customers and help your business stand out in the competitive Pasadena market.

Utilize Reviews and Referrals

Word-of-mouth is powerful, especially in a close-knit community like Pasadena. The truth is:

  • Actively ask your happy customers for reviews on platforms like Yelp and Google, which are frequently used by locals.
  • Create a referral program that rewards existing customers for bringing in new ones, possibly offering discounts for referrals that lead to new business.
  • Share testimonials on your website and social media to build credibility, showcasing how your business contributes to the Pasadena community.

Engage on Social Media

Social media isn't just for big brands; it can be a game changer for small businesses too. Here’s how:

  • Choose the platforms where your target audience hangs out; Instagram and Facebook are popular in Pasadena, especially for local events.
  • Share engaging content that showcases your products/services and the personality of your business, perhaps highlighting your participation in the Pasadena ArtNight.
  • Use local hashtags and engage with other Pasadena businesses to expand your reach, fostering a sense of community.
  • Consider running targeted ads to reach specific demographics in your area, especially during local events that draw crowds.

Network and Attend Local Events

Building relationships in your community can lead to new customers. Here’s what works:

  • Attend local networking events, chamber of commerce meetings, and trade shows to connect with other business owners and potential clients, particularly during events like the Pasadena Open Market.
  • Partner with local organizations or charities for events—this increases your visibility and demonstrates community support, especially in the context of Pasadena's many cultural festivals.
  • Host your own events or workshops to showcase your expertise while bringing people into your space, tapping into the local enthusiasm for learning and community engagement.

Remember, growing your customer base takes time and persistence, but with these strategies, you can create a solid foundation for your business in Pasadena. Keep hustling, stay connected with your community, and watch your business flourish in this vibrant and supportive environment.
