How Do I Get More Customers in Bremerton?

5 min read

Here's the thing: attracting customers in Bremerton isn't just about having a great product or service; it's about weaving yourself into the fabric of the community. With its rich maritime history, beautiful waterfront views, and vibrant local culture, there are countless opportunities to connect with potential customers both online and offline. Whether you’re nestled in the downtown area or located near the scenic Bremerton Marina, understanding how to engage with the unique community here will set you apart from the competition.

Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Building your online presence is crucial for reaching customers in Bremerton. Here are some effective strategies that can help you stand out:

  • Create a user-friendly website: Ensure your website is easy to navigate, visually appealing, and mobile-responsive. Include essential information such as your location, hours of operation, and contact details. Consider a blog section where you share local stories or tips, which can enhance your SEO.
  • Utilize social media: Platforms like Facebook and Instagram are powerful tools for engaging with your audience. Post regularly about local events, share behind-the-scenes looks at your business, and encourage user-generated content by asking customers to tag you in their photos.
  • Optimize for local search: Use Bremerton-specific keywords throughout your website, including terms like "Bremerton coffee shop" or "Bremerton art supplies." Make sure your Google My Business profile is complete and accurate, as it’s key for local SEO.
  • Encourage reviews: Happy customers are your best advocates. After a purchase, follow up with a thank-you email and a gentle reminder to leave a review on platforms like Google and Yelp. You might even consider offering a small discount or incentive for their next visit in exchange for their feedback.
  • Run targeted online ads: Use platforms like Facebook Ads to create campaigns targeting specific demographics in Bremerton. You can focus on age groups, interests, and even local events to reach potential customers more effectively.

Offline Strategies to Connect with the Community

While online strategies are essential, don’t overlook the power of face-to-face interactions. Here are some valuable offline approaches to help you connect with the local community:

  • Participate in local events: Engage in community gatherings like the Bremerton Summer Brewfest or the Kitsap County Fair. Set up a booth to showcase your products or services, and consider offering samples or demonstrations to draw in crowds.
  • Host workshops or classes: Share your expertise by hosting workshops that cater to local interests. For instance, if you're a bakery, consider a cupcake decorating class. This not only showcases your skills but also builds a loyal customer base.
  • Collaborate with local businesses: Team up with fellow businesses for cross-promotions. For example, a local coffee shop could partner with a nearby bookstore for a "Coffee and Book Night," where both businesses benefit from increased foot traffic.
  • Join the Bremerton Chamber of Commerce: This organization is a great way to network with other local business owners, gain access to resources, and stay informed about community events that could benefit your business.
  • Utilize local advertising: Create eye-catching flyers or posters and distribute them in community centers, local gyms, and popular spots like the Harborside Fountain Park. Consider sponsoring local sports teams or events to boost your visibility.

Embracing the Bremerton Business Community

The Bremerton business community is rich with potential allies and resources. Engaging with fellow business owners can provide you with support and collaboration opportunities. Here’s how to immerse yourself:

  • Attend local business meetups: Participate in networking events hosted by organizations like the Bremerton Downtown Association. This is a perfect opportunity to meet fellow entrepreneurs, share ideas, and forge partnerships.
  • Support local initiatives: Get involved with community projects, such as cleanup days or charity fundraisers. This not only builds goodwill but also showcases your commitment to the local area.
  • Share resources: Consider creating a local business group where you can share experiences, tips, and even resources like marketing tools or suppliers. Such collaboration can lead to joint promotions or events that benefit all parties.
  • Engage in community service: Volunteer your time or services for community projects. Whether it’s sponsoring a youth sports team or organizing a charity drive, these actions will enhance your visibility and foster a positive image in the community.

Bremerton is filled with opportunities for small businesses to thrive, both online and offline. By leveraging these strategies and actively connecting with your community, you can successfully attract more customers.
