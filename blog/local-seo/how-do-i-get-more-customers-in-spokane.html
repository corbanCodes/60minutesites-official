How Do I Get More Customers in Spokane?

9 min read

Spokane is a vibrant city located in eastern Washington, known for its stunning natural beauty and a rapidly growing local economy. With a rich history tied to the railroad and mining, Spokane has transformed into a hub for small businesses, making it an ideal location for entrepreneurs looking to attract more customers. The city's unique blend of urban amenities and outdoor recreation opportunities, combined with a strong sense of community, provides a supportive environment for businesses to thrive.

Building Your Foundation with a Professional Website

The truth is, a professional website is the cornerstone of getting customers online. It's where potential clients will first learn about your services. Make sure it includes: - Clear and concise information about your offerings, including local elements, such as Spokane's unique attractions or services that cater to residents. - Easy navigation and fast loading times to keep users engaged. - Contact information that’s easy to find, ideally including a local phone number with the 509 area code. - Strong calls to action that guide visitors on what to do next, such as signing up for a newsletter about Spokane events. - Mobile responsiveness, as many users will be on their phones, especially when exploring local businesses while on the go.

Utilizing Google to Your Advantage

Google is often the first place people turn to when searching for services. Here’s how to leverage it: - Claim your Google My Business listing and keep it updated with accurate information about your Spokane location. - Add high-quality images of your work or storefront, showcasing the beauty of Spokane, such as the Spokane River or Riverfront Park. - Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews, highlighting local landmarks or events they enjoyed. - Use local keywords in your content, such as 'Spokane dining' or 'Spokane outdoor activities,' to help you rank better in search results. - Post updates and promotions to keep your audience engaged, especially during local events like Bloomsday or the Spokane County Interstate Fair.

Harnessing the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Reviews can make or break a small business. Here’s what actually works: - Ask for reviews from happy customers in person or through follow-up emails, perhaps after a local event they attended. - Respond to all reviews, positive or negative, to show you value feedback and are engaged with the Spokane community. - Create a referral program to incentivize your current customers to spread the word about your business, possibly rewarding them with discounts for referring friends. - Feature testimonials prominently on your website and social media, especially those that mention local aspects of your service or product.

Engaging on Social Media and Networking

Social media is a powerful tool to connect with the local community. Consider these strategies: - Share behind-the-scenes content to build a personal connection, highlighting your involvement in Spokane's local culture. - Run local promotions or contests to increase engagement, perhaps during popular Spokane events like the Spokane International Film Festival. - Join local Facebook groups or create an Instagram account that highlights your business and its connection to Spokane's vibrant community. - Attend community events, such as the Spokane Farmers' Market or Art on the Avenue, and network with other local businesses to build relationships and get referrals.

Building your customer base in Spokane might take some hustle, but with the right strategies in place, you can see real growth. Stay committed, leverage your community, and take advantage of Spokane's unique charm and opportunities, and watch your business thrive!
