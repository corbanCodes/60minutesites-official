How Do I Get More Customers in Idaho Falls?

5 min read

Real talk: getting more customers in Idaho Falls isn't just about having a great product or service. It's about knowing your audience and where to find them. Whether you're nestled in the heart of downtown next to the iconic Idaho Falls Riverwalk or out by the scenic Snake River Greenbelt, there are numerous ways to attract new faces to your business. With the local community thriving, tapping into the right strategies can significantly enhance your visibility and customer base.

Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Building an online presence is crucial for small businesses. Here are some effective strategies:

  • Create a user-friendly website: Use platforms like 60 Minute Sites to design a website that reflects your brand and is easy to navigate. Ensure it is mobile-responsive, as many customers will access your site via smartphones. Include clear calls to action, such as 'Contact Us' or 'Shop Now.'
  • Optimize your website for local SEO: Use keywords specific to Idaho Falls in your website content, such as 'best coffee in Idaho Falls' or 'Idaho Falls boutique.' Ensure your business is listed on Google My Business, complete with your address, hours, and customer reviews.
  • Leverage social media: Engage with your community on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. Share local events, highlight customer stories, and post eye-catching visuals of your products. Consider running targeted ads to reach specific demographics in Idaho Falls.
  • Offer promotions or discounts: Use email newsletters to keep customers informed about special offers, loyalty programs, or seasonal sales. For example, send out a monthly newsletter that includes a discount for first-time visitors to your store.
  • Encourage online reviews: Request satisfied customers to leave reviews on Google and Yelp, as positive feedback builds credibility. You might even consider incentivizing reviews by offering a small discount on their next purchase.

Offline Strategies to Connect with Local Customers

Don’t underestimate the power of face-to-face connections. Here are some offline strategies:

  • Participate in local events: Engage with the community by setting up a booth at farmers markets or local festivals like the Idaho Falls Art & Soup event, where you can showcase your offerings and meet potential customers in person.
  • Network with other small business owners: Join local meetups or business networking groups to share referrals and collaborate on promotions. Establishing relationships with fellow entrepreneurs can lead to mutually beneficial opportunities.
  • Host workshops or classes: Organize events in your area of expertise that can draw in new customers. For example, if you run a craft store, consider a DIY workshop that encourages attendees to create their own projects using your materials.
  • Utilize local bulletin boards: Advertise your services on community bulletin boards found in local coffee shops, libraries, and grocery stores. This can be a great way to reach residents who prefer local shopping options.
  • Engage with local schools or charity organizations: Consider sponsorship or partnership opportunities with schools, such as providing discounts for students or donating to local charity events, which can increase your visibility and goodwill in the community.

Understanding the Idaho Falls Business Community

The Idaho Falls business community is a vibrant tapestry of entrepreneurs and innovators. It's not just about competition; it’s about collaboration and support. Here are some key aspects:

  • Join the Idaho Falls Chamber of Commerce: This organization offers a wealth of resources, networking opportunities, and support for local business owners. Being a member can give you access to various events and workshops that can enhance your business acumen.
  • Attend networking events: Participate in local networking events, such as Business After Hours, where you can meet other professionals, share your experiences, and learn from their journeys.
  • Participate in local initiatives: Join community efforts that promote shopping at small businesses, like Small Business Saturday. Collaborating with other businesses for joint promotions can help draw in larger crowds.
  • Support fellow entrepreneurs: Share your colleagues' events and promotions on your social media platforms. Building a supportive network can create a positive ripple effect throughout the community.
  • Explore local partnerships: Teaming up with other businesses for co-marketing campaigns can help you reach new audiences. For instance, a bakery could partner with a local coffee shop to promote a coffee and pastry deal.

Remember, building a customer base takes time and effort, but with the right mix of online and offline strategies tailored to the unique Idaho Falls community, you can create lasting connections that will benefit your business for years to come.
