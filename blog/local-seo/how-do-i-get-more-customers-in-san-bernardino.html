How Do I Get More Customers in San Bernardino?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Getting customers in San Bernardino can feel like a tough climb, but there are tried-and-true strategies that can help your small business stand out. Whether you're a new startup or an established service provider, tapping into both online and offline methods can bring in more customers than you think. San Bernardino, located in the Inland Empire region of Southern California, is known for its diverse population and rich history, making it a unique market for businesses.

Build a Professional Website

The truth is, your website is the foundation of getting customers online. It’s your digital storefront, and it needs to impress. Here's what actually works:

  • Ensure your website is mobile-friendly; most customers will be browsing on their phones, especially with the high percentage of mobile users in urban areas like San Bernardino.
  • Include clear contact information and a simple way to book your services, such as online scheduling tools.
  • Use high-quality images and clear descriptions of your services, highlighting any local aspects that resonate with residents.
  • Optimize for local SEO; include keywords like 'San Bernardino services', 'Inland Empire', and specific neighborhoods to help locals find you.
  • Have a blog or resource section that provides value and showcases your expertise, perhaps focusing on local events or tips relevant to San Bernardino.

Leverage Google My Business

Claiming and optimizing your Google My Business listing is a game changer. I've seen this work time and time again:

  • Keep your information up-to-date; hours, services, and contact details should be accurate, especially considering the diverse demographics in San Bernardino.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews; these can boost your visibility dramatically and help build trust in a community that values recommendations.
  • Post updates and special offers directly to your listing to engage potential customers, particularly during local events like the San Bernardino County Fair.
  • Use local keywords in your business description to improve search rankings, ensuring you connect with residents searching for local services.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Reviews can make or break your business. Here’s how to make them work for you:

  • Ask for reviews after completing a job; make it part of your routine to follow up with customers.
  • Display positive reviews on your website and social media to build credibility in a city where word-of-mouth is crucial.
  • Create a referral program that rewards customers for bringing in new business, capitalizing on the close-knit community in San Bernardino.
  • Attend local networking events or join business groups to meet other entrepreneurs and share referrals, with organizations like the San Bernardino Chamber of Commerce offering valuable connections.

Engage on Social Media

Social media isn't just for big brands; it's crucial for local businesses too. Here’s what you should focus on:

  • Choose platforms where your audience hangs out (Facebook and Instagram are popular in San Bernardino, especially among younger demographics).
  • Post regularly; share behind-the-scenes content, customer testimonials, and local stories that resonate with the community.
  • Run local ads targeting specific demographics in San Bernardino to reach new customers, taking advantage of local events or holidays.
  • Engage with your followers by responding to comments and messages promptly, fostering a sense of community and customer loyalty.

Get Involved in Your Community

Building relationships in the community can lead to loyal customers. Here’s how to immerse yourself:

  • Sponsor or participate in local events, fairs, or markets, such as the San Bernardino International Film Festival or local farmer's markets, to increase visibility.
  • Partner with other local businesses for cross-promotions, leveraging the strong community spirit in San Bernardino.
  • Offer workshops or free demos of your services to showcase your expertise, perhaps at local community centers.
  • Join local business associations to connect with other entrepreneurs and participate in initiatives aimed at boosting local economic growth.
