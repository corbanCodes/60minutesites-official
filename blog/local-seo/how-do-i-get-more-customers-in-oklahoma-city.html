How Do I Get More Customers in Oklahoma City?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Getting customers in Oklahoma City can be a game-changer for your small business. With a vibrant local economy and a supportive community, there are plenty of opportunities to attract new customers and grow your brand. Oklahoma City, the capital of Oklahoma, is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and a booming economy that provides a fertile ground for small businesses to flourish.

Building Your Online Presence

A professional website is your foundation for getting customers online. Here's what actually works:

  • Optimize your website for local SEO to appear in search results when people look for services in Oklahoma City. Utilize keywords that reflect local landmarks and neighborhoods, such as ‘Bricktown dining’ or ‘Midtown retail.’
  • Use clear and compelling calls to action to guide visitors on what to do next, such as ‘Visit us in the Plaza District’ or ‘Order online for pickup at our Northwest OKC location.’
  • Make sure your site is mobile-friendly since many customers search on their phones, especially while on the go in areas like the bustling Automobile Alley.
  • Regularly update your content with blogs or resources that showcase your expertise and connect with local events, such as sharing insights about the annual Festival of the Arts.

Harnessing the Power of Google

Google is where most customers start their search, so you need to be visible. The truth is, claiming your Google My Business listing can make a huge difference:

  • Ensure your business info is accurate and up to date, including hours of operation and location in relation to popular spots like the Myriad Botanical Gardens.
  • Add high-quality images of your products or services, ideally featuring local attractions or recent local events.
  • Encourage customers to leave reviews, as positive feedback boosts your visibility and credibility. In Oklahoma City, people often trust recommendations from local sources.
  • Use posts on your Google listing to share updates, promotions, or events, particularly during key local happenings such as the Oklahoma City Memorial Marathon.

Maximizing Reviews and Referrals

Reviews can make or break a small business. Here’s how to leverage them:

  • After every job, ask satisfied customers to leave a review on Google or Yelp. You might mention how much you appreciate the support of the Oklahoma City community.
  • Create a referral program that rewards customers for bringing in new clients, such as offering discounts for referrals that lead to sales, particularly during local events like First Friday Gallery Walks.
  • Share reviews on your social media and website to build trust with potential customers, highlighting stories from locals in your marketing.
  • Respond to all reviews, both positive and negative, to show that you value feedback and are engaged with your customer base.

Engaging on Social Media

Social media is a powerful tool for connecting with your local audience. Here’s what works:

  • Choose platforms where your target audience hangs out—Facebook and Instagram are popular in Oklahoma City. Consider using TikTok to engage with a younger demographic.
  • Post engaging content that highlights your services, community involvement, or even behind-the-scenes looks at your business, like how you prepare for the local Oklahoma State Fair.
  • Use local hashtags to reach a wider audience in Oklahoma City, such as #OKCEvents or #SupportLocalOKC.
  • Run targeted ads to reach specific demographics in your area, focusing on neighborhoods like Nichols Hills or Edmond.

Networking and Community Involvement

The hustle of running a local business is tough, but networking can open doors:

  • Attend local business events or join organizations like the Greater Oklahoma City Chamber to meet other entrepreneurs. Events such as the OKC Small Business Expo are great for making connections.
  • Collaborate with other small businesses for cross-promotions or joint events, especially in areas like Plaza District, known for its vibrant arts scene.
  • Participate in community events or sponsor local activities to raise awareness of your brand. Annual events like the OKC Arts Festival provide excellent exposure.
  • Build relationships with local influencers who can promote your business to their followers, focusing on those who highlight local culture and attractions.

Remember, building a customer base in Oklahoma City takes time and effort, but with the right strategies, you can grow your business and thrive in this supportive community. Stay focused, keep hustling, and don’t hesitate to reach out for help when you need it! Consider leveraging local resources such as the Oklahoma Small Business Development Center for additional guidance.
