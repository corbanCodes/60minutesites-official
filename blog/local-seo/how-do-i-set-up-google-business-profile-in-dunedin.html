Creating Your Google Business Profile

Setting up your Google Business Profile can seem daunting, but it’s actually quite straightforward. Follow these steps to get started:

  • Visit the Google Business Profile website. Click on 'Start now.' This is where your journey begins towards establishing an online presence.
  • Enter your business name and address. Make sure this information is accurate; it’s vital for local searches. If your business is located near iconic landmarks like the Dunedin Railway Station or the Octagon, be sure to mention this in your profile to attract more visitors.
  • Select the appropriate business category. Choose the category that best describes your business. For example, if you run a café, select 'Café' or 'Coffee Shop' to ensure you're found by people searching for those specific services.
  • Add your business phone number and website URL. If you have a website, make sure to include it. This is your chance to direct potential customers to more detailed information about your services.
  • Verify your business. This usually involves receiving a postcard with a verification code. Ensure your business address is correct to avoid delays.
  • Complete your profile. After verification, add high-quality photos of your products or services, your business hours, and a compelling description that highlights what makes your business unique. Consider using local buzzwords or references to connect with the Dunedin community.
  • Encourage clients to leave reviews. This boosts your profile’s visibility and credibility. Engage with your customers and ask them to share their experiences. Positive reviews not only enhance your reputation but also improve your ranking in local search results.

How Locals in Dunedin Search for Businesses

Understanding how residents of Dunedin search for businesses can give you an edge. Here are some common patterns:

  • Direct Google Searches. Many locals rely on Google searches directly, typing in keywords like 'best coffee in Dunedin' or 'pet grooming near me.' Researching popular keywords related to your business can help optimize your profile.
  • Google Maps Usage. Locals often use Google Maps to find businesses. Ensure your location is marked accurately. If you're near popular spots like the Otago Museum or the Dunedin Botanic Garden, make sure to highlight this in your listings.
  • Word-of-Mouth Recommendations. Residents look for recommendations from friends and family, so fostering a strong community presence can lead them to check online reviews as well. Attend local events or sponsor community activities to spread the word about your business.
  • Social Media Integration. Social media platforms are frequently used to recommend local spots. Link your profiles to your Google Business Profile. Creating engaging content on platforms like Facebook and Instagram can draw more traffic to your profile.
  • Community Engagement. Participating in community events and local gatherings can significantly boost your visibility. Consider sponsoring local sports teams or hosting workshops to connect with your audience.
