Why a Google Business Profile Matters

A Google Business Profile is essential for attracting local customers and enhancing your online visibility. Here’s what it can do for you:

  • Showcase Your Business Hours and Location: Clearly displaying your hours helps potential customers plan their visits, especially if you're located near popular spots like the Oklahoma Aquarium or the Jenks Historic District.
  • Enable Customer Reviews and Ratings: Positive reviews can significantly influence new customers. Encourage happy patrons to leave feedback about their experiences, which can help you build a trustworthy reputation.
  • Provide Essential Contact Information: Ensure that your phone number, website, and even social media links are easily accessible, allowing customers to reach out with questions or to place orders.
  • Include Photos to Attract Attention: High-quality images of your products or services can captivate potential customers. Consider showcasing images of your storefront, your team, or even special events you host.
  • Offer Insights into How Customers Find Your Business: Google provides analytics on how customers discovered your business, which can inform your marketing strategies and help you understand customer behavior.

Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Creating a Google Business Profile might seem daunting, but it's quite straightforward. Follow these steps to establish your presence:

  • Sign in to Google My Business: Use your Google account to start. If you don’t have one, creating an account is quick and easy.
  • Enter Your Business Name: Make sure it matches exactly with what you use offline. Consistency is key for brand recognition.
  • Add Your Location: If you serve customers at your location, include your address. If you're a service area business, specify the areas you cover to attract nearby clients.
  • Choose Your Business Category: Select a category that best describes your business. This helps Google direct relevant searches to your profile. Be specific; for example, if you run a bakery, choose "Bakery" instead of just "Food."
  • Fill in Contact Details: Include your phone number and website. Make sure these details are up to date so customers can easily reach you.
  • Verify Your Business: Google will send a postcard to your address to confirm. This step is crucial for your profile to be fully functional, so keep an eye out for that postcard.
  • Optimize Your Profile: Add photos, update business hours, and encourage reviews. Regular updates and engaging content can keep your profile fresh and relevant.

  • Community Recommendations: Jenks residents rely on word-of-mouth and online reviews. Engage with your customers and encourage them to share their experiences, which can help your business grow organically.
  • Local Events: Businesses that participate in community events often get more visibility. Consider partnering with local organizations or sponsoring events, which can enhance your profile and attract more customers.
  • Convenience: Searchers value hours of operation and easy access information. Make sure your hours are accurate, especially during holidays or special occasions, as this can impact foot traffic.
