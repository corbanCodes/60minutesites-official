How Do I Get More Customers in Bend?

5 min read

Attracting customers in Bend, Oregon, requires a thoughtful blend of creativity, community connection, and understanding the unique local culture. With its breathtaking landscapes, from the majestic peaks of the Cascade Mountains to the serene banks of the Deschutes River, Bend is not just a beautiful backdrop but a vibrant hub for small businesses. This rich tapestry of outdoor adventures, craft breweries, and local artisans creates an ideal environment for entrepreneurs looking to thrive both online and offline.

Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Building a strong online presence is essential for small businesses in Bend. Here are some actionable strategies to help you get started:

  • Create a user-friendly website with 60 Minute Sites: A well-designed website can serve as your online storefront. Ensure it reflects your brand’s personality and showcases your products and services effectively. Use high-quality images, clear descriptions, and easy navigation to enhance user experience.
  • Utilize social media platforms: Engage with the local community through platforms like Instagram and Facebook. Share stories about your business, customer testimonials, and behind-the-scenes looks at your operations. For example, if you own a coffee shop, consider posting videos of your baristas crafting specialty drinks or hosting community events.
  • Implement local SEO practices: Optimize your website for local searches by using keywords relevant to Bend, such as "best coffee in Bend" or "outdoor gear shop Bend." This includes updating your Google My Business listing, ensuring your business name, address, and phone number are consistent across all online platforms, and gathering positive reviews.
  • Encourage customer reviews: Actively seek feedback from your customers and encourage them to leave reviews on platforms like Yelp and Google. This builds credibility and can significantly influence potential customers. Consider following up with a thank you note or offering a small incentive for taking the time to share their experience.
  • Run targeted online ads: Use Facebook and Instagram ads to reach potential customers in the Bend area. Tailor your ads based on interests and demographics to ensure they resonate with local consumers. Highlight special promotions or events to draw attention.

Offline Strategies to Build Customer Relationships

Face-to-face interactions can make a significant difference in attracting customers. Here are some effective approaches:

  • Participate in local events: Engage with your community by setting up a booth at farmers markets, art fairs, or local festivals. This not only allows you to showcase your products but also helps you connect personally with potential customers. For instance, if you run a handmade jewelry business, consider offering a workshop where participants can create their own pieces.
  • Offer promotions for first-time visitors: Encourage foot traffic by providing special discounts or a free item with purchase for first-time customers. This creates an incentive for newcomers to explore what your business has to offer.
  • Network with other local businesses: Forming partnerships can amplify your reach. Collaborate on events or promotions with businesses that complement yours. For example, if you own a bakery, consider teaming up with a local florist to create a special package for weddings or events.
  • Host workshops or classes: Organize events related to your business that invite the community in. This could include cooking classes, craft nights, or fitness sessions. This not only positions your business as an expert in the field but also fosters a sense of community.
  • Utilize flyers and brochures: Distribute informative and visually appealing flyers in popular local spots such as coffee shops, libraries, or community centers. Include a compelling call-to-action and your contact details to encourage prospective customers to reach out or visit.

The Bend Business Community: A Collaborative Network

Bend is home to a vibrant and collaborative business community that can be a tremendous asset for your small business. Here’s why engaging with this network is beneficial:

  • Local chambers of commerce: Joining the Bend Chamber of Commerce can provide invaluable networking opportunities. These organizations often host events where you can meet other local business owners, share ideas, and learn from one another.
  • Collaborative events: Participate in community-focused events that allow businesses to showcase their products together. For instance, the Bend Summer Festival brings together various local vendors, offering a perfect platform to gain exposure and attract new customers.
  • Promotion of local products: Bend residents take pride in supporting local businesses. Capitalize on this by emphasizing your commitment to the community and sustainability. Share your story, such as how your products are sourced or made locally.
  • Engaging with local groups: Join local business groups or associations where you can share resources, collaborate on marketing efforts, or assist each other in navigating challenges. This not only strengthens your business but also fosters a sense of camaraderie among local entrepreneurs.

Attracting more customers in Bend involves a thoughtful combination of online and offline strategies, as well as tapping into the supportive local business community. By implementing these tailored approaches, you can create meaningful connections with potential customers.
