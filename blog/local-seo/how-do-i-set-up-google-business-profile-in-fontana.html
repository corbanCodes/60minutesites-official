What Makes a Great Google Business Profile

Here's the secret to standing out on Google: a well-optimized profile. Trust me on this one; it’s simpler than you think. Here’s what you need to focus on:

  • Accurate business name: Make sure it's the name people know you by.
  • Complete address: This helps locals find you easily in neighborhoods like Sierra Lakes or Citrus Heights.
  • Phone number: Always include a contact number to facilitate easy communication.
  • Business hours: Update them regularly, especially during holidays and during local events such as the Fontana Days Festival.
  • Category selection: Choose the most relevant categories for your business; for example, if you’re a coffee shop, ensure you select ‘Café’ or ‘Coffee Shop’.
  • Photos: Add high-quality images of your products or services to attract attention, showcasing the unique aspects of your business, whether you’re in the historic downtown area or near the Auto Club Speedway.
  • Customer reviews: Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews and respond to them promptly to build trust within the community.
  • Posts: Share updates, promotions, and events to keep your audience engaged; consider sharing news about local partnerships or community involvement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I create a Google Business Profile?

Simply go to the Google Business Profile website, sign in with your Google account, and follow the prompts to set up your profile.

Q: Can I change my business information later?

Yes! You can update your information whenever you need to, so don’t worry if things change.

Q: How long does it take for my profile to show up on Google?

Once you verify your business, it usually appears within a few days to a week.

Q: Are there any costs associated with Google Business Profile?

No, it’s completely free to set up and maintain your profile.

Q: How can I encourage customers to leave reviews?

Ask happy customers directly or provide links to your Google Business Profile in follow-up emails, especially after they visit your business or attend an event.
