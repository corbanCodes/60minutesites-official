How Do I Get More Customers in Milwaukee?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Getting customers in Milwaukee can feel like climbing a mountain, but it's totally doable with the right strategies. The local market, known for its strong sense of community and support for small businesses, is packed with opportunities. With a diverse population and a thriving arts scene, there are plenty of ways to stand out from the competition and connect with potential customers.

Building Your Online Presence

The truth is, a professional website is the foundation of getting customers online. Here’s what actually works:

  • Invest in an easy-to-navigate website that showcases your services, particularly those that resonate with Milwaukee's culture, such as artisanal crafts, local foods, or services that cater to the city's festivals.
  • Optimize your site for local SEO to appear in search results when folks are looking for services you offer in Milwaukee, such as incorporating keywords like 'Milwaukee home services' or 'best coffee in Milwaukee.'
  • Create valuable content that answers common questions or problems your customers face, such as guides on enjoying local attractions like the Milwaukee Art Museum or summer festivals.
  • Use clear calls to action to guide visitors toward contacting you or booking your services, such as integrating local events or special promotions during popular Milwaukee seasons.

Harnessing Google My Business

I’ve seen this work time and time again—Google My Business is a game changer for local visibility. Here’s how to make the most of it:

  • Claim and verify your listing to gain control over your business information, ensuring that Milwaukee residents can easily find you.
  • Keep your hours, address, and services updated to attract local customers, especially during events like the Wisconsin State Fair or Summerfest when foot traffic increases.
  • Add photos regularly to keep your listing fresh and engaging, showcasing your storefront, team, or any unique aspects of your business that reflect Milwaukee's character.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews, as these can significantly influence new customers, especially in a city where word-of-mouth matters.

Leverage Reviews and Referrals

Social proof is powerful. Here's how to get more reviews and referrals:

  • Ask for reviews after a job well done; most people are happy to help, especially when they feel a connection to local businesses.
  • Offer incentives for customers who refer you to their friends or family, perhaps by collaborating with other Milwaukee-based businesses to provide joint discounts.
  • Highlight positive reviews on your website and social media to build trust, particularly focusing on testimonials from local clients who resonate with your Milwaukee-centric values.
  • Respond to reviews—thank people for positive feedback and address any negative comments professionally, showcasing your commitment to customer satisfaction.

Networking and Community Engagement

The hustle of running a local business can be tough, but connecting with your community can lead to valuable opportunities. Consider these strategies:

  • Attend local networking events to meet other business owners and potential clients, such as those hosted by the Greater Milwaukee Chamber of Commerce or local entrepreneurial meetups.
  • Partner with complementary businesses for cross-promotions or events, especially during popular Milwaukee seasons or festivals, to maximize exposure.
  • Join local chambers of commerce or business groups to expand your network and gain insights into the unique aspects of Milwaukee's economy.
  • Participate in community events—this not only raises your profile but also shows you're invested in Milwaukee, whether it's sponsoring a local sports team or participating in charity events.

Social Media Marketing

Don't underestimate the power of social media! Here’s how to use it effectively:

  • Choose the platforms where your target audience hangs out—Facebook and Instagram are often great for local service businesses, while TikTok can engage a younger demographic.
  • Share behind-the-scenes content, customer testimonials, and tips related to your services, incorporating local flavors or seasonal themes that resonate with Milwaukee residents.
  • Engage with your followers by responding to comments and messages promptly, and consider creating polls or questions that relate to local interests, such as favorite Milwaukee restaurants.
  • Run local ads targeting specific demographics in Milwaukee to reach potential customers, especially during peak times such as the holiday shopping season or summer festivals.
