How Do I Get More Customers in Ontario?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

If you're running a small business in Ontario, California, you know how competitive things can get. With a population of over 180,000 and a diverse economic base, Ontario presents both challenges and opportunities for entrepreneurs. With the right strategies, you can attract more customers and stand out in this vibrant community, known for its rich history and local spirit.

Build a Professional Website

The truth is, a professional website is the foundation of getting customers online. It's often the first impression potential customers will have of your business. Here's what actually works:

  • Ensure your website is mobile-friendly. Many customers will check you out on their phones.
  • Optimize for local SEO. Use keywords like 'Ontario CA' and your specific services to show up in local searches.
  • Include clear calls to action. Make it easy for visitors to contact you or book your services.
  • Showcase customer testimonials. Social proof helps build trust.
  • Keep your content fresh. Regularly update your site with blog posts or news about your business. Consider mentioning popular local attractions such as the Ontario Mills shopping center or the historic downtown area to engage visitors.

Leverage Google My Business

Google My Business is a game changer for local businesses. Here’s how to make the most of it:

  • Claim and verify your business listing to show up on Google Maps. Ontario's strategic location near the intersection of the I-10 and I-15 freeways makes this crucial for visibility.
  • Fill out every section: hours, services, photos, and more. Highlight your business's unique offerings, especially if you're near landmarks like the Ontario International Airport.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews. Positive reviews can make all the difference.
  • Post updates and offers directly on your Google profile to engage potential customers.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Word of mouth is still one of the strongest forms of advertising. Here’s how to tap into it:

  • Ask happy customers for reviews on Google, Yelp, or Facebook. Ontario residents value local recommendations, so this is key.
  • Create a referral program that rewards customers for bringing in new clients.
  • Network with other local businesses. Partner for cross-promotions that benefit both parties, especially with businesses in the thriving Ontario Town Square.

Utilize Social Media Effectively

Social media can be a powerful tool for connecting with your community. Here’s what works:

  • Choose platforms that align with your audience. Instagram is great for visual businesses, while Facebook is good for community engagement.
  • Share behind-the-scenes content to humanize your business.
  • Run local promotions or contests to increase engagement.
  • Use geo-tags to reach a local audience, particularly during events like the Ontario Arts Festival.
  • Engage with your followers; respond to comments and messages promptly.

Get Involved in Networking and Local Events

Networking is crucial in a tight-knit community like Ontario. Here’s how to get involved:

  • Join local business organizations, like the Ontario Chamber of Commerce, which hosts regular mixers and events.
  • Attend networking events, trade shows, or fairs to meet potential customers and other business owners. Ontario hosts a variety of community events throughout the year, such as the Ontario Harvest Festival.
  • Sponsor local events or sports teams to increase your visibility.
  • Collaborate with local charities to give back and gain exposure, which can resonate well in a community that values philanthropy.

Running a local business in Ontario, California is no small feat, but with the right strategies in place, you can attract more customers and foster a loyal community. Keep hustling, stay engaged with your audience, and remember that every connection counts. Leverage the unique characteristics of Ontario, such as its cultural events and community spirit, to enhance your business's visibility and reputation.
