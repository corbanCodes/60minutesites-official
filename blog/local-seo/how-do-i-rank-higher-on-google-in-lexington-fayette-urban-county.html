Optimizing Your Google My Business Listing

Here's the thing: your Google My Business (GMB) listing is like your digital storefront. To make the most of it, do the following: - Claim and verify your GMB listing - Fill out all sections completely, including business hours, address, and phone number - Use high-quality images showcasing your business, products, or services - Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive reviews - Regularly update your listing with posts about specials or events. Consider highlighting unique features like the 'Lexington Art Walk' or 'Thoroughbred Park' in your posts to attract local visitors.

Local Keyword Research

Look, I get it—finding the right keywords is crucial. Start by: - Using tools like Google Keyword Planner to identify terms people search for in Lexington-Fayette, such as 'best bourbon tours in Lexington' or 'family-friendly activities near the Kentucky Horse Park' - Including local landmarks or neighborhoods in your content (think 'near the Kentucky Horse Park' or 'in downtown Lexington') - Analyzing competitors to see what keywords they're ranking for and adjusting your strategy accordingly. Don't forget to incorporate seasonal events like the 'Lexington Bluegrass Festival' into your keyword strategy.

Creating Local Content

The good news is that creating content that resonates with your community can significantly boost your visibility. Consider: - Writing blog posts about local events or festivals, like the Lexington Bluegrass Festival, which attracts thousands of visitors each year - Sharing stories about your business's connection to the local culture, such as your involvement in the Kentucky Bourbon Festival - Featuring local partnerships or collaborations with other businesses, like collaborating with local artists from the Lexington Art League or sponsoring events at the downtown Lexington Farmers Market.

Building Local Backlinks

Backlinks are like votes of confidence for your business. Here’s how to earn them locally: - Partner with local organizations, such as the Lexington Chamber of Commerce, and get featured on their websites - Sponsor local events or charities like the annual 'Race for the Roses' and ask for a link back to your site - Connect with local bloggers or online publications that cover Lexington-Fayette, such as 'Lexington Family Magazine' or 'Ace Weekly,' and pitch them your story. This can enhance your credibility within the local community.
