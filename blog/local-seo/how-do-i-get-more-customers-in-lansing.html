How Do I Get More Customers in Lansing?

5 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Lansing, Michigan, is a city bursting with local charm and a diverse community that thrives on supporting small businesses. If you’re looking to draw in more customers, you're in luck—there are numerous strategies you can employ that bridge both online and offline realms. By combining these approaches, you'll create a compelling presence that resonates with the local populace. Let’s dive into some practical, actionable steps to get your business noticed in Lansing.

Harnessing Online Strategies

Your online presence is the gateway to attracting new customers. Here are some effective tactics to consider:

  • Create a user-friendly website: A well-designed website is essential for potential customers to find you and learn about your products or services. Ensure that your site is mobile-friendly, loads quickly, and features clear navigation. Incorporate high-quality images, customer testimonials, and a blog that offers valuable insights about your industry.
  • Utilize social media: Engage actively with your audience on platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter. Share updates about your business, behind-the-scenes content, and promotions. Don’t forget to showcase local events and partnerships, which can further enhance your connection to the community.
  • Leverage local SEO: Optimize your website with local keywords that are relevant to Lansing. Use phrases like “best coffee shop in Lansing” or “Lansing home repair services.” Listing your business on Google My Business and ensuring your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) information is consistent across platforms will help you appear in local searches.
  • Encourage online reviews: Cultivating positive reviews on platforms like Google and Yelp not only boosts your credibility but also increases your visibility. Offer incentives for customers to leave reviews, such as discounts on their next purchase or entry into a giveaway.
  • Start an email newsletter: Regularly sending out a newsletter can keep your customers informed about promotions, new products, and upcoming events. Share stories or tips that resonate with your audience, and consider featuring a ‘customer of the month’ to foster a sense of community.

Effective Offline Strategies

While online strategies are vital, the power of offline tactics should not be overlooked. Consider these approaches that can make a significant impact:

  • Network within the community: Attend local events, such as the Lansing Art Gallery & Education Center openings or the Great Lakes Folk Festival, to meet other business owners and potential customers. Joining the Lansing Regional Chamber of Commerce can also provide networking opportunities and resources.
  • Participate in local events: Setting up booths at popular venues like the Lansing City Market or the East Lansing Art Festival can showcase your products and create personal connections with the community. Consider offering samples or demonstrations to attract passersby.
  • Collaborate with other local businesses: Partnering with complementary businesses—like a local bakery teaming up with a coffee shop—can help you reach a broader audience. Joint promotions, such as “buy one, get one half-off” deals, can be mutually beneficial and attract new customers.
  • Utilize flyers and posters: Distributing well-designed flyers or posters in local hotspots such as coffee shops, community centers, or libraries can help draw attention to your offerings. Make sure to include a strong call-to-action and any special promotions.
  • Offer promotions or discounts: Attract new customers by introducing special deals for first-time visitors. Consider a ‘bring a friend’ discount or a loyalty program that rewards recurring customers, which can encourage word-of-mouth referrals.

The Lansing Business Community

Lansing boasts a vibrant business community that actively supports and uplifts local entrepreneurs. Here’s why you should get involved:

  • Local networking events: Attend gatherings specifically tailored for small business owners, such as the Lansing Small Business Fair, to exchange ideas, resources, and form collaborations.
  • Resources and support: Organizations like the Lansing Economic Area Partnership provide valuable resources for business development and growth. They offer workshops, mentoring programs, and even funding opportunities to help you thrive.
  • Community spirit: Residents of Lansing are passionate about supporting local businesses. Engaging with the community through initiatives like sponsorships for local sports teams or schools can enhance your visibility and build goodwill.
  • Shared marketing opportunities: Joining forces with other local businesses for shared marketing initiatives can broaden your reach. Consider co-hosting events or creating bundled offers that encourage customers to explore multiple local businesses.

By combining these online and offline strategies, you can significantly boost your customer base in Lansing.
