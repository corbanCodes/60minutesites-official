Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Creating a Google Business Profile is straightforward, but following critical steps will make your business shine online. Here’s how to set it up effectively:

  • Visit the Google Business Profile website and click on 'Manage now.' This will take you to the dashboard where you can begin the setup process.
  • Enter your business name and address. Be sure to use your full business name as it appears in the real world, and enter a complete address. If your business offers services in a specific area, specify the locations you serve, whether it’s the bustling downtown area or the scenic neighborhoods near the Columbia River.
  • Choose your business category. Selecting the right category is essential because it helps Google match your business with relevant searches. Think about what keywords locals might use, such as “coffee shop,” “landscaping services,” or “family-friendly restaurants.”
  • Fill in your contact information. Provide your phone number and website URL. If you’re just starting and don’t have a website, consider using 60 Minute Sites to design a professional-looking site quickly. They make it easy to get online and showcase your business.
  • Verify your business. Google will require you to verify your business to ensure that you are the legitimate owner. You may receive a postcard from Google with a verification code, or you might have the option to verify by phone or email. This step is crucial for establishing trust with potential customers.
  • Add engaging photos of your business, products, or services. High-quality images can significantly attract more customers. Capture the essence of Wenatchee in your photos—think about including images of your storefront, your team at work, or happy customers enjoying your offerings. This visual storytelling can create a connection with potential clients.
  • Keep your profile updated. Regularly update your profile with your hours of operation, special announcements, and seasonal offers. If you’re running a promotion during the Apple Blossom Festival or offering a winter discount, make sure that information is visible to catch the interest of locals.

How Locals in Wenatchee Search for Businesses

Understanding how Wenatchee residents search for businesses can help you tailor your profile effectively and attract more customers. Locals often:

  • Use Google Search and Maps. When looking for nearby services, especially for dining options or local shops, residents frequently rely on Google. Make sure your profile is complete so that when someone searches for “best coffee in Wenatchee,” your business pops up.
  • Look for reviews and ratings. Reviews are a key factor in decision-making for Wenatchee locals. Encourage satisfied customers to leave positive feedback and respond to reviews to show that you value customer input and are engaged with the community.
  • Search for seasonal activities and events. Whether it’s a summer farmers market or a holiday festival, locals often look for businesses that align with seasonal events. Make sure to highlight any special offerings that coincide with local happenings, such as participating in the Wenatchee Valley Music Festival or offering seasonal dishes.
  • Rely on social media recommendations. Many residents check platforms like Facebook or Instagram for recommendations from friends and family. By maintaining an active presence on these platforms and linking them to your Google Business Profile, you can not only enhance your visibility but also create a community around your brand.

By optimizing your Google Business Profile, you can make it easier for people in Wenatchee to discover, learn about, and choose your business over the competition.
