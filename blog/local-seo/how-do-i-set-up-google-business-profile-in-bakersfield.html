Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Here's the secret to getting started: First, you need to claim your business on Google. It’s completely free and can help you get noticed by people searching for what you offer. Here's what to do:

  • Go to the Google Business Profile page and click 'Start Now'.
  • Enter your business name and address. Make sure the address is accurate, as locals will be searching for businesses near them. Bakersfield's neighborhoods, like Seven Oaks and Downtown, have their own unique clientele, so make sure to specify your location.
  • Choose the right category for your business. This helps Google show your profile to the right audience. Categories like 'Restaurant', 'Retail', or 'Service Provider' can significantly influence visibility.
  • Add your contact information, including phone number and website. Consider including your social media handles to connect with customers on multiple platforms.
  • Upload high-quality photos. Trust me on this one, people are drawn to visuals. Show off your products, services, and even your team! Highlighting unique aspects of your business, such as a local specialty dish or a signature product, can attract attention.
  • Write a compelling business description. Make it engaging and highlight what makes you unique. If you have any local partnerships or community involvement, mention that as well.
  • Verify your business. Google will send you a postcard or call your business to confirm it's really you.

Optimizing Your Google Business Profile

Once your profile is set up, optimizing it is key to standing out. The best part is, it’s not as hard as it sounds. Here are some practical tips:

  • Keep your information up to date. If your hours change, update them immediately. For example, during the busy harvest season, you might have extended hours to accommodate more customers.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews. Respond to reviews, both positive and negative, to show you care about feedback. In a community like Bakersfield, where personal relationships matter, engaging with customers online is essential.
  • Use Google Posts to share updates, special offers, or events. This keeps your profile fresh and engaging. If you’re hosting a local event, like a pop-up market or a community fundraiser, make sure to post about it.
  • Add attributes that highlight your services, like 'women-owned', 'outdoor seating', or 'free Wi-Fi'. These details can help you attract specific audiences, like remote workers or families.
  • Use local keywords in your business description. Think about what Bakersfield locals might type into Google when searching for businesses like yours, such as 'best tacos in Bakersfield' or 'local coffee shop near Riverwalk Park'.

Q: How long does it take to set up a Google Business Profile?

Setting it up can take just a few minutes! Verification might take a few days depending on the method you choose. In busy seasons, such as during the oil harvest or the California State Fair, expect a slight delay.

Q: Can I change my business details later?

Absolutely! You can update your hours, address, and other information anytime. If you’re seasonal, like a summer ice cream shop, make sure to keep your hours updated.

Q: Is it worth it to ask customers for reviews?

Definitely! Positive reviews boost your visibility and credibility, so don't hesitate to ask satisfied customers to share their experiences. In a community like Bakersfield, where connections matter, personal endorsements can significantly influence new customer decisions.
