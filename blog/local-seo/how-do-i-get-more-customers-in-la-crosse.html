How Do I Get More Customers in La Crosse?

5 min read

Let me be direct with you: running a small business in La Crosse, Wisconsin, can be both rewarding and challenging. You want to attract more customers, but knowing how to do that can feel overwhelming. Fortunately, there are plenty of effective strategies, both online and offline, that can help you grow your customer base in this charming city nestled along the Mississippi River.

Online Strategies to Attract Customers

Building an online presence is essential for small businesses today. Here are some actionable strategies you can implement:

  • Create a user-friendly website: Your website is often the first impression potential customers will have of your business. Make sure it is visually appealing, easy to navigate, and mobile-friendly. Include high-quality images of your products or services, clear descriptions, and contact information. A well-organized site will not only attract visitors but also encourage them to stay longer and explore.
  • Utilize social media platforms: Engage with your audience on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. Share behind-the-scenes content, special promotions, and customer testimonials. For example, if you own a local café, consider posting daily specials or inviting customers to share their favorite coffee moments using a unique hashtag. This can foster a sense of community and encourage user-generated content.
  • Invest in local SEO: To help your business appear in searches relevant to La Crosse, optimize your website for local search terms. Use keywords that include your location, such as "best coffee shop in La Crosse" or "La Crosse handcrafted gifts." Register your business on Google My Business, and ensure your NAP (Name, Address, Phone number) information is consistent across all platforms.
  • Encourage customer reviews: Positive feedback boosts credibility and can significantly influence potential customers. Ask satisfied customers to leave reviews on platforms like Google and Yelp. Consider offering a small incentive, like a discount on their next purchase, to encourage them to share their experiences.
  • Consider online advertising: Use targeted ads on platforms like Google Ads or Facebook Ads to reach La Crosse residents specifically. For instance, if you're hosting a seasonal sale, create ads that highlight the event and run them a week prior to generate buzz.

Offline Strategies to Attract Customers

While an online presence is important, don't underestimate the power of traditional marketing techniques. Here are some ideas:

  • Participate in local events: Connect with potential customers face-to-face by setting up a booth at local fairs, farmers' markets, or festivals like Oktoberfest or Riverfest. These events not only allow you to showcase your products but also enable you to connect with the community in a meaningful way.
  • Network with other local businesses: Create mutually beneficial partnerships with nearby businesses. For example, a local bakery could collaborate with a coffee shop to offer a combined discount or a special treat. This not only helps you reach a wider audience but also strengthens community ties.
  • Use flyers or business cards: Distribute flyers or leave business cards in local hotspots like coffee shops, libraries, or community centers. Make sure they are visually appealing and contain a clear call to action, such as visiting your website or coming in for a special offer.
  • Host open house events: Attract foot traffic by hosting open house events or special promotions. For instance, if you run a boutique, consider a seasonal sale or a themed event that encourages customers to come in, browse, and enjoy refreshments. This personal touch can make a lasting impression.
  • Offer loyalty programs or referral discounts: Encourage word-of-mouth marketing by creating loyalty programs that reward repeat customers. For example, offer a free item after a certain number of purchases or discounts for referring friends. This not only incentivizes existing customers but also helps you tap into their networks.

The La Crosse Business Community

The La Crosse business community is vibrant and supportive, making it a great environment for small businesses to thrive. Here are some key aspects to consider:

  • Engage with local chambers of commerce: Joining groups like the La Crosse Area Chamber of Commerce can provide valuable networking opportunities. These organizations often host events, workshops, and mixers that allow you to connect with other business owners and share resources.
  • Join community groups or forums: Participate in local business development groups on social media or through community websites. Platforms like Nextdoor can connect you with nearby residents and businesses, offering a great opportunity for collaboration and support.
  • Collaborate with other businesses: For joint promotions or events, consider teaming up with other businesses. For instance, if you own a gym, collaborate with a health food store to offer a health-focused event or seminar. This not only broadens your reach but also fosters community spirit.
  • Stay updated on local policies: Being informed about local policies and initiatives that affect small businesses can help you navigate the landscape more effectively. Engage with local government and support initiatives that benefit your business community.
  • Utilize resources offered by local institutions: Take advantage of workshops and seminars offered by local institutions like the Small Business Development Center. These resources can provide valuable insights into business growth strategies and help you refine your approach.

Attracting more customers in La Crosse doesn't have to be a daunting task. By implementing both online and offline strategies and tapping into the supportive local business community, you can create a strong customer base. Remember, building relationships is key in a close-knit community like La Crosse.
