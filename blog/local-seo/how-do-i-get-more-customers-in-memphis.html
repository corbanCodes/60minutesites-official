How Do I Get More Customers in Memphis?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Getting customers in Memphis can be a game-changer for your small business. With its vibrant culture, rich history, and a supportive community, there are plenty of ways to attract more clients and thrive in this unique market. Memphis is renowned for its music scene, delicious barbecue, and significant historical landmarks, making it a captivating destination for both locals and visitors alike.

Build a Professional Website

Your website is the foundation of your online presence. It should be professional, easy to navigate, and optimized for local search. Here's what actually works:

  • Make sure your website loads quickly – nobody has time to wait.
  • Include clear calls to action – tell visitors exactly what you want them to do.
  • Optimize for mobile – many people will check your site on their phones, especially while exploring the city's attractions.
  • Use local SEO techniques to rank higher in search results – think keywords like 'Memphis [your service]'.
  • Keep your content fresh – regularly update with blogs or news about local events, like the Beale Street Music Festival or the World Championship Barbecue Cooking Contest, to keep visitors engaged.

Leverage Google My Business

Setting up your Google My Business profile is crucial for visibility. The truth is, most people use Google to find services. Make sure to:

  • Claim and verify your listing to show up on local searches.
  • Keep your information accurate – hours, address, and services, especially if you’re near popular Memphis locations like Graceland or the National Civil Rights Museum.
  • Upload high-quality photos – people love visuals, especially for local businesses. Showcase images of your offerings alongside iconic Memphis landmarks.
  • Encourage happy customers to leave reviews – positive feedback can influence potential clients.
  • Post updates or special offers regularly to engage your audience, perhaps in conjunction with local events.

Harness the Power of Reviews and Referrals

Word-of-mouth is still one of the strongest tools for small businesses. I've seen this work time and time again:

  • Ask satisfied customers to leave reviews on Google and Yelp – these can boost your credibility.
  • Create a referral program to reward customers who bring in new business – a little incentive can go a long way.
  • Share testimonials on your website and social media – let others do the selling for you, especially if they mention your business in relation to popular Memphis experiences.

Engage on Social Media

Social media is a powerful way to connect with your audience. Here's how to make it work for you:

  • Choose the right platforms – focus on where your target customers hang out, like Facebook or Instagram, especially for promoting local events.
  • Post regularly and share engaging content – think behind-the-scenes looks, customer stories, and promotions centered around Memphis culture.
  • Use local hashtags to increase your reach – Memphis is known for its community spirit, so tap into that with tags like #MemphisEats or #MemphisMusic.
  • Engage with followers by responding to comments and messages – build that relationship with local customers who appreciate personal touches.

Network with Local Businesses

Building connections in the Memphis business community can lead to new opportunities. Here's what you can do:

  • Attend local events and business meetups – the more you network, the more visibility you gain. Look for opportunities at the Memphis Chamber of Commerce.
  • Partner with complementary businesses for cross-promotion – think about ways to help each other out, especially with neighboring businesses in areas like Cooper-Young or downtown Memphis.
  • Join local chambers or business associations – these can provide valuable resources and connections, particularly for small businesses.
  • Volunteer in community events – it shows you care and can attract local followers, such as participating in the Memphis in May festival.
