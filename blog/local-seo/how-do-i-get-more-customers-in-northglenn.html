Online Strategies to Boost Your Customer Base

Building a strong online presence is essential for small businesses today. Here’s how to effectively harness the internet to draw in more customers:

  • Create a user-friendly website with 60 Minute Sites: Your website is often the first impression potential customers will have of your business. Make sure it is visually appealing, easy to navigate, and mobile-friendly. Include clear calls to action, high-quality images of your products or services, and contact information. With 60 Minute Sites, you can quickly set up a professional site tailored to your needs.
  • Leverage social media platforms: Engage with your local audience on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. Post regularly about your offerings, share behind-the-scenes content, and highlight any special events or promotions. Consider using local hashtags and geotags to increase your visibility among Northglenn residents.
  • Utilize Google My Business: Ensure your business appears in local searches by creating a Google My Business listing. This allows you to provide crucial information such as hours of operation, location, and services offered. Regularly update your listing with new photos and posts to keep it fresh and engaging.
  • Encourage online reviews: Positive reviews can greatly enhance your credibility. Ask satisfied customers to leave reviews on platforms like Google, Yelp, or Facebook. Consider creating a follow-up email template thanking customers for their business and gently prompting them to share their experience online.
  • Run targeted online ads: Use platforms like Facebook Ads or Google Ads to reach Northglenn residents specifically. You can tailor your ads based on demographics, interests, and location, ensuring that your message reaches the right audience.

Offline Strategies to Attract Local Customers

Don’t forget the power of face-to-face interaction and community involvement. Here are some effective offline strategies to consider:

  • Participate in local events and fairs: Northglenn hosts numerous community events throughout the year, such as the Northglenn Summer Concert Series and the Northglenn Arts & Crafts Fair. Setting up a booth or sponsoring an event gives you the chance to showcase your products or services directly to potential customers.
  • Collaborate with other local businesses: Partnering with neighboring businesses can enhance your reach. For example, if you run a coffee shop, consider teaming up with a nearby bakery for a “coffee and pastry” promotion. This cross-promotion exposes both businesses to each other’s customer base.
  • Engage with community organizations: Connect with local schools, sports teams, and charities. Offering your services for fundraisers or donating items for auctions can significantly raise your visibility and demonstrate your commitment to the community.
  • Offer in-store promotions or discounts: Create enticing offers that encourage walk-in customers. Consider hosting “happy hour” discounts or “buy one, get one” deals during peak shopping times.
  • Utilize local newspapers and community boards: Advertise in the Northglenn-Thornton Sentinel or post flyers in local establishments like coffee shops and community centers. A well-placed ad can attract the attention of individuals who may not be active online.

  • Community events: The Northglenn Marketplace is a hub for local gatherings. Participating in or sponsoring events at this venue can give your business significant exposure. Look for events like the Northglenn Farmers Market, where you can set up a booth and engage with the community.
  • Local meet-up groups: Look for local business groups or meet-ups that focus on entrepreneurship and marketing. These gatherings can be a goldmine for sharing challenges, solutions, and ideas, as well as forming valuable connections.
  • Neighborhood associations: Engage with local associations to better understand the needs and preferences of residents. This insight can help you tailor your products or services to meet local demand.
  • Relationship building: Cultivate relationships with other local businesses. Collaborations can lead to referrals and joint promotions, benefitting everyone involved. For example, a florist and a wedding planner could work together to offer special packages for couples.
