How Do I Get More Customers in Austin?

9 min read

Get a Professional Local SEO Website in 60 Minutes →

Austin, Texas, known as the Live Music Capital of the World, is a vibrant city brimming with opportunities for small businesses to thrive. With a dynamic blend of tech innovation, a burgeoning cultural scene, and a strong local community, there’s no shortage of potential customers eager to discover what you have to offer. The city’s unique spirit is defined by its motto, "Keep Austin Weird," which celebrates local artisans, musicians, and entrepreneurs.

Build a Professional Website

In a tech-savvy city like Austin, having a professional website is essential for reaching customers online. This is your digital storefront, and it needs to shine. Here's what actually works:

  • Invest in a clean, user-friendly design that reflects your brand and resonates with Austin's creative vibe.
  • Optimize for mobile, as a significant number of users search for services on their phones while exploring the city.
  • Include clear calls to action (CTAs) to guide visitors on what to do next, like signing up for local events or subscribing to newsletters.
  • Showcase testimonials and case studies from local Austin clients to build trust and credibility.
  • Ensure your contact information is easy to find, perhaps even including a local area code.

Harness the Power of Google My Business

Claiming and optimizing your Google My Business profile is crucial for enhancing local visibility in a competitive market like Austin. I've seen this work time and time again:

  • Fill out all the details: business hours, services, and location, ideally with references to local landmarks.
  • Upload high-quality images showcasing your business, especially if you’re near popular areas like South Congress or the Domain.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews; they genuinely influence potential clients who often rely on feedback from fellow Austinites.
  • Regularly post updates about your services or promotions, aligning them with local events or festivals.

Leverage Reviews and Referrals

Positive reviews can be a game changer for your business in Austin's close-knit community. The hustle of running a local business can be tough, but word-of-mouth is still powerful:

  • Ask happy customers to write reviews on Google, Yelp, or Facebook, particularly after attending local events or markets.
  • Create a referral program offering discounts for customers who bring in new clients, tapping into Austin’s communal spirit.
  • Share success stories on your social media to inspire others and create a sense of belonging within the community.

Get Active on Social Media

Social media isn’t just for influencers; it’s a great way to engage with your local Austin audience:

  • Focus on platforms where your target demographic hangs out, like Instagram or Facebook, which are popular among Austin residents.
  • Share behind-the-scenes content to humanize your brand, especially your involvement in local culture or events.
  • Run local promotions or contests that resonate with the community and reflect Austin's eclectic character.
  • Collaborate with local influencers or businesses for increased exposure, leveraging the city’s vibrant network of creatives.

Networking and Local Events

Austin has a robust community that loves to support local businesses. Here’s how to tap into that:

  • Attend local networking events or join business groups like the Austin Chamber of Commerce to meet like-minded entrepreneurs.
  • Participate in local markets or fairs, such as the Austin Farmers’ Market, to showcase your offerings and connect with residents.
  • Partner with other local businesses for cross-promotions, especially during events like the Pecan Street Festival.
  • Get involved in community events to raise your profile and establish genuine relationships with locals.

Running a local business in Austin is an exhilarating journey filled with growth potential amidst a diverse and engaged community. Stay focused, be proactive in your marketing efforts, and remember that building relationships in your community will yield long-term rewards. Embrace the local culture, and let it inspire your business strategies.
