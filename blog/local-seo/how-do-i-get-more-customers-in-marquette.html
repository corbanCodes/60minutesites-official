How Do I Get More Customers in Marquette?

5 min read

Let's get real for a moment: running a small business in Marquette, Michigan, is a rewarding challenge, especially when trying to attract more customers. Whether you own a cozy café near Presque Isle Park, known for its stunning views of Lake Superior, or a boutique on Washington Street, bustling with both locals and tourists, finding fresh ways to engage your community can make all the difference. With the charm of Marquette’s historic downtown and the vibrant local culture, there are countless opportunities to connect with potential customers and grow your business.

Online Strategies to Boost Your Customer Base

Building a strong online presence is essential for attracting customers in today’s tech-driven world. Here are some focused strategies to help you make an impact:

  • Create an attractive, user-friendly website: Your website is often the first impression potential customers have of your business. Use tools like 60 Minute Sites to craft a visually appealing and easy-to-navigate website that highlights your products or services. Ensure it is mobile-friendly, as many customers search on their phones while on the go.
  • Engage with your audience on social media: Platforms like Facebook and Instagram are invaluable for connecting with your community. Share captivating photos of your offerings, promote local events, and offer exclusive social media discounts. Consider hosting live sessions or Q&A events to interact directly with your followers.
  • Utilize Google My Business: Ensure your business appears in local searches and on Google Maps by claiming your Google My Business listing. This free tool allows you to manage how your business appears online, including hours of operation, contact information, and even customer reviews. Encourage customers to leave reviews here to enhance your credibility.
  • Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews: Positive feedback on platforms like Yelp and TripAdvisor can significantly influence potential customers. Consider creating a small incentive, like a discount on their next purchase, for customers who take the time to review your business.
  • Start an email newsletter: Keep your customers informed about new products, special offers, and local happenings through an engaging email newsletter. Offer a sign-up incentive, such as a discount or a freebie, to grow your subscriber list.

Offline Strategies to Attract Local Customers

Don’t underestimate the power of traditional marketing methods in Marquette. Here are some effective offline strategies to consider:

  • Host or participate in local events: The Marquette Blues Festival and farmers' markets are perfect opportunities to connect with the community. Set up a booth to showcase your products or offer a special promotion for attendees. Engaging with locals in person can create lasting impressions.
  • Collaborate with other local businesses: Build relationships with fellow business owners for cross-promotions. For instance, if you run a café, partner with a nearby bakery to offer a combined breakfast special. Joint events can also draw larger crowds.
  • Utilize local newspapers and radio stations: Advertise special deals or events through local media outlets. Consider writing a guest column in the Marquette Monthly or participating in a local radio talk show to discuss your business and share insights.
  • Print eye-catching flyers or postcards: Distribute these materials in the neighborhood, especially in high-traffic areas. Highlight your unique offerings or upcoming events, and include a call to action that encourages people to visit your establishment.
  • Create a referral program: Reward customers for bringing in friends and family. Offer discounts or freebies for successful referrals, turning your existing customers into advocates for your business.

Connecting with the Marquette Business Community

The Marquette business community is vibrant and supportive, making it easier for you to find collaboration opportunities. Here are ways to get involved:

  • Join the Marquette Chamber of Commerce: This organization offers networking events, resources, and support tailored to local businesses. Attending their events can help you meet other entrepreneurs and learn about local initiatives.
  • Participate in local business associations: Engage with groups focused on business growth and community improvement. Sharing insights with fellow business owners can provide fresh perspectives and strategies.
  • Attend local workshops and seminars: Keep an eye out for events at places like Northern Michigan University or local libraries that offer workshops on marketing, business growth, and financial management. These opportunities can enhance your skills and provide valuable knowledge about the market.
  • Build relationships with local influencers: Identify local bloggers or social media personalities who align with your brand. Collaborating with them can give your business exposure to a wider audience.
  • Follow local news outlets: Stay informed about community projects, events, and opportunities for involvement. This knowledge can help you align your business with community needs and interests.

By combining both online and offline strategies and connecting with the Marquette business community, you can effectively attract more customers. With dedication and creativity, you’ll not only draw more customers but also build lasting relationships within the Marquette community.
