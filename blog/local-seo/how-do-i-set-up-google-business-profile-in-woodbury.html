Why You Need a Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile acts like your online storefront, and it’s crucial for attracting customers in Woodbury. Here’s why:

  • Enhances your visibility on Google Maps and Search: When locals search for businesses, your listing can appear prominently in search results and on Google Maps, increasing your chances of being discovered. Think about how often you rely on Google Maps when looking for a new coffee shop or restaurant in the area.
  • Allows you to showcase your services, hours, and location: Customers can quickly see what you offer, when you’re open, and exactly where to find you. This is especially important for Woodbury residents who appreciate convenience and accessibility—whether they’re visiting the Woodbury Commons Premium Outlets or enjoying a day at the nearby Woodbury Park.
  • Lets you interact with customers through reviews and Q&A: Engaging with customer feedback not only builds trust but also enhances your reputation. Responding to reviews, whether positive or negative, shows that you care about your customers and their experiences.
  • Gives you insights on customer behavior and preferences: Google provides analytics that can help you understand what attracts customers to your business, allowing you to tailor your offerings and marketing strategies to meet their needs better.

Steps to Set Up Your Google Business Profile

Getting started with your Google Business Profile is straightforward. Follow these steps:

  • Go to the Google Business Profile website and click on "Manage now": This will direct you to the setup page where the magic begins.
  • Sign in with your Google account or create one if needed: If you don’t already have a Google account, take a few moments to create one. It’s free and opens up a world of tools to help manage your business.
  • Enter your business name and address: Be sure to fill this out accurately, as consistency is key for local SEO. If you’re located in a well-known part of Woodbury, like near the Woodbury Common Premium Outlets, mention that in your address.
  • Select your business category: This helps Google understand what you offer. For example, if you run a bakery, selecting “Bakery” as your category will ensure you show up in relevant searches.
  • Add your phone number and website: Make it easy for potential customers to reach out. If you don’t have a website yet, consider creating one with the help of 60 Minute Sites, which can help you build an attractive and functional site quickly.
  • Complete your profile with business hours, photos, and a description: Highlight what makes your business unique. If you’re a local coffee shop, consider adding images of your cozy seating area or a signature drink. Your description should reflect your brand’s personality and values.
  • Verify your business: Follow the verification steps provided by Google, usually involving a postcard sent to your business address. This step is crucial for ensuring that your listing is genuine and trustworthy.

How Locals in Woodbury Search for Businesses

Understanding how the community searches can help you tailor your profile effectively. Here are some insights:

  • Many locals use phrases like "best pizza near me" or "hair salons in Woodbury": Optimize your profile with keywords that reflect common searches. If you’re a pizzeria, for example, make sure to use language that locals might type into Google.
  • Residents often rely on Google Maps: Especially when they’re out and about. Ensure your business location is marked accurately to avoid potential customers getting lost while looking for you.
  • Reviews play a significant role: Locals trust recommendations from fellow Woodbury residents. Encourage satisfied customers to leave glowing reviews, as this social proof can greatly influence new customers’ decisions.
  • People look for specific amenities: Whether it’s outdoor seating at a café or drive-thru options at a fast-food restaurant, make sure to highlight these features in your profile. This can help set you apart from competitors and attract more foot traffic.
