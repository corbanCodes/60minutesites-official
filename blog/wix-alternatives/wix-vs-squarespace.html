Wix vs Squarespace: Honest Comparison for 2026

12 min read January 2026

Wix and Squarespace are the two most popular website builders for small businesses and entrepreneurs. Both promise easy website creation without coding, professional templates, and all-in-one solutions. But they take fundamentally different approaches to website building, and the right choice depends on what matters most to you.

This comparison examines both platforms honestly, including their strengths and weaknesses, to help you make an informed decision. Neither platform is objectively better; they serve different users with different priorities.

The Fundamental Difference

Before diving into features, understand the core philosophy difference between these platforms.

Wix prioritizes flexibility. It gives you maximum control over design, letting you place elements anywhere on the page with complete freedom. This freedom comes with responsibility; you can create beautiful designs or chaotic messes depending on your skills.

Squarespace prioritizes design consistency. It constrains your choices in ways that make professional results more likely. You have less freedom, but that limitation means even design novices typically end up with polished-looking websites.

Ease of Use

Wix Experience

Wix uses a true drag-and-drop editor where you can position elements anywhere on the page. This feels intuitive initially but becomes challenging when you need consistent alignment, proper spacing, or responsive mobile layouts.

The interface offers tremendous options, which can overwhelm newcomers. Every element has extensive customization settings, and the app market adds hundreds more possibilities. For people who enjoy exploring features, this is exciting. For people who want to finish quickly, it becomes exhausting.

Wix offers an AI-assisted design option called ADI that creates initial websites based on your answers to questions. Results vary but can provide a starting point that requires less building from scratch.

Squarespace Experience

Squarespace uses a more structured editing approach. Instead of free-form placement, you work within a grid system that maintains alignment and proportions automatically. This feels more restrictive but produces consistently professional results.

The learning curve is moderate. Squarespace requires understanding how sections, blocks, and layouts work together. Once you grasp the system, editing becomes straightforward, but the initial learning takes longer than Wix's intuitive drag-and-drop.

Fewer customization options mean fewer decisions to make. For many users, this constraint is actually helpful rather than limiting.

Verdict: Ease of Use

Wix is easier to start but harder to finish well. Squarespace is harder to start but easier to end up with professional results. Choose based on whether you want maximum flexibility or guided simplicity.

Design and Templates

Wix Templates

Wix offers over 800 templates across numerous categories. The variety is impressive, and you can find industry-specific designs for almost any business type. Template quality varies; some are excellent while others look dated.

Important limitation: Once you choose a Wix template and build your site, switching templates means starting over. Your content does not transfer. This makes the initial template choice consequential.

The free-form editor means you can customize templates extensively, but this also means you can break the design if you are not careful.

Squarespace Templates

Squarespace offers around 150 templates, significantly fewer than Wix. However, Squarespace templates share underlying frameworks, and recent updates allow switching templates without losing content.

Template quality is consistently high. Squarespace is known for beautiful, modern, often minimalist designs. They look professional out of the box with less customization required.

The structured editor limits how much you can deviate from the template design, which maintains professional appearance but may feel restrictive if you have specific design visions.

Verdict: Design

Squarespace wins for consistently professional aesthetics with less effort. Wix wins for variety and maximum customization potential. If design quality matters more than uniqueness, Squarespace is safer.

Pricing Comparison

Wix Pricing

Wix offers a free plan with significant limitations including Wix branding and a Wix subdomain. Paid plans in 2026:

  • Light: $17/month - Remove Wix ads, custom domain, basic features
  • Core: $29/month - More storage, accept payments, basic analytics
  • Business: $36/month - Priority support, professional logo, more storage
  • Business Elite: $159/month - VIP support, unlimited storage, advanced features

Note that domain registration is separate and typically adds $15-20/year. Many useful features require app installations, some of which have their own monthly costs.

Squarespace Pricing

Squarespace has no free plan but offers a 14-day trial. Paid plans in 2026:

  • Personal: $16/month - Basic website, free custom domain first year
  • Business: $23/month - Professional email, premium integrations, basic e-commerce
  • Commerce Basic: $27/month - Full e-commerce, no transaction fees
  • Commerce Advanced: $49/month - Advanced e-commerce features

Squarespace includes a free custom domain for the first year on annual plans, making the initial year less expensive than it appears.

Verdict: Pricing

For basic business websites, pricing is comparable. Squarespace's included features often mean the total cost is lower than Wix when you add necessary apps. Calculate your specific needs rather than comparing base prices.

E-commerce Capabilities

Wix E-commerce

Wix offers solid e-commerce features for small to medium online stores. Product management, payment processing, shipping options, and tax calculations are all available. The platform integrates with major payment providers and shipping carriers.

Transaction fees apply on lower plans. The Business plan and above remove these fees but cost more monthly. App integrations can extend functionality but add complexity and potential cost.

Squarespace E-commerce

Squarespace e-commerce is streamlined and attractive. Product pages look beautiful by default, and the checkout process is clean. The platform handles basics well and includes features like inventory alerts, customer accounts, and abandoned cart recovery.

Commerce plans remove transaction fees entirely. Integration options are more limited than Wix, but necessary features are built in rather than requiring apps.

Verdict: E-commerce

Both platforms work for small online stores. Wix offers more flexibility and integration options. Squarespace offers more consistent design and simpler setup. For serious e-commerce businesses, Shopify probably beats both.

SEO Capabilities

Wix SEO

Wix has improved its SEO capabilities significantly over the years. The platform offers customizable URLs, meta descriptions, heading tags, alt text, and other standard SEO elements. The Wix SEO Wiz provides guided setup for basic optimization.

Technical SEO is partially limited because Wix controls the hosting infrastructure. Site speed varies, and you cannot implement every optimization that might be possible on other platforms.

Squarespace SEO

Squarespace includes standard SEO tools: custom URLs, meta descriptions, heading control, and alt text. The built-in analytics help track search traffic, and the platform generates sitemaps automatically.

Like Wix, technical SEO has limits because Squarespace controls hosting. However, Squarespace sites tend to have better baseline performance, which helps search rankings.

Verdict: SEO

Both platforms can rank well with proper optimization. Neither matches WordPress flexibility for advanced SEO. Squarespace's better typical performance gives it a slight edge, but content quality matters more than platform choice for most businesses.

Blogging

Wix Blogging

Wix includes blogging features with categories, tags, scheduling, and social sharing. The writing experience is decent, and you can customize blog layouts. RSS feeds and blog-specific SEO settings are available.

Squarespace Blogging

Squarespace has traditionally strong blogging features, reflecting its origins as a blogging platform. The writing experience is clean, and blog posts integrate seamlessly with the site design. Multiple authors, scheduling, categories, and tags are all supported.

Verdict: Blogging

Squarespace's blogging is more polished, but Wix's is adequate for most business blogs. Neither matches dedicated blogging platforms like WordPress or Ghost.

Customer Support

Wix Support

Wix offers 24/7 callback support on premium plans, extensive help documentation, a large community forum, and in-editor help. Phone support availability varies by plan level. Response quality is generally good.

Squarespace Support

Squarespace provides 24/7 email support and live chat on all plans. Phone support is not available. Their help guides are well-written and comprehensive, and the community forum is active.

Verdict: Support

Wix wins if phone support matters to you. Both platforms have solid documentation and eventually solve problems. Neither offers the kind of personalized support you would get from working with a developer or agency.

Who Should Choose Wix

Consider Wix if:

  • You want maximum creative control over design
  • You need a specific feature available through the Wix App Market
  • You prefer phone support availability
  • You enjoy learning platforms and customizing details
  • You want to start with a free option and upgrade later

Who Should Choose Squarespace

Consider Squarespace if:

  • Design quality and aesthetics are top priorities
  • You want professional results without design expertise
  • You prefer fewer choices and simpler decisions
  • Clean, modern, minimalist design appeals to you
  • You might want to change templates later

Who Should Consider Neither

Both Wix and Squarespace require you to build and maintain your own website. If you lack time for this, consider alternatives:

Done-for-you services: Have professionals build and maintain your site for monthly fees comparable to premium Wix or Squarespace plans.

WordPress with managed hosting: More flexibility than either platform but requires professional setup for best results.

Shopify: If e-commerce is your primary focus, a dedicated platform may serve you better than either general-purpose builder.

The best website platform is one that actually results in a professional, effective website. If DIY builders have not worked for you in the past, the issue may be the DIY approach rather than the specific platform.
