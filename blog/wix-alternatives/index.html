Wix has become one of the most recognizable names in website building. Their advertising is everywhere, and their promise of easy, drag-and-drop website creation appeals to business owners who want to get online without learning to code. But after using Wix, many people discover that what looked simple in advertisements becomes complicated in practice.

This guide is for business owners who are considering Wix, currently using Wix and frustrated, or simply want to understand all their options before making a decision. We will cover Wix honestly, including what it does well, and then explore every major alternative available in 2026.

Why Business Owners Look for Wix Alternatives

People search for Wix alternatives for different reasons. Some have never used Wix and want to compare options before committing. Others have been using Wix and encountered limitations that are holding back their business. Understanding these common frustrations helps clarify what to look for in an alternative.

Time Investment Exceeds Expectations

The biggest complaint about Wix is the time required to build and maintain a professional-looking website. While Wix markets itself as a quick solution, the reality is that designing a website from scratch, even with templates, takes significant time. Most business owners underestimate this. They plan to have their site done in a weekend and find themselves still working on it weeks later.

This is not necessarily Wix's fault. Any DIY website builder requires learning the platform, making design decisions, writing content, and troubleshooting issues. The problem is that the marketing creates expectations that do not match reality.

Hidden Costs Add Up

Wix's pricing structure can be confusing. The free plan includes Wix branding and a Wix subdomain, which looks unprofessional for businesses. Upgrading to remove these costs money. Then you discover that features you assumed were included, like accepting payments or removing storage limits, require even higher tier plans.

When you add up the actual cost of a Wix website with a custom domain, no ads, adequate storage, and essential features, the price is often higher than alternatives that include more by default.

SEO Concerns

Search engine optimization on Wix has improved over the years, but it still presents challenges. Wix sites can rank well, but many business owners find the SEO tools limited compared to other platforms. The technical SEO setup, like site speed and URL structure, is partially outside your control since Wix hosts everything.

Design Limitations

Wix offers tremendous creative freedom in some ways but restrictive limitations in others. Once you choose a template, changing to a different one means starting over. Certain design elements cannot be customized beyond the options Wix provides. For business owners with specific brand requirements, these limitations become frustrating.

Performance Issues

Some Wix websites load slowly, particularly those with many elements, large images, or complex layouts. Since you cannot optimize the underlying code or choose your hosting infrastructure, you are dependent on Wix's performance. This can affect both user experience and search rankings.

Lock-In Concerns

When you build on Wix, your website exists within their ecosystem. You cannot export your site to another platform easily. If you decide to leave, you essentially start over elsewhere. This vendor lock-in makes some business owners uncomfortable, especially as their business grows.

What Wix Does Well

Before exploring alternatives, let us acknowledge where Wix genuinely excels. A fair comparison requires understanding the full picture.

Ease of Getting Started

Wix makes it very easy to sign up and begin building. The interface is intuitive for people who have never built a website before. You can see changes in real-time as you make them, which feels more accessible than platforms that require previewing separately.

Template Variety

Wix offers hundreds of templates across dozens of industries. No matter what type of business you run, you can probably find a template designed for your industry. The templates are professionally designed and look modern.

All-In-One Platform

Wix handles hosting, security certificates, and technical maintenance. You do not need to find separate hosting, worry about updates, or manage server configuration. For people who want nothing to do with technical details, this simplicity has value.

App Ecosystem

The Wix App Market offers hundreds of add-ons for functionality like booking, email marketing, live chat, and more. While some apps cost extra, the ecosystem means you can add features without coding.

Customer Support

Wix provides support through multiple channels, including phone support on higher-tier plans. Their help documentation is extensive. When you encounter problems, you have resources to find answers.

Where Wix Falls Short

Understanding Wix's limitations helps you evaluate whether alternatives better address your specific needs.

Template Switching

If you want to change your website's template after building it, you lose your content and must recreate your site from scratch. This limitation is significant because your design preferences may evolve, or you may discover after launching that a different template would work better.

Code Access

While Wix offers some custom code options through Velo, you cannot access or modify the core code of your website. Developers or agencies who want to make customizations beyond what Wix allows will hit walls.

Export Options

You cannot export a Wix website to move it elsewhere. Your content, including text and images, can be manually copied, but the actual website, its design, structure, and functionality, stays with Wix. Moving away means rebuilding entirely.

E-commerce Limitations

Wix's e-commerce features work for basic online stores, but businesses with complex product catalogs, multiple shipping options, or high transaction volumes often outgrow the platform. Transaction fees on lower plans also reduce margins.

Multilingual Support

While Wix offers multilingual capabilities, implementing them adds complexity and cost. Businesses serving multiple language markets may find more streamlined solutions elsewhere.

Loading Speed

Wix sites often score lower on speed tests compared to optimized alternatives. While Wix has made improvements, you have limited ability to optimize performance since you cannot control the hosting infrastructure or code efficiency.

DIY Website Builder Alternatives

If you want to maintain control over building and maintaining your website yourself, several alternatives to Wix offer different strengths.

Squarespace

Squarespace is Wix's most direct competitor in the website builder space. It appeals to design-conscious users who prioritize aesthetics. Squarespace templates tend toward clean, modern, minimalist designs that work well for creative professionals, portfolios, and businesses where visual presentation matters.

Pros: Beautiful templates, integrated blogging, reliable performance, strong e-commerce for small stores, good image handling

Cons: Less flexible than Wix, fewer templates, steeper learning curve for some, limited third-party integrations

Best for: Creative professionals, portfolios, restaurants, small retailers who prioritize design quality

Webflow

Webflow bridges the gap between website builders and custom development. It provides visual design tools while generating clean, professional code. This appeals to designers who want more control than Wix offers but do not want to code from scratch.

Pros: Professional-quality output, design flexibility, clean code, CMS capabilities, good animations

Cons: Steep learning curve, more expensive, overkill for simple sites, requires design knowledge

Best for: Designers, agencies, businesses with complex design requirements, marketing sites

Shopify

If your primary goal is selling products online, Shopify is purpose-built for e-commerce. While it can create general business websites, its strength is online stores. The platform handles inventory, payments, shipping, taxes, and other e-commerce complexities.

Pros: Excellent e-commerce features, payment processing, inventory management, extensive app ecosystem, scalable

Cons: Transaction fees unless using Shopify Payments, expensive apps, less suitable for non-e-commerce sites

Best for: Online stores, retail businesses, product-based companies

GoDaddy Website Builder

GoDaddy's website builder offers extreme simplicity at the cost of flexibility. It uses AI to generate websites quickly based on your answers to questions. This works for business owners who want the fastest possible path to having something online.

Pros: Very fast setup, simple interface, integrated domain registration, affordable entry plans

Cons: Limited customization, basic features, less professional results, limited e-commerce

Best for: Very small businesses, those needing just a simple online presence quickly

Content Management System Alternatives

Content management systems offer more power and flexibility than website builders but require more technical knowledge or professional help to implement.

WordPress

WordPress powers over 40% of websites on the internet. It is an open-source platform that can create virtually any type of website. The flexibility comes with complexity. WordPress itself is free, but you need hosting, a theme, plugins, and often professional help.

Pros: Unlimited flexibility, thousands of themes, massive plugin ecosystem, you own your site, strong SEO capabilities

Cons: Requires hosting, security responsibility, updates and maintenance, steeper learning curve, can break

Best for: Blogs, content-heavy sites, businesses that will grow significantly, those who want full ownership

Ghost

Ghost is a streamlined platform focused on publishing and content creation. If your website is primarily about content, such as a blog, newsletter, or publication, Ghost offers an excellent writing experience with membership and subscription features built in.

Pros: Beautiful writing experience, built-in membership, fast performance, clean interface, SEO-friendly

Cons: Limited for non-content sites, fewer design options, requires Ghost hosting or self-hosting

Best for: Bloggers, publishers, creators with membership models, content-first businesses

Done-For-You Website Services

Many business owners discover that their real objection is not to Wix specifically but to DIY website building in general. If your time is better spent running your business than learning web design, done-for-you services may be the right alternative.

What Done-For-You Means

Done-for-you website services handle the entire process: design, development, content placement, and often ongoing maintenance. You provide information about your business and receive a finished website without dragging and dropping anything yourself.

Benefits of Done-For-You

Time savings: Hours or days instead of weeks or months. Professionals who build websites daily complete the work faster than someone learning as they go.

Professional results: Websites built by professionals typically look more polished and function better than DIY attempts. Design principles, user experience best practices, and technical optimization come standard.

No learning curve: You do not need to learn any platform. Your involvement is providing content and feedback, not making design decisions or troubleshooting technical issues.

Ongoing support: Many done-for-you services include maintenance and updates. When you need changes, someone else handles them.

When Done-For-You Makes Sense

Done-for-you services make sense when your time has significant value. If you bill clients by the hour, every hour spent on your website is an hour not generating revenue. If your business requires your attention, diverting focus to web design has opportunity costs.

Service-based businesses, particularly those where the owner is the primary service provider, often benefit most from this approach. A plumber, consultant, or therapist building their own website loses billable time and produces amateur results compared to professionals.

How to Choose the Right Alternative

With so many options, choosing the right alternative depends on your specific situation. Consider these factors:

Your Time Availability

Be honest about how much time you can dedicate to building and maintaining a website. DIY platforms require ongoing attention. If you do not have time for that, done-for-you services or hiring a professional make more sense regardless of which platform they use.

Your Technical Comfort

Some people genuinely enjoy learning new technology and building things. Others find it frustrating and would rather focus on their area of expertise. Neither preference is wrong, but it should influence your choice.

Your Budget

Calculate the true cost of each option, including your time. A free Wix site costs nothing in dollars but may cost dozens of hours. A done-for-you service costs more in dollars but saves those hours for revenue-generating activities.

Your Business Type

E-commerce businesses have different needs than service businesses. Content-focused sites have different requirements than portfolios. Choose a platform or service that aligns with your primary website purpose.

Your Growth Plans

Where will your business be in three years? Platforms with limitations may work fine today but constrain you later. Consider whether an alternative can grow with your business or whether you will need to migrate again.

Migrating Away from Wix

If you are currently on Wix and want to move to an alternative, understand that migration requires rebuilding your site elsewhere. Wix does not offer export options.

What You Can Take

You can copy your content: text, images, videos, and documents. You can move your domain name if you own it. Customer data and email lists can be exported from Wix apps in most cases.

What You Cannot Take

Your website design, structure, and functionality must be recreated on the new platform. Any custom features built with Wix-specific tools will need alternative implementations.

Planning Your Migration

Document your current site structure before starting. List all pages, their content, and their purpose. Catalog any features or integrations you use. This inventory guides rebuilding on your new platform.

Consider timing carefully. Avoid migrating during your busiest business period. Plan for potential disruption during the transition and communicate with customers if necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wix bad for small businesses?

Wix is not inherently bad. It works well for some businesses, particularly those where the owner enjoys building their own website and has time to invest in learning the platform. It becomes problematic when business owners lack time, end up with amateur results, or outgrow the platform's capabilities.

What is the best Wix alternative for beginners?

For beginners who want to build their own site, Squarespace offers a more guided experience with consistently professional results. For beginners who prefer not to build anything, done-for-you services eliminate the learning curve entirely.

Can I move my Wix site to WordPress?

You cannot directly export a Wix site to WordPress. You would need to recreate your site on WordPress, copying content manually. Your domain can be transferred once the new site is ready.

Is Squarespace better than Wix?

Neither is objectively better. Squarespace offers more design consistency and slightly better performance. Wix offers more flexibility and customization options. The better choice depends on your priorities.

How much does it cost to have someone build a website instead of using Wix?

Costs vary widely. Freelance developers charge $500-5000+ for basic business sites. Agencies charge $3,000-20,000+. Done-for-you services like 60 Minute Sites offer professional websites for monthly rates comparable to Wix subscriptions.

Do Wix sites rank well on Google?

Wix sites can rank well on Google, but they face some technical SEO limitations compared to platforms like WordPress. Success depends more on content quality and SEO effort than platform choice, though some platforms make optimization easier.

Should I use Wix or hire someone?

Use Wix if you have more time than money, enjoy building things yourself, and need a relatively simple site. Hire someone if your time is valuable, you want professional results, or you would rather focus on running your business than learning web design.
