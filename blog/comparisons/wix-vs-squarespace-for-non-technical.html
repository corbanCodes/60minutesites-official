Wix vs Squarespace for Non-Technical Users

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Imagine trying to build a sandcastle with your bare hands—overwhelming, right? Now picture having a bucket and shovel at your disposal. That's the difference between navigating website builders like Wix and Squarespace for non-technical users. Both platforms promise to help you create a stunning site, but how do they stack up for those who aren't fluent in tech jargon? Let’s dive deeper into the specifics of each platform to help you make an informed decision.

User-Friendliness

When it comes to ease of use, both Wix and Squarespace cater to non-technical users, but they each have their own flavor:

  • Wix: Offers a drag-and-drop interface that feels intuitive. You can literally place elements anywhere on the page, which is great for creative freedom. If you want to move that photo to the left or add a text box in the middle of your design, you can do it with ease. Wix also includes a tutorial and support section that can guide you through the initial setup, making it particularly user-friendly for beginners.
  • Squarespace: Features a more structured layout system, which might feel limiting at first but ultimately helps in maintaining a clean and professional look. The platform uses a grid layout that ensures your elements remain aligned, which can be a relief for those who might struggle with design principles. Squarespace also offers a variety of video tutorials and live chat support, making it easier for users to learn as they go.

Templates and Design Flexibility

Your website's design is often the first impression potential customers get, so it matters. Here's how Wix and Squarespace compare:

  • Wix: Has an extensive library of over 500 templates and allows you to customize them freely. This can be perfect for those who want their website to reflect their personality or brand. You can modify colors, fonts, images, and layouts without any coding knowledge. For example, if you run a bakery in the heart of Brooklyn, you can choose a template that features warm colors and inviting images that resonate with your unique style.
  • Squarespace: Known for its elegant and modern designs, aimed at creatives like photographers and artists. The templates are visually stunning and come with high-quality image displays, which are ideal for showcasing portfolios. However, customization options are somewhat limited, which may be a drawback for those who want to inject more personal flair into their sites. If you’re a photographer looking to create a portfolio that highlights your work without distractions, Squarespace’s minimalistic designs might be just what you need.

Features and Functionality

Both platforms offer a range of features, but they cater to different needs:

  • Wix: Offers a plethora of apps and integrations through its App Market, which means you can add functionality easily as your needs grow. Whether you want to integrate a booking system for your local yoga studio or an email marketing tool for your online retail shop, Wix makes it straightforward. This is ideal for those who want to experiment with features and customize their websites extensively. Think of it as building a toolbox where you can add new tools as your skills improve.
  • Squarespace: While it has fewer third-party integrations, it comes with built-in features tailored for blogging, e-commerce, and portfolios, making it great for users who want a more cohesive experience. For instance, if you’re starting a blog about local hiking trails in the Smoky Mountains, Squarespace’s blogging tools can help you format your posts beautifully and manage SEO without needing additional plugins. This all-in-one functionality allows you to focus on creating content rather than managing multiple tools.

Pricing

Budget is often a deciding factor, so let's break it down:

  • Wix: Offers a free plan that includes Wix branding and ads, which can be a good starting point if you’re testing the waters. Paid plans vary, giving you more options based on what features you need. The Combo plan is perfect for personal use, while the Business Unlimited plan suits small businesses looking to sell online. This flexibility allows you to scale up as your needs grow, making it budget-friendly for startups.
  • Squarespace: Does not have a free plan but offers a 14-day free trial, allowing you to explore its features before committing. Their pricing is straightforward, with plans that cater to different needs, from personal sites to fully-fledged online stores. The basic plan is ideal for personal websites, while the advanced e-commerce plan includes features like abandoned cart recovery and advanced analytics, appealing to those who want simplicity over options.

Ultimately, both Wix and Squarespace have their strengths, but choosing the right one depends on your specific needs and comfort levels. If you value creative freedom and customization, Wix might be your best bet. On the other hand, if you prefer a polished, professional look with built-in features, Squarespace could be the way to go.
