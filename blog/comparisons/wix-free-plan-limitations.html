Wix Free Plan Limitations: What You Can't Do

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Here's the thing: opting for the free version of Wix might seem like a great way to dip your toes into website building, but it comes with some significant limitations that could impact your business. If you're considering this path, let’s break down what you can’t do on the Wix free plan and what that might mean for you. Whether you’re a small business owner, a freelancer, or someone looking to showcase your portfolio, understanding these constraints is crucial for setting realistic expectations about your online presence.

Advertisement of Wix Branding

One of the first things you’ll notice on your free Wix site is the prominent branding. This can have far-reaching implications for your site's professionalism. Here’s what to consider:

  • Your site will display Wix ads, which can detract from your professional image. Imagine visiting a website that looks promising, only to be interrupted by a large advertisement promoting the very platform it was built on. This could lead potential customers to question your credibility.
  • You cannot remove these ads unless you upgrade to a paid plan. If you're serious about your brand, investing in a paid plan to eliminate these distractions may be worth considering.

Limited Storage and Bandwidth

The free plan has restrictions on storage and bandwidth that can hinder your growth. Here’s what to keep in mind:

  • You get only 500 MB of storage, which can quickly fill up if you include images, videos, and other media. For instance, a few high-quality images can eat up this space rapidly, leaving you with limited options to expand your site with engaging content.
  • Your bandwidth is capped, limiting the number of visitors your site can handle without experiencing slowdowns. If your site experiences a spike in traffic, you might find that loading times slow down significantly, which could frustrate visitors and lead to a loss of potential customers.

No Custom Domain

Using a free plan means you won’t have a custom domain name, which can impact your brand’s credibility:

  • Your website URL will include 'wixsite.com,' making it harder for potential customers to remember or find you. For example, a URL like 'yourbusinessname.wixsite.com' can feel less trustworthy compared to 'yourbusinessname.com.'
  • A custom domain provides a professional touch that can enhance your business presence. It communicates to your audience that you’re serious about your brand and gives an impression of stability and professionalism.

E-commerce Limitations

If you’re thinking about selling products online, the free plan isn’t the way to go:

  • You can’t use Wix’s e-commerce capabilities without upgrading. This means no online store, payment processing, or inventory management for your business. If you have a product to sell, being unable to showcase it effectively online could mean missing out on significant revenue potential.
  • Furthermore, the inability to integrate with payment solutions might limit your ability to reach customers who prefer to shop online. In an environment where e-commerce is booming, this limitation can be particularly detrimental.

Limited Access to Features and Apps

A significant drawback is that many premium features and apps are not available on the free plan:

  • Access to advanced analytics, marketing tools, and third-party apps is restricted. For instance, you won't be able to utilize email marketing integrations or advanced SEO tools that can help your site gain visibility in search engines.
  • You miss out on features that could enhance your site's functionality and user experience. Imagine wanting to integrate a chat feature to engage with potential customers in real-time, only to find it's locked behind a paywall. This can limit your ability to connect with your audience effectively.

Choosing the Wix free plan may seem tempting, but its limitations can pose challenges for your business.
