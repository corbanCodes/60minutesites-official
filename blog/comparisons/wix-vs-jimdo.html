Wix vs Jimdo: Small Business Comparison

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Choosing between Wix and Jimdo is akin to selecting between two beloved local coffee shops that each offer a unique blend of flavors. Both platforms cater to small businesses, but they serve different tastes and needs. Let's take a closer look at how these website builders compare, so you can confidently select the one that perfectly aligns with your business goals and style.

Ease of Use

When it comes to building your website, user-friendliness can make a world of difference. Here's how Wix and Jimdo compare in terms of ease of use:

  • Wix: The drag-and-drop interface is incredibly intuitive, resembling a creative playground where you can arrange elements like text boxes, images, and videos with ease. For instance, if you run a local bakery, you can easily showcase your delicious pastries with captivating images and enticing descriptions, all without needing to know a lick of code.
  • Jimdo: This platform adopts a more streamlined, wizard-like approach that allows you to create a website rapidly. While it offers fewer customization options, this can actually be beneficial if you want to launch your site quickly. For instance, if you're a freelance graphic designer, you can set up a portfolio and get your services in front of potential clients in no time.

Design Flexibility

Your website's design plays a critical role in attracting and retaining customers. Let's see how Wix and Jimdo stack up in this area:

  • Wix: With hundreds of stunning templates tailored to various industries—from restaurants to retail—you'll find a design that resonates with your brand's identity. For example, if you own a quaint coffee shop, you can choose a template that highlights your cozy atmosphere and rich coffee selections, complete with customizable fonts and colors to match your branding.
  • Jimdo: While it offers fewer templates, Jimdo focuses on simplicity and functionality. This can be advantageous if you appreciate a clean, no-frills design. For example, a personal trainer might prefer the straightforward, professional layout that Jimdo provides, allowing their services and client testimonials to take center stage without unnecessary distractions.

SEO Capabilities

For small businesses, being found online is crucial. Let’s delve into the SEO capabilities of each platform:

  • Wix: With a robust suite of SEO tools at your disposal, including customizable URLs, meta tags, and an integrated SEO wizard, Wix empowers you to enhance your site's visibility on search engines. For example, if you own a local flower shop, you can optimize your website for phrases like "flower delivery in [Your City]," which can drive targeted traffic to your business.
  • Jimdo: While Jimdo provides basic SEO functionalities such as Google Analytics integration and mobile optimization, it may lack the comprehensive tools offered by Wix. This could limit your ability to fine-tune your search engine performance. However, for a small business with a primarily local clientele, Jimdo’s basic features might suffice for initial growth.

E-commerce Features

If your goal is to sell products online, the e-commerce features of each platform are key considerations:

  • Wix: Offering a complete e-commerce solution, Wix includes a range of features such as abandoned cart recovery, multiple payment gateways, and customizable product pages. For example, if you run a handmade jewelry business, you can set up an online store with beautiful images and descriptions, and even send reminder emails to customers who left items in their cart, encouraging them to complete their purchase.
  • Jimdo: While Jimdo provides basic e-commerce functionality, it may not be as feature-rich as Wix if you intend to scale your online store. It’s suitable for small operations, but larger businesses with extensive product lines may find it lacking in capabilities like inventory management and advanced shipping options.

Pricing

Budget considerations are always essential when choosing a website builder. Here’s a breakdown of the pricing structures:

  • Wix: Offers a diverse range of pricing plans, starting from a free option (which includes Wix branding and ads) to premium plans that unlock advanced features and additional storage. Depending on your needs, you might find a plan that fits your budget while providing the tools you need to grow, such as the Business Unlimited plan for e-commerce.
  • Jimdo: Typically features a simpler pricing structure with straightforward plans that may appeal to those who prefer transparency. You won’t find as many options as Wix, but this can make it easier to choose a plan that meets your needs without feeling overwhelmed. For instance, the Jimdo Grow plan allows e-commerce functionalities at a reasonable price, which might be perfect for small shops just starting out.

Ultimately, the decision between Wix and Jimdo hinges on your business's unique needs and your personal preferences. If you crave extensive design flexibility and robust e-commerce capabilities, Wix could be the ideal choice for you. On the other hand, if you favor simplicity and a rapid setup process, Jimdo might just be the right fit. With their expertise, you can be confident that your online presence will shine.
