How Much Does Wix Really Cost Per Year

5 min read

When you're setting out to build a website, having a clear understanding of the costs involved is crucial to avoid any unexpected financial surprises later on. If you're eyeing Wix as your website-building platform, it's important to dig deeper into what Wix really costs each year. This way, you can effectively budget and make well-informed decisions that align with your goals.

Understanding Wix Pricing Plans

Wix offers a variety of pricing plans tailored to meet different needs and preferences. Here’s a detailed overview of the main plans you can choose from:

  • Free Plan: This plan allows you to sign up and start building a website without any financial commitment. However, it comes with Wix ads displayed on your site and a Wix subdomain (like username.wixsite.com/sitename), which may not present the professional image you desire.
  • Combo Plan: Ideal for personal use, this plan typically costs around $14/month. It removes Wix ads and allows for a custom domain, making your site look more professional. This is a good choice for blogs, portfolios, or personal sites.
  • Unlimited Plan: Priced at about $18/month, this plan is perfect for entrepreneurs and small businesses. It offers unlimited bandwidth and additional storage, which is essential if you plan to upload a lot of content or media files.
  • Business Basic Plan: At approximately $23/month, this plan is tailored for online stores, enabling e-commerce capabilities. It includes features like secure online payments and the ability to sell products or services directly from your site.
  • VIP Plan: For about $39/month, you receive priority support and advanced features designed for users who want to take their site to the next level, including more storage, increased bandwidth, and additional marketing tools.

Hidden Costs to Consider

While you may have a basic understanding of Wix’s pricing, it's important to look out for additional costs that can affect your overall budget:

  • Domain Registration: If you want a unique domain name (like www.yoursite.com), registering it can cost around $15/year. This is a small price to pay for establishing your brand identity online.
  • Premium Apps: The Wix App Market is filled with features that can enhance your site, but many of these premium apps may require additional fees. For instance, if you’re looking for advanced analytics or marketing tools, those could come with their own subscription costs.
  • Image and Stock Photos: High-quality images are vital for creating an appealing website. If you don’t have your own images, purchasing stock photos can add up quickly, with prices ranging from $1 to $10 per image, depending on the source.
  • SEO Tools: Investing in premium SEO tools can greatly enhance your visibility on search engines. While Wix provides basic SEO features, more advanced options may come at an extra cost, which is worth considering if you're serious about attracting traffic.

Calculating the Total Annual Cost

To truly understand the annual cost of using Wix, it’s important to factor in your chosen plan alongside any additional expenses:

  • If you opt for the Combo Plan at $14/month, your total annual cost would be approximately $168, which does not include domain registration or any premium apps.
  • The Unlimited Plan at $18/month would bring your yearly total to about $216, plus any extra app fees you might incur for additional features.
  • The Business Basic Plan, priced at $23/month, results in an annual cost of around $276, and you’ll want to consider the costs of domain registration and any e-commerce tools you may need.

By calculating these costs, you’ll see how quickly expenses can add up, especially if you decide to incorporate premium features or services to enhance your site’s functionality.

Is Wix Worth It?

The value of Wix ultimately hinges on your unique needs and circumstances. Here are some factors to consider when evaluating whether Wix is the right platform for you:

  • Your Budget: Reflect on how much you’re willing to invest in your website. Wix offers various plans that can cater to different budgets, but it’s crucial to know your limits.
  • The Features You Need: Think about what functionalities are essential for your website. If you require e-commerce capabilities, the Business Basic Plan may be worth the investment. If you’re simply showcasing a portfolio, the Combo Plan may suffice.
  • Your Time Investment: Consider how much time you want to spend designing and maintaining your website. Wix is user-friendly, but if you’re looking for a more complex site, you might need to invest additional time or money.

If you’re searching for a platform that is intuitive and offers a wide range of customizable options, Wix could be a valuable investment. Just be sure to account for all potential costs to sidestep any surprises in the future.
