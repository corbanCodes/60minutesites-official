Wix vs Squarespace for Complete Beginners

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Imagine trying to build your dream home with nothing but a toolbox and a vision. That's what choosing a website builder like Wix or Squarespace feels like for beginners. Each platform has its own unique features, and diving in can be a bit overwhelming, but don't worry—I'm here to help you find the right fit. Whether you're looking to start a blog, showcase your portfolio, or launch an online store, understanding the nuances of these two popular platforms will empower you to make an informed decision. Let's explore the details that can transform your website-building experience into something truly rewarding.

Ease of Use

If you're just starting out, user-friendliness is key. Here's how Wix and Squarespace stack up:

  • Wix: Features a drag-and-drop editor, making it incredibly easy to place elements exactly where you want them. You can click and drag images, text boxes, and other elements without any coding knowledge. With Wix, you can also take advantage of the Wix ADI (Artificial Design Intelligence), which creates a personalized site for you based on your answers to a few simple questions.
  • Squarespace: Offers a more structured interface with pre-designed templates, which can be a bit more challenging for absolute beginners. While the platform is aesthetically pleasing and intuitive, it may take some time to get used to the layout and options available. Unlike Wix, customization is somewhat limited to the template's structure.
  • Recommendation: If you're looking for extensive customization and enjoy a hands-on approach, Wix might be your best bet. If you prefer polished designs with less fuss and are okay with a more guided structure, check out Squarespace. Remember, the platform you choose should align with how much flexibility you want versus how much guidance you need.

Design and Templates

Your website's appearance is crucial. Let's break down the template offerings:

  • Wix: Offers a vast library of over 500 templates across various categories, from business to photography to blogs, giving you plenty of choices. Each template is highly customizable, allowing you to alter colors, fonts, and layouts to match your personal style. The flexibility is ideal if you want your website to feel unique and tailored to you.
  • Squarespace: Features about 100 templates, but they are known for their sleek, modern aesthetic that can make your site look professionally designed right out of the box. Squarespace's templates are particularly good for creatives, as they focus on visual storytelling and high-quality imagery, making them perfect for photographers, artists, and designers.
  • Recommendation: Choose Wix for variety and creative control, but if a sophisticated look is what you seek and you want to impress visitors with a polished design, Squarespace is a strong contender. Consider your target audience and the impression you want to leave when making this decision.

Pricing Options

Budget is always a consideration. Here’s what you can expect:

  • Wix: Has a free plan, but it comes with ads that can detract from your site's professionalism. Paid plans start affordably, giving you the option to scale up as your needs grow. For example, the Combo plan is great for personal use, while the Business Unlimited plan allows for e-commerce capabilities.
  • Squarespace: Does not offer a free tier, but the plans include hosting and are slightly more comprehensive from the start. Their plans range from personal to advanced e-commerce options, which means you can choose a plan based on the features you need right from the beginning.
  • Recommendation: If you're looking to dip your toes without immediate costs, Wix is the way to go. For a more integrated experience that includes hosting and a professional feel from the start, Squarespace offers solid value. Always factor in what features you might need in the future to avoid having to switch platforms later on.

E-commerce Features

If you’re aiming to sell products online, e-commerce capabilities matter:

  • Wix: Provides a straightforward e-commerce setup with plenty of customizable options. You can easily add products, manage inventory, and accept payments through various gateways like PayPal and Stripe. The Wix App Market also offers additional tools to enhance your store's functionality, such as shipping solutions and marketing integrations.
  • Squarespace: Offers built-in e-commerce solutions that are easy to manage, with great looking product displays that enhance the shopping experience. You can create beautiful product pages, manage inventory, and offer subscriptions or digital products. Their integrated marketing tools also help you promote your store effectively, which is a plus for growing businesses.
  • Recommendation: For a simple online store, Wix is user-friendly and perfect for beginners. If you want to showcase art or high-end products with a stunning visual appeal, Squarespace excels in delivering an exceptional shopping experience. Think about what type of products you’re selling and how you want them to be presented before making a choice.

Customer Support

When you're starting out, support can make a big difference. Here's how they compare:

  • Wix: Offers 24/7 support via phone and email, along with an extensive help center filled with articles, videos, and forums. Their customer service team is known for being responsive, which can be a lifesaver if you encounter issues as a beginner.
  • Squarespace: Provides email support and live chat during certain hours, with a focus on self-help resources. Their help center is well-organized, featuring numerous articles and guides that cover everything from basic setups to advanced features. However, some users may find the limited hours of live support a challenge.
  • Recommendation: If you value immediate assistance, Wix has the edge with its round-the-clock support. Squarespace is great if you enjoy exploring solutions independently and can navigate the wealth of resources available. Assess your comfort level with troubleshooting and decide accordingly.
