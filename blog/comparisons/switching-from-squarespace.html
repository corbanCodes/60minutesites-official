How to Switch From Squarespace

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Here's the reality: choosing the right website builder can feel like navigating a maze. If you've decided it's time to leave Squarespace and explore new horizons, you're not alone. Many small business owners find themselves wanting more flexibility or specific features that just aren’t available on their current platform. Whether you're looking for more customization options, better e-commerce functionalities, or simply a more user-friendly experience, transitioning away from Squarespace can open up a world of possibilities. Let’s walk through the steps to ensure your website transformation is smooth and successful.

Assess Your Current Site

Before you dive into switching from Squarespace, take a moment to evaluate your existing site. This will help you create a plan for your new website. Consider the following:

  • What features are essential for your business? Make a list of the functionalities you currently use and identify which ones are critical for your operations. For instance, do you rely heavily on blogging, e-commerce, or a specific booking system?
  • Which pages do you need to keep? Review your site map and determine which pages are performing well and should be kept in the new design. This could include your homepage, product pages, and contact information.
  • How does your current design reflect your brand? Analyze the design elements that resonate with your audience. Are there specific colors, fonts, or layouts that align with your brand identity?
  • Are there integrations with tools you use that need to be addressed? List any third-party tools you use, such as social media plugins, email marketing services, or analytics tools. Make sure your new platform can seamlessly integrate with them.

Choose a New Platform

Once you know what you need, it’s time to find a new website builder that meets those requirements. Here are some options to consider:

  • 60 Minute Sites – This platform is perfect for small businesses needing fast setup and easy editing. With user-friendly templates and quick customization options, you can get your site up and running swiftly.
  • WordPress – Great for flexibility and customization, WordPress offers thousands of themes and plugins. While there’s a learning curve, the potential for creating a unique site is vast. Consider using a managed WordPress hosting service to simplify the setup process.
  • Wix – Known for its drag-and-drop features, Wix is incredibly user-friendly, making it ideal for those who prefer a visual approach to website building. You can also explore their extensive app market for additional functionalities.
  • Shopify – If e-commerce is your primary focus, Shopify is a leading choice. With built-in payment processing, inventory management, and a variety of apps to enhance your store, it’s designed specifically for online selling.

Export Your Content

Squarespace allows you to export some of your content, but not everything. Here’s how to make the most of it:

  • Go to your Squarespace settings and select 'Advanced', then 'Import/Export'. Familiarize yourself with the export options available.
  • Choose the export format (usually XML for WordPress) that best suits your new platform. Keep in mind that not all content types will transfer, so some manual work will be necessary.
  • Manually copy over any content that doesn’t transfer, like text for images or specific design elements. This is your chance to refresh content as you transition, so take advantage of it!

Set Up Your New Site

Now it’s time to build your new site. If you choose 60 Minute Sites, you’ll benefit from:

  • User-friendly templates tailored for small businesses. You can choose from a variety of designs that suit your brand aesthetics and functional requirements.
  • Customer support to answer any questions during your transition. Their team is available to guide you through the process, ensuring you feel confident every step of the way.
  • Easy integration with tools like social media and email marketing. This feature is crucial for maintaining your existing marketing strategies while enhancing your online presence.

Redirect Your Domain

To ensure visitors can still reach your site, don’t forget to redirect your domain. This step is essential for maintaining your traffic and keeping your brand continuity:

  • Access your domain settings in Squarespace. This is where you’ll manage your domain settings and ensure everything is correctly configured for the new site.
  • Follow their instructions to connect your domain to your new website builder. Each platform will have specific steps, so take your time to ensure accuracy.
  • Check that everything works correctly before the final switch. It’s a good idea to preview your site and test all links to confirm that your users will have a seamless experience.

Switching from Squarespace doesn't have to be overwhelming. With careful planning and the right platform like 60 Minute Sites, you can create a website that truly represents your business and its needs. Embrace this opportunity to enhance your online presence, engage your audience more effectively, and ultimately, drive your business forward. Remember, the journey of building a website is not just about the destination but about crafting an experience that resonates with your brand identity and connects with your customers. Take it one step at a time, and don’t hesitate to reach out for help when you need it!
