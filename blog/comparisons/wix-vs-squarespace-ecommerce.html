Wix vs Squarespace for Ecommerce

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Imagine you're setting up a quaint storefront in the heart of your favorite neighborhood, but instead of bricks and mortar, you’re choosing between two online platforms. Wix and Squarespace are like two charming shops on the same street, each offering unique features for your eCommerce venture. Whether you're selling handmade crafts, vintage clothing, or gourmet foods, the right platform can make all the difference in attracting customers and growing your business.

Wix: A Playground for Creativity

Wix is often celebrated for its flexibility and customization options, making it a great choice for those who want to play around with design. Here’s what you can expect with Wix for eCommerce:

  • Intuitive drag-and-drop editor: This feature allows even the most novice users to create stunning layouts effortlessly. You can move elements around with ease, create custom sections, and see the results in real time.
  • A vast selection of templates: Wix offers hundreds of templates tailored for various industries. If you're running a local bakery, for instance, you can find a template that reflects the warmth and charm of your shop, complete with sections for your menu and customer testimonials.
  • Robust app market: The Wix App Market has over 250 applications that can enhance your eCommerce functionality. For example, you can integrate tools for inventory management, email marketing, or even add a live chat feature to connect with customers directly.
  • Affordable pricing tiers: Wix provides a range of pricing plans, making it accessible for everyone from budding entrepreneurs to established businesses. Their eCommerce plans also come with unlimited bandwidth and storage, which is perfect as your store grows.
  • Built-in SEO tools: With Wix’s SEO Wiz, you can easily optimize your site to rank better in search results. This tool provides customized SEO plans, so you can focus on keywords that matter most to your business and target audience.

Squarespace: Streamlined Elegance

Squarespace, on the other hand, is known for its sleek designs and cohesive aesthetics, ideal for branding. Here’s what makes Squarespace stand out in the eCommerce space:

  • Beautiful, minimalist templates: Squarespace’s templates are designed to be visually stunning and are mobile-optimized right out of the box. This means your online store will look great on any device, helping to enhance the shopping experience for your customers.
  • Strong emphasis on high-quality imagery: For brands that depend on visuals—like fashion boutiques or artisanal crafts—Squarespace’s platform allows you to showcase your products with stunning photography. You can easily create visually appealing galleries and slideshows to captivate visitors.
  • Integrated eCommerce features: Squarespace offers built-in eCommerce tools such as abandoned cart recovery and product reviews. This means you can recover potentially lost sales by sending reminders to customers who didn’t complete their purchases.
  • Comprehensive analytics tools: With Squarespace, you can track your online performance with detailed analytics. You’ll have access to important metrics like sales by product, traffic sources, and conversion rates, allowing you to make informed business decisions.
  • Seamless integration with social media: Squarespace makes it easy to promote your products on social media. You can link your Instagram feed directly to your store, allowing visitors to shop directly from your social posts.

Choosing the Right Fit for Your Business

Deciding between Wix and Squarespace depends largely on your specific needs and brand identity. Consider the following:

  • If you prioritize customization and playful design: Wix may be the way to go. It’s ideal for those who enjoy experimenting with layouts and features to create a unique online presence.
  • If you want a polished and professional look: Squarespace could serve you better. Its templates are designed to be cohesive and elegant, which can help in establishing a strong brand identity from the get-go.
  • Evaluate your budget: Both platforms offer a variety of pricing plans, but you should consider what features are essential for your business. For instance, if you need advanced eCommerce tools, make sure to compare those specific plans.
  • Think about your technical skills: Wix offers more hands-on building, while Squarespace is more straightforward. If you’re someone who enjoys tinkering and learning, Wix could be an exciting playground. Conversely, if you prefer a simpler, guided setup, Squarespace may be more appealing.
  • Look at your target audience: Understanding your audience can significantly impact your choice. If your customers are likely to be drawn in by striking visuals, Squarespace's aesthetic might resonate better. If your audience appreciates creativity and a personal touch, Wix could be the perfect fit.

Both Wix and Squarespace have their strengths when it comes to eCommerce, and your choice should align with your business goals.
