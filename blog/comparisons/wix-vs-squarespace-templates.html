Wix vs Squarespace Templates Comparison

5 min read

Get a Professional Comparisons Website in 60 Minutes →

Here's the thing: choosing the right website builder can feel a bit like picking a favorite child when both are great in their own way. Wix and Squarespace both offer stunning templates that can make your site shine, but they have some key differences that can impact your choice. Let’s dive into the nitty-gritty of Wix vs Squarespace templates so you can make an informed decision. Whether you’re launching a local business in Portland or showcasing your photography portfolio, understanding these platforms will help you create a website that truly reflects your style and objectives.

Template Variety and Design Flexibility

Wix gives you an impressive variety of over 800 templates, catering to almost every type of business. Whether you’re running a trendy café in the heart of the Pearl District or a boutique in Sellwood, you’ll find something that resonates. The customization options are extensive, allowing you to drag and drop elements freely without restrictions. You can adjust colors, fonts, and layouts with ease, making it very appealing for those who enjoy a hands-on approach. For example, if you own a flower shop in the Alberta Arts District, you can easily use a floral template and customize it to showcase your unique arrangements. On the other hand, Squarespace offers around 100 templates that are known for their clean, modern aesthetic. While this may seem limited, each template is meticulously crafted, making it ideal for creatives such as photographers, artists, and designers who value visual storytelling. Here’s a quick comparison:

  • Wix: 800+ templates, extensive design freedom, great for beginners who want to personalize their site.
  • Squarespace: 100+ templates, sleek and professional design, best for creatives looking for elegant, minimalist layouts.

Ease of Use and Customization Options

Navigating the user interface can make or break your website-building experience. Wix provides a very intuitive drag-and-drop builder that feels like playing with digital Lego bricks—perfect for those who want to dive in without a steep learning curve. You can easily add elements like text boxes, images, and videos with just a few clicks. For instance, if you’re a food blogger based in the Hawthorne District, you can quickly create a mouthwatering recipe page without any technical hurdles. In contrast, Squarespace’s interface, while slick, has a slightly steeper learning curve due to its more structured approach. You’ll find that it guides you through the process, which can be beneficial if you prefer a more organized method. However, this might be less appealing if you enjoy the freedom of exploring creative possibilities on your own. Here's how they stack up:

  • Wix: User-friendly interface, easy customization with drag-and-drop; perfect for those who want quick results.
  • Squarespace: More structured layout, great for those who prefer guidance and a more professional workflow.

Mobile Responsiveness and SEO Features

Both platforms understand the importance of mobile responsiveness, especially with so many users accessing websites from their smartphones. Wix ensures that your site looks good on mobile devices, but sometimes the mobile version lacks the flexibility you might want. You might find that certain elements don’t display as intended, which can be frustrating. Squarespace, on the other hand, automatically optimizes your template for mobile, giving you a seamless experience across devices. This is especially valuable if you’re a local business owner in a bustling area like the Pearl District, where customers often search for services on the go. Regarding SEO, both platforms offer basic tools to help your site rank better in search engines, but Squarespace tends to have a slight edge with more built-in features like clean URLs and automatic XML sitemaps. Here's a breakdown:

  • Wix: Mobile-friendly but less optimization control; you might need to manually adjust settings for the best display.
  • Squarespace: Automatic mobile optimization, robust SEO tools, making it easier for customers to find your business online.

Cost and Value for Money

Understanding the price point of each service can help you budget better for your small business needs. Wix offers a free plan with Wix branding and ads, which can be a great way to get started if you're just experimenting with your online presence. However, many find that the premium plans, which range from affordable to higher-end options starting at about $14 per month, offer much more value, especially when you factor in features like a custom domain and additional storage. Squarespace doesn’t have a free tier, but its plans start at $16 per month and include features that many businesses find valuable right off the bat, such as unlimited bandwidth and storage. For a local service provider, investing in a Squarespace plan can pay off quickly with the quality and professionalism it offers. Here’s how it looks:

  • Wix: Free plan available, premium options starting at $14/month; suitable for those looking to test the waters.
  • Squarespace: No free plan, starting at $16/month with comprehensive features that encourage growth.

Deciding between Wix and Squarespace templates depends on your specific needs and design preferences. Whether you're drawn to the wide variety of Wix or the sleek designs of Squarespace, you can find the perfect template to represent your brand.
