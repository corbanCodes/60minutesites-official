How Much Does a Restaurant Website Cost in 2026

7 min read

Restaurant owners researching website costs find wildly different numbers depending on where they look. Quotes range from free DIY tools to custom developments exceeding $10,000. Understanding what drives these differences helps you choose the right option for your restaurant without overspending or ending up with a site that does not serve your needs.

This guide breaks down restaurant website costs by approach, what you get at each price point, and how to determine which investment makes sense for your situation.

Restaurant Website Cost Overview

Restaurant websites fall into four general categories based on how they are built and maintained. Each category involves different upfront costs, ongoing expenses, and trade-offs.

Free DIY Options: $0 Upfront

Several platforms offer free website builders that restaurants can use to create basic sites. Google Sites, Wix free tier, and WordPress.com offer no-cost options. However, free comes with significant limitations.

Free sites typically display ads from the platform, use subdomains that look unprofessional, offer limited design options, and lack features restaurants need like online ordering integration. The hidden cost is your time to build and maintain the site, plus the professional credibility you sacrifice.

Best for: Restaurants that genuinely cannot afford any website investment and need something minimal to establish basic online presence.

DIY Website Builders: $150-$400 per Year

Paid tiers of Squarespace, Wix, and similar platforms remove ads, allow custom domains, and provide more features. Restaurant-specific platforms like BentoBox and ChowNow offer tailored solutions at similar price points.

At this level, you handle setup, design, and ongoing updates yourself. The platform provides templates and tools, but you invest your time in learning the system and maintaining the site. For restaurant owners with limited technical comfort, this can become a significant time burden that takes focus away from running the restaurant.

Typical costs:

  • Platform subscription: $12-35 per month
  • Domain name: $10-20 per year
  • Premium features or apps: $0-50 per month
  • Your time: Significant for setup, moderate for maintenance

Best for: Restaurant owners with time and interest in managing their own website who want more professional results than free options allow.

Template-Based Professional Sites: $500-$1,500 per Year

Services that provide professionally designed templates with setup assistance fall into this middle tier. You get a polished, restaurant-appropriate design without learning website building yourself. Many include hosting, updates, and basic support.

This approach balances cost with quality. You do not get a fully custom design, but you get a professional result that matches what higher-end restaurants have. The time investment is minimal compared to DIY options.

Typical costs:

  • Monthly service fee: $40-125 per month
  • Setup fee: $0-500 one-time
  • Includes: Hosting, SSL, templates, basic support
  • Your time: Minimal for setup and maintenance

Best for: Restaurant owners who want professional results without the time investment of DIY or the cost of custom development.

Custom Website Development: $3,000-$15,000+

Custom development means a designer and developer create a website specifically for your restaurant. Every element is tailored to your brand, and functionality is built to your exact specifications.

Custom sites offer the most professional results but come with corresponding costs. Development typically takes weeks to months. Ongoing maintenance requires either technical skills or continued agency support. Changes and updates often require developer involvement.

Typical costs:

  • Initial development: $3,000-15,000+
  • Hosting: $20-100 per month
  • Maintenance and updates: $50-200 per month
  • Major changes: $500-2,000+ per project

Best for: Established restaurants with strong brands, multiple locations, or unique requirements that template solutions cannot accommodate.

Online Ordering System Costs

If your restaurant offers takeout or delivery, online ordering adds both value and cost. Understanding ordering system pricing helps you budget appropriately.

Third-Party Platforms

Services like DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub charge 15-30 percent commission on each order. While they provide exposure and logistics, these fees add up quickly. A restaurant processing $10,000 monthly in third-party orders might pay $1,500-3,000 in commissions.

Direct Ordering Systems

Alternatives like Square Online, Toast, or ChowNow charge lower fees for orders placed through your own website. Typical costs are $0-100 monthly plus 2-3 percent payment processing. The savings versus third-party commissions often justify dedicated ordering systems for restaurants with significant takeout volume.

Additional Costs to Consider

Beyond the website itself, several related costs factor into your total investment.

Professional Photography

Quality food photography significantly impacts how appetizing your website appears. Professional food photography sessions typically run $300-1,000 depending on your market and the number of dishes photographed. This is often worth the investment, as compelling images drive orders.

Domain Name

Your website address costs $10-50 per year for standard domains. Premium or highly desirable domains can cost significantly more. Many website services include a domain in their pricing.

Menu Updates

If someone else maintains your website, budget for menu update costs. Some services include unlimited updates; others charge per change. Clarify this before committing to any service, as restaurants frequently update menus.

Reservation System Integration

Platforms like OpenTable or Resy charge per-cover fees ($0.25-1.00 per seated diner) or monthly subscriptions ($200-500+). Factor these costs if you plan to integrate reservations into your website.

Calculating Your Return on Investment

Website costs should be evaluated against the business they generate. A $1,000 annual investment that brings in $10,000 in direct orders that would have gone through commission-heavy third parties pays for itself ten times over.

Consider what your website enables:

  • Direct orders without third-party commissions
  • Reservations that fill otherwise empty tables
  • New customers who find you through search
  • Reduced phone time answering questions the website could answer
  • Gift card sales that provide upfront revenue

Even modest improvements in any of these areas can justify reasonable website investments. The question is not whether you can afford a website, but whether you can afford to lose business to competitors with better online presence.

Making the Right Choice for Your Restaurant

Choose your approach based on your specific situation:

New restaurants with tight budgets might start with DIY builders while establishing themselves, then upgrade as revenue allows.

Established restaurants without technical resources benefit from template-based professional services that deliver quality without requiring their time.

High-volume restaurants should prioritize direct online ordering systems that reduce third-party commissions, as the savings quickly justify the investment.

Multi-location or upscale concepts may require custom development to properly represent their brand and handle complex requirements.

Whatever you choose, view your website as an investment in revenue generation rather than just a cost. The right website pays for itself many times over through orders, reservations, and customers it brings through your door.
