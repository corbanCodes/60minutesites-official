How Much Does a Fitness Website Cost in 2026

7 min read

Gym owners evaluating website options face a wide range of pricing, from free DIY builders to custom development projects costing tens of thousands of dollars. Understanding what you get at each price point helps you make an informed decision that balances budget with functionality.

This guide breaks down realistic costs for fitness websites in 2026, covering upfront expenses, ongoing costs, and the hidden fees that can surprise gym owners who did not plan ahead.

Fitness Website Cost Overview

Website costs fall into three main categories: initial setup, ongoing monthly expenses, and optional add-ons. Here is what to expect at each level:

DIY Website Builders: $0-50/month

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and Weebly allow gym owners to build websites themselves using drag-and-drop interfaces. While affordable, this option requires significant time investment and may lack fitness-specific features.

Typical costs:

  • Free tier: Limited features, platform branding, no custom domain
  • Basic paid plans: $15-25/month with custom domain and branding removal
  • Business plans: $25-50/month with e-commerce and booking features

What you get: Basic website functionality, templates, hosting included, and varying levels of customization. Most platforms offer scheduling widget integrations, though setup may require technical knowledge.

What you miss: Fitness-specific design elements, professional appearance, expert guidance, and time. Many gym owners spend 20-40 hours building their first website, time that could go toward training clients or managing operations.

Professional Template Sites: $40-150/month

Template-based solutions designed specifically for fitness businesses offer a middle ground between DIY and custom. These include industry-specific designs with features gym owners actually need.

Typical costs:

  • Entry level: $40-60/month with basic templates and hosting
  • Professional: $60-100/month with more templates, integrations, and support
  • Premium: $100-150/month with advanced features and priority support

What you get: Professional design built for fitness businesses, mobile-responsive layouts, integration points for gym management software, and faster launch times measured in hours rather than weeks.

What you miss: Complete custom design and fully unique layouts. Template sites look professional but share structural similarities with other sites using the same template.

Custom Website Development: $3,000-15,000+

Custom websites are built from scratch by developers and designers specifically for your gym. This option makes sense for established gyms with unique requirements or strong branding needs.

Typical costs:

  • Basic custom: $3,000-5,000 for a simple 5-7 page site
  • Mid-range custom: $5,000-10,000 for more pages and functionality
  • Full custom: $10,000-25,000+ for complex features and integrations

Plus ongoing costs:

  • Hosting: $20-100/month depending on traffic and requirements
  • Maintenance: $100-500/month for updates, security, and changes
  • Domain: $10-20/year

What you get: Completely unique design, exactly the features you specify, full ownership of the code, and a site that looks nothing like any competitor.

What you miss: Speed to launch and budget efficiency. Custom sites take 4-12 weeks to build, and any change requires developer involvement, adding ongoing costs.

Hidden Costs to Consider

The price you see advertised rarely tells the full story. Plan for these additional expenses:

Domain Name: $10-50/year

Your domain (yourgym.com) is a separate purchase from your website. Basic .com domains cost around $10-15/year, though premium domains with desirable names can cost hundreds or thousands.

Professional Email: $5-12/user/month

Email addresses matching your domain (info@yourgym.com) typically require Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or similar services. Most gyms need at least 2-3 email accounts.

Gym Management Software Integration

Your website likely needs to connect with Mindbody, Zen Planner, Wodify, or similar software. Integration complexity varies:

  • Simple embed: Often free but limited in appearance
  • Styled integration: May require custom work or premium plans
  • Full API integration: Could add $500-2,000+ to custom development

Photography

Professional photos of your facility, trainers, and members in action dramatically improve your website. Budget $300-1,000 for a professional photo session, or invest time in taking quality photos yourself.

Ongoing Updates and Changes

Websites are not "set and forget." Budget time or money for:

  • Schedule changes and updates
  • New trainer additions or departures
  • Pricing updates
  • New program launches
  • Security updates and maintenance

With DIY and template solutions, you handle these yourself. With custom sites, changes often require developer involvement at $50-150/hour.

Cost Comparison: First Year Total

Here is what each option typically costs for the first year of operation:

DIY website builder (business plan):

  • Platform: $300-600/year
  • Domain: $15/year
  • Your time: 20-40 hours (value that yourself)
  • Total: $315-615 plus your time

Professional template site:

  • Platform: $500-1,800/year
  • Domain: $15/year (often included)
  • Setup time: 2-8 hours
  • Total: $500-1,800

Custom website:

  • Development: $5,000-15,000
  • Hosting: $240-1,200/year
  • Maintenance: $1,200-6,000/year
  • Domain: $15/year
  • Total: $6,455-22,215 first year

Which Option Is Right for Your Gym

The right choice depends on your specific situation:

Choose DIY if: You have more time than money, enjoy technical projects, have simple website needs, or are testing a new gym concept before investing in a professional presence.

Choose professional templates if: You want a professional appearance without custom development costs, need fitness-specific features, value your time, and want to launch quickly with minimal technical involvement.

Choose custom development if: You have an established gym with strong branding requirements, need unique functionality that templates cannot provide, have budget for ongoing development costs, and can wait 4-12 weeks for launch.

The Real Question: Return on Investment

Website cost matters less than what that investment returns. A $100/month website that brings in two new members each month pays for itself many times over. A $10,000 custom site that does not convert visitors is an expensive failure.

Focus on getting a professional, functional website live quickly rather than pursuing perfection. You can always upgrade later once your membership revenue supports a larger investment. The most expensive website mistake is not having an effective online presence while your competitors capture potential members searching for gyms in your area.
