Essential Pages Every Real Estate Agent Website Needs

8 min read

A real estate website needs more than a homepage and contact form. Buyers and sellers have different needs. Local expertise must be demonstrated. Lead capture opportunities require strategic placement. Understanding which pages to include and what purpose each serves helps you build a complete online presence.

This guide covers the essential pages every real estate agent website should have, what content belongs on each, and how they work together to serve visitors and generate leads.

Core Pages Every Agent Needs

These pages form the foundation of any real estate website. Without them, your site is incomplete.

Homepage

Your homepage is the digital equivalent of your listing's curb appeal. Most visitors land here first, and you have seconds to convince them to explore further.

An effective real estate homepage includes a clear statement of who you are and who you serve, your primary service areas, quick access to property search, featured or recent listings, trust indicators like credentials and transaction volume, clear calls-to-action for buyers and sellers, and prominent contact information.

Keep your homepage focused. It should direct visitors to the content they need, not overwhelm them with everything at once. Think of it as a navigation hub that establishes credibility and guides visitors deeper into your site.

About Page

Real estate is personal. Clients want to know who will guide them through major financial decisions. Your about page should build genuine connection while establishing professional credibility.

Include your real estate background and what drew you to the industry, your approach to working with clients, areas of expertise and specialization, credentials, designations, and achievements, personal elements that humanize your professional profile, community involvement and local connections, and a professional photo that appears current and approachable.

Write in first person. Let your personality come through. The goal is for visitors to feel like they already know you before making contact.

Property Listings Page

Visitors expect to find property content on a real estate website. How you present listings depends on your approach and budget.

With IDX integration, you can offer full MLS search functionality directly on your site. Without IDX, you can feature your own active and sold listings, linking to portals for broader search capabilities.

Regardless of approach, your listings page should be easy to navigate, mobile-friendly, and include clear contact CTAs on every listing. Showcase sold properties to demonstrate your track record.

Contact Page

When visitors decide to reach out, the contact page should make it effortless. Include multiple contact methods: phone, email, text, and a contact form. If you have an office, include the address and hours. State your typical response time. Let visitors know what to expect after they make contact.

The contact form should capture enough information to understand the inquiry without being so long that visitors abandon it. Name, contact info, whether they are buying or selling, and a message field usually suffice.

Buyer-Focused Pages

Dedicated content for buyers demonstrates that you understand their needs and are prepared to help them navigate the purchase process.

Buyer Resources Hub

Create a landing page that organizes all buyer-related content. This page serves as a navigation point to more detailed resources while providing value in itself.

Include an overview of the buying process, links to detailed guides, access to property search, buyer consultation scheduling, and downloadable resources or guides.

Home Buying Process Guide

Walk potential buyers through the purchase process step by step. Cover getting pre-approved for financing, defining search criteria and priorities, the property search and showing process, making offers and negotiations, the inspection and due diligence period, closing process and final walkthrough, and what happens after closing.

This content provides genuine value while keeping buyers on your site and establishing your expertise.

First-Time Buyer Page

First-time buyers have unique concerns and questions. A dedicated page addressing their specific situation shows you understand their needs. Cover common first-time buyer fears and questions, special programs and incentives available, what to expect throughout the process, and how you help first-time buyers specifically.

Seller-Focused Pages

Sellers also need dedicated content that addresses their concerns and demonstrates your ability to market and sell properties effectively.

Seller Resources Hub

Similar to the buyer hub, create a landing page that organizes seller content. Include an overview of the selling process, links to detailed guides, home valuation request, listing consultation scheduling, and information about your marketing approach.

Home Selling Process Guide

Explain what sellers should expect from listing to closing. Cover preparing the home for sale, pricing strategy and market analysis, marketing and showing process, receiving and negotiating offers, managing contingencies and inspections, and the closing process.

Home Valuation Page

Offer sellers a way to get an estimate of their home's value. This is a powerful lead capture tool. Include a form requesting property details and contact information. Explain what they will receive (CMA report, consultation call) and when they will receive it.

Be clear about what the valuation involves. Automated estimates have limitations; a comparative market analysis from an agent is more accurate but requires more information.

Local Area Pages

Demonstrating local expertise is crucial for real estate agents. Area-focused content serves both visitors and search engines.

Neighborhood and Community Guides

Create dedicated pages for each neighborhood or community in your primary service area. Include housing stock and architectural styles, price ranges and market conditions, school information and ratings, walkability and transportation options, dining, shopping, and entertainment, parks and recreation, community character and demographics, and what makes the area unique.

These pages provide genuine value to relocating buyers while helping you rank in local search results.

Service Area Overview

If you serve multiple areas, create an overview page that lists all the neighborhoods and communities you cover. Brief descriptions of each area with links to detailed guides help visitors navigate to relevant content.

Market Reports

Regular market updates demonstrate ongoing engagement with local real estate conditions. Monthly or quarterly reports covering sales volume, median prices, inventory levels, and market trends establish you as an informed local expert.

These can be blog posts, downloadable PDFs, or dedicated pages updated regularly.

Trust-Building Pages

Beyond content pages, certain elements build credibility throughout your site.

Sold Properties Gallery

A dedicated page showcasing your successfully closed transactions demonstrates your track record. Include property photos, sale prices (where permitted), neighborhoods, and brief descriptions. Recent solds are most relevant, but a history of transactions shows sustained success.

Reviews and Client Feedback

If you have client feedback to share, a testimonials page consolidates social proof. Link to your Google Business Profile and other platforms where visitors can read unfiltered reviews. Third-party validation is more credible than curated quotes.

Optional But Valuable Pages

Depending on your business model and resources, these additional pages can strengthen your site.

Blog or Market Updates

Regular content updates help with SEO and demonstrate ongoing expertise. If you commit to blogging, post consistently. Topics might include market analysis, buyer and seller tips, neighborhood spotlights, and real estate news commentary.

Only add a blog if you will maintain it. An abandoned blog with posts from years ago looks worse than no blog at all.

Open Houses Calendar

If you hold regular open houses, a dedicated page listing upcoming events makes your site a destination for buyers actively searching. Include dates, times, addresses, and property highlights.

Team Page

If you work with a team, introduce each member with photos, bios, and contact information. Help visitors understand team roles and who they might work with.

Page Planning and Priorities

If you are building a new site or rebuilding an existing one, prioritize pages based on impact:

Start with essentials: Homepage, about, listings, and contact. These form your minimum viable website.

Add client resources: Buyer and seller pages that provide value and capture leads.

Build local content: Neighborhood guides that demonstrate expertise and boost local SEO.

Expand as resources allow: Blog, market reports, and other content that requires ongoing maintenance.

Each page should serve a clear purpose. If you cannot articulate why a page exists and how it helps visitors or generates leads, question whether you need it.
