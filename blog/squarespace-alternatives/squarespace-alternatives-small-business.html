Squarespace Alternatives

Best Squarespace Alternatives for Small Business in 2026

10 min read January 2026

Squarespace has earned a strong reputation among small business owners looking to build their own websites. The beautiful templates and relatively straightforward interface make it an obvious choice for many. But after working with the platform, some business owners realize it is not quite the right fit. Maybe the time investment is more than expected. Maybe the costs add up faster than anticipated. Maybe specific business needs are not well served by the platform.

This guide is for small business owners specifically. Not enterprise companies with IT departments. Not tech startups with developer teams. Regular small businesses that need an effective website without spending a fortune or becoming web design experts.

What Small Businesses Actually Need

Before comparing alternatives, let us clarify what most small businesses genuinely need from a website. Understanding this helps filter out options that are overkill or inadequate.

Most small businesses need:

  • Professional appearance that builds credibility
  • Clear presentation of services or products
  • Easy way for customers to make contact
  • Basic information like hours, location, and policies
  • Mobile-friendly design since most visitors use phones
  • Fast loading speeds so visitors do not leave
  • Basic search visibility so people can find you

Most small businesses do not need:

  • Complex custom functionality
  • Advanced e-commerce with hundreds of products
  • Membership portals with gated content
  • Highly customized designs that look like nothing else
  • Integration with dozens of third-party tools

If your needs are in the first list, you have many good options. If you need items from the second list, your options narrow and costs increase.

Why Small Businesses Leave Squarespace

Understanding common exit reasons helps you evaluate whether alternatives address those issues.

Time Drain

Small business owners wear many hats. Adding "website designer" to the list often proves unsustainable. The initial build takes longer than expected, and ongoing maintenance, content updates, and troubleshooting continue indefinitely. Many business owners eventually decide their time is better spent elsewhere.

Cost Creep

Squarespace's base prices look reasonable, but feature requirements push you to higher tiers. Adding scheduling tools, email marketing, and other functionality adds more cost. When you honestly total everything, the "affordable" platform may cost more than alternatives.

Design Frustration

Squarespace templates are beautiful, but customizing them to match specific visions can be difficult. When the template does not quite allow what you want, the workarounds become tedious. Some users find themselves fighting the platform more than working with it.

Results Disappointment

After investing significant time, some business owners are not happy with what they created. The site looks acceptable but not truly professional. Design elements do not quite work. The overall impression falls short of competitors with professionally built sites.

Alternative 1: Wix

Wix is the most obvious alternative since it occupies similar market space. If you like the concept of building your own website but want a different tool, Wix offers a valid option.

Advantages for Small Business

Wix provides more creative freedom than Squarespace. The drag-and-drop editor lets you place elements anywhere rather than within template constraints. For business owners with specific layout ideas, this flexibility helps.

The Wix App Market offers hundreds of integrations. Whatever tools your business uses, Wix likely connects to them. This ecosystem extends functionality without custom development.

Wix also offers phone support, which Squarespace does not. When problems arise and you prefer talking to a person, this matters.

Disadvantages for Small Business

More creative freedom also means more opportunity for design mistakes. Squarespace's constraints prevent certain problems that Wix allows. Without design experience, the freedom can backfire.

Once you choose a Wix template, you cannot switch to another without rebuilding your site. This matches Squarespace's limitation. Plan your template choice carefully.

Wix sites have sometimes been criticized for slower performance, though this has improved. Test your site speed after building.

Best For

Small business owners who enjoy the DIY process, want more creative control than Squarespace offers, and have time to invest in learning and building.

Alternative 2: WordPress

WordPress powers over 40% of websites globally. Its flexibility is unmatched, but that flexibility comes with complexity that many small businesses do not need.

Advantages for Small Business

WordPress can become virtually any type of website. If your needs grow significantly, the platform can accommodate them. You are never locked into limitations.

You own your website completely. The files can be moved between hosts. You are not dependent on any single company's continued service.

The plugin ecosystem is massive. Whatever functionality you need, a plugin probably exists. Many are free or inexpensive.

Disadvantages for Small Business

WordPress requires separate hosting, which you must select, pay for, and occasionally deal with. This adds complexity compared to all-in-one platforms.

Security and maintenance become your responsibility. WordPress sites require updates to prevent vulnerabilities. Without attention, sites can be hacked or break.

The learning curve is steeper. Building an effective WordPress site typically requires more knowledge than Squarespace, unless you hire help.

Best For

Small business owners who want complete ownership and flexibility, are willing to learn or hire someone for setup, and can commit to ongoing maintenance.

Alternative 3: Shopify

If your small business sells products, Shopify deserves consideration even though it is primarily an e-commerce platform.

Advantages for Small Business

Shopify handles e-commerce better than general website builders. Inventory management, payment processing, shipping calculations, and tax handling are built in and well-designed.

The platform scales seamlessly. Whether you sell ten products or ten thousand, Shopify can handle it without requiring migration to a more robust solution.

POS integration allows using Shopify for in-person sales too, unifying your online and offline business.

Disadvantages for Small Business

If you do not sell products, Shopify is not the right choice. You can build non-e-commerce pages, but the platform is designed for selling.

Transaction fees apply unless you use Shopify Payments. These reduce your margins on every sale.

The app ecosystem, while extensive, can add significant cost. Essential functionality often requires paid apps.

Best For

Product-based small businesses where online selling is the primary or significant website purpose.

Alternative 4: Done-For-You Services

Done-for-you website services represent a fundamentally different approach. Rather than providing tools for you to build, professionals handle everything.

Advantages for Small Business

Time savings are substantial. Instead of spending 20-60 hours learning a platform and building a site, you spend a few hours providing information and reviewing work. For busy small business owners, this difference is decisive.

Professional results come standard. People who build websites daily know design principles, user experience best practices, and technical requirements. The finished product typically exceeds what DIY efforts produce.

Ongoing maintenance is usually included. When you need changes, someone else handles them. When something breaks, it is not your problem to diagnose.

Disadvantages for Small Business

You have less direct control. Changes go through a process rather than happening immediately when you think of them. For some business owners, this feels limiting.

Monthly costs may exceed DIY platform fees, though when you honestly account for your time investment, the math often favors done-for-you services.

Best For

Small business owners whose time has significant value, who prefer professional results, and who would rather run their business than learn web design.

Alternative 5: Simple Single-Page Solutions

Some small businesses do not need a full multi-page website. A well-designed single page with essential information may suffice.

Advantages for Small Business

Simplicity. One page means fewer decisions, faster building, and easier maintenance. Visitors find information quickly without navigating.

Cost can be minimal. Some single-page options are free or very inexpensive since the scope is limited.

Mobile experience is often better. A single well-designed page scrolls naturally on phones without complex navigation.

Disadvantages for Small Business

SEO is limited. Search engines prefer more content to understand what your business offers. A single page limits what you can rank for.

Business growth may require starting over. If your needs expand, a single page cannot accommodate them without fundamental changes.

Best For

Very small businesses, freelancers, or those with simple needs who want a basic professional presence quickly and inexpensively.

Making Your Decision

Consider these questions honestly:

How much time can you realistically spend? Any DIY platform requires hours initially and ongoing maintenance. If you are already stretched thin, done-for-you services may be the practical choice regardless of their cost.

What does your time cost? Calculate honestly. If you bill $75 per hour and spend 40 hours building a website, you have invested $3,000 worth of time. Compare this to alternatives.

Do you enjoy this type of work? Some people genuinely like building websites. Others dread it. Working within your preferences produces better results than fighting them.

What are your actual needs? Resist the temptation to choose platforms based on features you might need someday. Choose based on what you need now, with reasonable growth capacity.

How important is the outcome? For some businesses, the website is a minor touchpoint. For others, it is central to acquiring customers. Investment should match importance.

WordPress offers power but demands more from you. Unless you specifically need its flexibility, the added complexity may not serve small business needs well.
