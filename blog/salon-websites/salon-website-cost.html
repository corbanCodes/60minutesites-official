How Much Does a Salon Website Cost in 2026

7 min read

Salon owners asking about website costs often receive frustratingly vague answers. "It depends" does not help you budget. This guide provides realistic price ranges for different approaches to salon websites in 2026, so you can make an informed decision based on your budget and needs.

The total cost of a salon website includes initial setup, ongoing hosting, domain registration, and potentially booking software integration. Understanding all these components helps you compare options accurately.

Website Cost Options Overview

Salon websites fall into three main categories, each with different cost structures, time investments, and results.

DIY Website Builders

Initial cost: $0-200
Monthly cost: $12-50
Time investment: 10-40 hours

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and GoDaddy Website Builder allow you to create a website yourself using drag-and-drop interfaces. The platforms provide templates, hosting, and basic functionality in one package.

The financial cost is low, but the time cost is significant. Building a professional-looking website takes longer than marketing suggests, especially if you have no design experience. You will spend hours learning the platform, choosing and customizing templates, writing content, and troubleshooting issues.

Results vary widely depending on your design sense and patience. Some salon owners create attractive, functional sites. Others end up with something that looks homemade and unprofessional despite hours of effort.

Professional Template-Based Sites

Initial cost: $0-500
Monthly cost: $40-150
Time investment: 1-5 hours

This approach uses professionally designed templates built specifically for salons. You provide your content and branding; the template provides the design and structure. Some services include setup assistance or handle the entire process for you.

Template-based solutions strike a balance between cost and quality. You get professional design without paying for custom development. The main limitation is that your site will share a design foundation with other salons using the same template, though customization with your branding, colors, and imagery makes each site distinct.

This option works well for salons that want professional results without the time investment of DIY or the budget for custom development.

Custom Website Development

Initial cost: $2,000-10,000+
Monthly cost: $50-200 (hosting and maintenance)
Time investment: 10-20 hours (consultation and review)

Custom development means hiring a designer or agency to build a website specifically for your salon. Every element is created from scratch based on your brand, needs, and preferences.

Custom sites offer the most flexibility and can be truly unique. However, the cost is substantial, and the process takes weeks or months. For most single-location salons, this level of investment is difficult to justify when template options can achieve similar results.

Custom development makes sense for established salons with strong brands, multiple locations, or specific functionality requirements that templates cannot accommodate.

Additional Cost Factors

Beyond the website itself, several other costs factor into your total investment.

Domain Name

Cost: $10-50 per year

Your domain name (yoursalon.com) requires annual registration. Common extensions like .com typically cost $10-15 per year. Premium or short domain names may cost more if purchasing from someone who already owns them.

Some website services include a free domain for the first year, but check renewal costs. The domain you choose becomes your online identity, so consider it a small but important investment.

Professional Email

Cost: $0-6 per user per month

Email addresses matching your domain (you@yoursalon.com) look more professional than generic Gmail addresses. Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 offer business email starting around $6 per user per month. Some hosting plans include email at no additional cost.

Online Booking Platform

Cost: $0-100+ per month

Booking platforms like Vagaro, Square Appointments, Fresha, and Booksy charge various amounts based on features and number of users. Some offer free tiers with basic functionality. Others charge per staff member or take a percentage of bookings.

Consider booking platform costs separately from website costs, as you need this functionality regardless of which website approach you choose.

Photography

Cost: $0-500+

Your website needs quality images of your work, space, and team. You can build a portfolio using smartphone photos if you establish consistent standards for lighting and composition. Professional photography provides better results but adds cost.

Consider photography an ongoing investment rather than a one-time expense. Continuously documenting your best work keeps your portfolio current and compelling.

Calculating Your Total Investment

When comparing website options, calculate the full first-year cost including all components:

DIY approach total: $150-800 first year, including platform subscription, domain, and email. Does not include the value of your time.

Template approach total: $500-2,000 first year, including template service, domain, email, and any setup fees.

Custom development total: $2,500-12,000 first year, including development, hosting, domain, email, and potential maintenance retainer.

What You Get at Each Price Point

Understanding what each approach provides helps you decide which fits your situation.

Free to $500

At this level, you are doing most of the work yourself using a website builder. You get functional but basic designs, limited customization, and varying quality depending on your skills. Expect to spend significant time learning and building.

$500 to $2,000

This range typically gets you a professional template customized with your content and branding. Design quality is high because templates are created by professionals. Setup may be included or available for a fee. Results look polished with less personal time investment.

$2,000 to $5,000

This budget allows for semi-custom development with professional design and more flexibility. You might get custom modifications to existing templates or simpler custom designs. Results are professional and more distinctive.

$5,000 and Above

Full custom development with unique design, custom functionality, and extensive consultation. Results can be exceptional, but the investment must be justified by business volume or specific requirements.

Making the Right Choice

The right investment level depends on your salon's situation.

New salons or tight budgets should consider DIY or basic template options. Get online with something professional enough to build credibility while you establish your business. You can upgrade later as revenue grows.

Established salons with steady business benefit from professional template solutions. The improved appearance and functionality justify the moderate investment, and you avoid the time sink of building it yourself.

Premium salons or multiple locations may justify custom development if their brand positioning demands uniqueness or their operations require custom functionality.

Remember that a website is an investment in client acquisition. Even modest improvements in conversion rate quickly pay for the website cost. One additional client per month easily justifies a $100 monthly investment in most markets.
