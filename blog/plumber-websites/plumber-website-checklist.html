Plumber Website Checklist: 12 Elements That Get Calls

8 min read

Most plumbing company owners understand they need a website, but knowing what separates an effective site from one that just exists is another matter entirely. A plumbing website that actually generates calls requires specific elements that generic business sites often overlook.

This checklist covers the 12 elements that distinguish plumbing websites that generate business from those that simply take up space on the internet. Use it to evaluate your current site or plan a new one that actually delivers results.

The Complete Plumber Website Checklist

Work through each item systematically and assess where your current website stands. Implementing even half of these elements will position you ahead of most competitors in your service area.

1. Phone Number Visible on Every Page

This sounds obvious, but countless plumbing websites bury their phone numbers or hide them on a contact page. Your phone number should appear in the header of every single page, sized large enough to read at a glance. On mobile devices, it must be tap-to-call enabled so visitors can call with one touch.

When someone has water flooding their basement, they do not have time to hunt through your website looking for how to reach you. Make your phone number impossible to miss.

2. Emergency Service Information Above the Fold

If you offer emergency plumbing services, this fact should be immediately visible when someone lands on your homepage. Do not make visitors scroll to discover whether you can help them at 3 AM. Include:

  • Clear statement that you offer 24/7 emergency service (if you do)
  • Emergency phone number prominently displayed
  • Response time information
  • Types of emergencies you handle

Emergency calls are often your most profitable work. Make sure visitors know immediately that you can help when crisis strikes.

3. Service Area Clearly Defined

Plumbing is inherently local. Visitors need to quickly confirm that you serve their location before investing any more time on your site. State your service area clearly on your homepage and consider a dedicated service area page that lists:

  • All cities and towns you serve
  • Neighborhoods within larger cities
  • Zip codes if relevant
  • Any variations in service (response times, pricing) by area

Clear service area information prevents wasted time for both you and potential customers outside your coverage zone.

4. Complete List of Services

Detail every plumbing service you provide. Be specific rather than vague. Instead of just listing "plumbing services," break it down into categories:

  • Emergency services (burst pipes, sewage backup, no hot water)
  • Drain services (cleaning, repair, camera inspection)
  • Water heater services (repair, replacement, tankless)
  • Fixture installation and repair
  • Pipe repair, replacement, and repiping
  • Sewer line services
  • Water treatment and filtration

Someone searching for "tankless water heater installation" wants to see that specific service mentioned, not guess whether it falls under your general offerings.

5. License and Insurance Display

Legitimate plumbing customers care about hiring licensed, insured professionals. Display your plumbing license number prominently on your website. Include:

  • License number and type
  • Issuing authority
  • Statement confirming liability insurance
  • Workers compensation coverage mention

This information answers questions that informed homeowners will have. Displaying it prominently signals that you operate a legitimate, professional business.

6. Mobile-Responsive Design

More than half of plumbing-related searches happen on mobile devices, and that percentage climbs even higher for emergency searches. Your website must work flawlessly on phones:

  • Text readable without zooming
  • Buttons large enough to tap easily
  • Navigation that works on small screens
  • Forms that function properly on mobile
  • Fast loading on cellular networks

Test your website on multiple phones and tablets. If it is difficult to use on mobile, you are losing calls to competitors with better mobile experiences.

7. Fast Loading Speed

Every second your website takes to load increases the chance that a potential customer will abandon it and call someone else. Plumbing customers, especially those with emergencies, have zero patience for slow websites. Ensure your site loads in under three seconds by:

  • Compressing images before uploading
  • Minimizing unnecessary scripts and plugins
  • Using a reliable hosting provider
  • Enabling browser caching

Test your site speed using Google PageSpeed Insights and address any issues identified.

8. Contact Form That Qualifies Leads

Not everyone wants to call, and some inquiries come outside business hours. A contact form captures these leads. Keep it concise but collect enough information to understand the customer's needs:

  • Name and contact information
  • Service type needed
  • Property location
  • Brief problem description
  • Urgency level

Respond to form submissions quickly. A lead that waits hours for a response often calls another plumber in the meantime.

9. About Page with Credentials

Homeowners let plumbers into their homes. They want to know who they are dealing with. Your about page should build confidence by covering:

  • Company history and founding story
  • Owner and key team member backgrounds
  • Years of experience
  • Training and certifications
  • Your approach to customer service

Keep it professional but personable. Customers hire competent professionals, but they also want to feel comfortable with whoever shows up at their door.

10. Clear Pricing Information

You cannot quote exact prices without seeing a job, but you can provide pricing guidance that builds trust:

  • Service call or diagnostic fee
  • Price ranges for common services
  • Your estimate process
  • Any emergency or after-hours rates
  • Payment options accepted

Transparency about pricing addresses customer concerns about being overcharged, especially during emergencies.

11. Reviews and Trust Signals

Social proof influences purchasing decisions. Make it easy for visitors to see that others have had positive experiences with your company:

  • Link to your Google Business Profile reviews
  • Display overall rating and review count
  • Include select written testimonials
  • Show industry association memberships
  • Display years in business

Authentic reviews from real customers carry more weight than any marketing copy you could write.

12. Clear Calls-to-Action

Every page on your website should have a clear next step for visitors. Do not use generic "Contact Us" buttons when you can be specific:

  • "Call Now for Emergency Service"
  • "Schedule Drain Cleaning"
  • "Get a Free Water Heater Quote"
  • "Book Your Service Appointment"

Specific calls-to-action tell visitors exactly what happens when they click and reduce friction in the conversion process.

Prioritizing Your Website Improvements

If your current website is missing multiple elements from this checklist, prioritize based on their impact on lead generation:

Fix first: Phone number visibility, emergency information, mobile responsiveness. These directly affect your ability to receive calls from potential customers.

Address next: Service area definition, complete service list, license display. These build the trust needed to convert visitors into leads.

Then improve: Page speed, contact form, about page content. These enhance visitor experience and support conversions.

Finally optimize: Reviews integration, pricing information, refined CTAs. These maximize conversion rates once the foundation is solid.

Taking Action on This Checklist

Review your current plumbing website against this checklist. Score each element as present, needs improvement, or missing. Create a prioritized list of fixes and work through them systematically.

A plumbing website that includes all 12 elements will outperform the vast majority of competitor sites that treat their online presence as an afterthought. Every element on this list exists because it directly contributes to turning website visitors into paying customers.
