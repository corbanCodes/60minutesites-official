Essential Pages Every Plumbing Website Needs

8 min read

A plumbing website needs more than just a homepage and a contact page to generate consistent leads. The right page structure helps visitors find information about their specific needs, builds trust in your company, and guides them toward contacting you. Understanding which pages matter and what each should include is essential for website effectiveness.

This guide covers every page your plumbing website should have, from the absolute necessities to pages that support growth and search visibility.

The Core Pages Every Plumbing Website Needs

These pages form the foundation of your website. Without them, you are missing essential opportunities to connect with potential customers.

Homepage

Your homepage is the front door of your online presence. Most visitors land here first, and it must accomplish several goals simultaneously:

Essential elements:

  • Company name and clear identification as a plumbing company
  • Phone number prominently displayed, tap-to-call on mobile
  • Service area identification
  • Emergency service availability (if applicable)
  • Overview of primary services
  • Trust indicators (license, insurance, years in business)
  • Clear call-to-action for scheduling or inquiries

The homepage should answer visitor questions before they think to ask: What do you do? Where do you work? Can you help me? How do I reach you? All of this should be clear within seconds of landing on the page.

Services Page

A comprehensive services page lets visitors confirm you handle their specific plumbing needs. Structure it by category for easy scanning:

Common service categories:

  • Emergency plumbing services
  • Drain and sewer services
  • Water heater services
  • Pipe repair and replacement
  • Fixture installation and repair
  • Water treatment and filtration
  • Commercial plumbing (if offered)

Each category should briefly describe what it includes with links to more detailed individual service pages if you have them.

About Page

Homeowners let plumbers into their homes and trust them with expensive repairs. Your about page builds the confidence needed for that trust:

Content to include:

  • Company history and founding story
  • Owner background and credentials
  • Team information (size, experience, training)
  • Licenses and certifications
  • Insurance coverage statement
  • Company values and service philosophy
  • Community involvement if applicable

Keep the tone professional but personable. You are building a connection with potential customers who want to feel comfortable with whoever shows up at their door.

Contact Page

Make reaching you as frictionless as possible. Your contact page should include multiple ways to connect:

Required elements:

  • Phone number (primary method for most plumbing customers)
  • Contact form for non-urgent inquiries
  • Email address
  • Physical address if you have a shop location
  • Business hours
  • Emergency contact information if different from main number
  • Service area reminder

Your contact form should capture enough information to qualify leads: name, contact info, service type needed, and a brief problem description.

Service Area Page

Plumbing is local. Clearly communicate where you work to help visitors quickly confirm you serve their location:

Content to include:

  • List of cities and towns served
  • Neighborhoods within larger cities if applicable
  • Counties or regions served
  • Any service limitations by area (response times, pricing differences)
  • Map showing coverage area (optional but helpful)

This page also supports local SEO by associating your business with specific geographic terms.

Individual Service Pages

Beyond a general services page, individual pages for major service categories improve both user experience and search rankings. Consider separate pages for:

Emergency Plumbing Services

If you offer emergency services, dedicate a page to them. This page should emphasize:

  • Types of emergencies you handle
  • Availability (24/7, after-hours, weekends)
  • Response time expectations
  • Emergency contact number (prominently displayed)
  • What to do while waiting for help
  • Service area for emergency calls

Drain and Sewer Services

Cover the full scope of drain-related services:

  • Drain cleaning and unclogging
  • Sewer line repair and replacement
  • Camera inspection services
  • Hydro jetting
  • Root intrusion solutions
  • Signs of drain problems to watch for

Water Heater Services

Water heaters are a significant service category deserving focused content:

  • Water heater repair
  • Tank water heater replacement
  • Tankless water heater installation
  • Maintenance services
  • Signs of water heater problems
  • Guidance on when to repair versus replace

Pipe Services

Pipe-related services often represent significant work:

  • Leak detection and repair
  • Pipe replacement
  • Repiping services
  • Frozen pipe prevention and repair
  • Gas line services if licensed

Fixture Installation

New fixtures represent both scheduled work and opportunities:

  • Toilet installation and repair
  • Faucet installation and repair
  • Garbage disposal installation
  • Sink installation
  • Shower and tub installation

Supporting Pages That Strengthen Your Site

Beyond the core pages, additional pages can improve user experience, support SEO, and address common customer questions.

FAQ Page

A frequently asked questions page addresses common customer concerns and reduces friction:

  • Pricing and payment questions
  • Scheduling and availability
  • Emergency service policies
  • Service guarantees
  • What to expect during service calls
  • DIY versus professional guidance

FAQs also support SEO by naturally incorporating questions people actually search for.

Pricing Page or Section

While exact quotes require assessment, pricing guidance builds trust:

  • Service call or diagnostic fees
  • Price ranges for common services
  • Factors that affect pricing
  • Payment options and financing if offered
  • Free estimate policy

Individual City or Area Pages

If you serve multiple distinct areas, individual pages for each can improve local search visibility:

  • Services offered in that location
  • Response times for that area
  • Any location-specific information
  • Local contact or dispatch information if applicable

These pages must contain unique, valuable content rather than just swapping city names. Search engines penalize duplicate content.

Reviews or Testimonials Page

While reviews should appear throughout your site, a dedicated page consolidates social proof:

  • Select written testimonials
  • Links to review profiles (Google, Yelp)
  • Overall rating display
  • Instructions for leaving reviews

Page Structure Best Practices

Regardless of which pages you include, follow these structural guidelines:

Consistent Navigation

Every page should include consistent header navigation and footer information. Visitors should always be able to find your phone number, access main pages, and understand where they are on your site.

Clear Calls-to-Action

Every page should have a next step for visitors. Service pages should encourage scheduling. Information pages should guide toward contact. Never leave visitors wondering what to do next.

Mobile-Friendly Design

All pages must work well on mobile devices. Test every page on phones to ensure readability, navigation, and functionality work properly on small screens.

Fast Loading

Optimize all pages for speed. Compress images, minimize unnecessary scripts, and ensure hosting can handle traffic. Slow pages lose visitors regardless of their content quality.

Building Your Page Structure

Start with the core five pages: homepage, services, about, contact, and service area. These establish a functional foundation for generating leads.

Add individual service pages as time allows, prioritizing your most requested services or those with the highest value. Each new page expands your ability to rank for specific search terms and address specific customer needs.

Supporting pages like FAQs and city-specific content can be added over time to strengthen your site further. The key is starting with a solid foundation and building from there.
