Best Practices

What Makes a Great Gym Website

Fitness websites that drive memberships share these essential elements.

Class Schedule Display

Easy-to-read class schedules organized by day, time, or instructor. Members can quickly find and plan their workouts for the week.

Membership Signup

Streamlined signup forms that capture leads 24/7. Remove friction from joining and let potential members sign up anytime.

Trainer Profiles

Showcase your training team with bios, certifications, and specialties. Help members find the right trainer for their goals.

Facility Showcase

High-quality photos of your equipment, studios, locker rooms, and amenities. Let visitors virtually tour your gym before visiting.

Mobile Responsive

Members check class times on their phones constantly. Your site must look and work great on every device.

Trial/Promo Offers

Prominently display free trials, first-month specials, or promotional rates. Give visitors a low-risk way to try your gym.

Fitness Website Questions

A gym website should include a homepage showcasing your facility and unique value, a class schedule page, membership options with pricing, trainer profiles with bios and certifications, a photo gallery or virtual tour of your facility, and a contact page with location and hours. Additional pages for specific programs (personal training, group fitness, nutrition coaching), success stories, and a blog can help attract and convert more visitors.

Our fitness templates include built-in class schedule displays that are easy to update. You can organize classes by day of the week, class type (yoga, HIIT, spin, etc.), or instructor. The schedules are mobile-friendly so members can quickly check times on their phones. We can also integrate with booking systems if you use one.

At minimum, include lead capture forms so interested visitors can request information or schedule a tour. Our templates include forms that integrate with email marketing and CRM systems.

Create individual trainer profile pages with professional photos, bios, certifications (NASM, ACE, etc.), specialties, and training philosophies. Include their experience working with different types of clients and any success stories. This helps potential members find trainers whose style and expertise match their fitness goals.

Include high-quality photos of your main workout areas, cardio equipment, free weights section, group fitness studios, locker rooms, and any unique amenities (pool, sauna, juice bar). Action shots of classes in session and trainer-client interactions work well. Avoid stock photos when possible. Authentic images of your actual facility build trust with potential members.
