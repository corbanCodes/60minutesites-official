Best Practices

What Makes a Great Photography Website

Photography websites that book clients share these essential elements.

Portfolio Gallery

Showcase your best work in a stunning gallery organized by category. Weddings, portraits, events, commercial - let visitors explore your style and expertise.

Online Booking

Let clients check your availability and book sessions directly on your website. Reduce back-and-forth emails and never miss a booking opportunity.

Pricing Packages

Display clear pricing packages with what's included. Help potential clients self-qualify and understand your value before reaching out.

About / Bio Page

Photography is personal. Share your story, your approach, and what makes you unique. Clients want to connect with the person behind the camera.

Mobile Responsive

Most potential clients will find you on their phones. Your portfolio must look stunning and load fast on every device.

Contact Forms

Capture inquiries with smart contact forms that gather event details, dates, and budget. Get all the info you need to respond with a perfect proposal.

FAQ

Photography Website Questions

A photography website should include a homepage showcasing your best work, a portfolio gallery organized by category (weddings, portraits, events, etc.), an about/bio page with your story and approach, a pricing page with clear packages, a booking or contact page, and client testimonials. Additional pages for blog posts and individual galleries can strengthen your online presence and SEO.

Quality over quantity. Display your 20-30 best images overall, with additional curated galleries for each specialty (10-15 images per category). Only show work that represents the type of photography you want to book more of. Visitors form impressions quickly, so lead with your strongest images and remove anything that doesn't showcase your best work.

Yes. Displaying clear pricing packages helps qualify leads and saves time for both you and potential clients. Include starting prices, what's included in each package, and optional add-ons. If your pricing varies significantly by project, at least show starting prices or price ranges. Transparency builds trust and attracts clients who are ready to book.

Focus on local SEO by including your city and specialty in page titles and descriptions (e.g., "Austin Wedding Photographer"). Add alt text to all images describing the photo content. Create a Google Business Profile. Write blog posts about local venues and photography tips. Our templates are pre-configured with proper SEO structure so you can focus on creating content.

This reduces email back-and-forth and helps you book more sessions with less administrative work.
