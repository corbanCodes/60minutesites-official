Best Practices

What Makes a Great Insurance Website

Insurance websites that convert visitors into policyholders share these essential elements.

Quote Request Forms

Prominent quote forms on every page capture leads instantly. Collect the right information to provide accurate quotes without overwhelming visitors.

Trust Badges

Display licensing information, professional certifications, and industry memberships. Trust is everything in insurance - show visitors they can count on you.

Carrier Logos

Showcase the insurance carriers you represent. Recognized brand logos build instant credibility and show the range of options you can offer clients.

Agent Bio Section

People buy from people they trust. A professional bio with your photo, experience, and credentials creates a personal connection with potential clients.

Mobile Responsive

Most people research insurance on their phones. Your site must look professional and function perfectly on every device to capture mobile leads.

Client Testimonials

Reviews from satisfied clients build trust and overcome objections. Feature testimonials prominently and link to your Google reviews for social proof.

An insurance agent website should include a homepage with clear value proposition, quote request forms on every page, services pages for each insurance type you offer (auto, home, life, business), an about page with your bio and credentials, carrier logos you represent, client testimonials, and multiple contact options including phone, email, and contact forms. Trust badges showing your licensing and professional affiliations are essential for credibility.

Insurance websites generate leads through prominent quote request forms, click-to-call buttons for mobile visitors, live chat options, and downloadable resources like coverage guides. Every page should have a clear call-to-action. Track which pages generate the most leads and optimize them.
