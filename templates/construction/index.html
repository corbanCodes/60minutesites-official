Best Practices

What Makes a Great Construction Website

Construction websites that generate leads share these essential elements.

Project Portfolio

Showcase completed projects with before/after photos, scope descriptions, and location details. Let your work speak for itself.

License Display

Prominently display your contractor license number, insurance information, and safety certifications to build instant trust.

Service Area Map

Clearly define which cities and regions you serve. Help visitors quickly confirm you work in their area.

Visible Contact Info

Phone number in the header, contact forms on every page. Make it easy for potential clients to reach you.

Mobile Responsive

Property owners often research contractors on their phones. Your site must look great on every device.

Client Reviews

Display testimonials and link to Google reviews. Social proof is essential for high-value construction projects.

FAQ

Construction Website Questions

A construction website should include a homepage, services page detailing your capabilities, project portfolio showcasing completed work, about page with company history and team info, and contact page with multiple ways to reach you. Additional pages for specific service areas, individual project case studies, and testimonials strengthen your online presence.

Document each project with quality photos showing different stages and the finished result. Include before/after comparisons when relevant. Write descriptions covering project scope, challenges overcome, and location. Organize projects by type (residential, commercial, renovations) so visitors can find relevant examples. Our templates include built-in portfolio layouts optimized for construction work.

Yes. Serious clients will verify your credentials before making contact. Displaying your contractor license number, insurance information, and any relevant certifications builds trust immediately. Our construction templates include dedicated sections for credentials and trust badges.

List the cities, counties, and regions you serve prominently on your homepage and in a dedicated service area section. For larger service areas, consider individual pages for major cities you target. Include a map showing your coverage area. Clear service area information helps visitors quickly confirm you work in their location.

Most construction companies do not list specific prices because project costs vary significantly based on scope, materials, and site conditions. However, you can provide general information about how pricing works, what factors affect costs, or starting price ranges for common project types. This helps qualify leads and set expectations.
