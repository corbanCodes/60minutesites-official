Best Practices

What Makes a Great Plumber Website

Plumber websites that generate calls share these essential elements.

Emergency Contact Visibility

Sticky header with phone number, prominent 24/7 emergency callouts, and click-to-call buttons. When pipes burst at 2AM, customers need to reach you instantly.

Service Area Display

Clearly show which cities and zip codes you serve. Help visitors quickly confirm you work in their area before they call.

License & Insurance Badges

Prominently display your plumbing license number, insurance information, and certifications. Build trust before customers let you into their homes.

Mobile Responsive

Most emergency plumbing searches happen on phones at 2AM. Your site must load fast and look great on every mobile device.

Online Scheduling

Let customers book appointments directly from your website. Reduce phone tag and capture leads even when you are on a job.

Customer Reviews

Display testimonials and link to Google reviews. Social proof is essential when homeowners are choosing who to trust with their plumbing.

FAQ

Plumber Website Questions

A plumber website should include a homepage with emergency contact prominently displayed, a services page detailing all plumbing services you offer (drain cleaning, water heaters, pipe repair, etc.), a service areas page listing cities and zip codes you cover, an about page with your license and insurance information, and a contact page with multiple ways to reach you. Individual service pages for specific offerings like water heater installation or sewer line repair help with local SEO.

Plumbing emergencies do not wait for business hours. When someone has a burst pipe, overflowing toilet, or sewage backup at 2AM, they need to find your phone number instantly. A sticky header that keeps your phone number visible while scrolling, prominent 24/7 emergency service callouts, and click-to-call buttons on mobile are essential. The faster they can contact you, the more likely they are to call you instead of a competitor.

Absolutely. Homeowners want to verify they are hiring a licensed, insured plumber before letting someone into their home to work on their water and sewage systems. Displaying your plumbing license number, liability insurance, and any certifications or bonding builds immediate trust. It also helps you stand out from unlicensed competitors and can be required by law in many areas.

List the cities, zip codes, and neighborhoods you serve prominently on your homepage and in a dedicated service area section. For larger service areas, consider individual pages for each major city you target. This helps with local SEO so people searching for "plumber in [city name]" can find you. Include a map showing your coverage area and be specific about response times for different locations.

Most plumbing companies do not list specific prices because job costs vary based on the problem, parts needed, and time required. However, you can mention service call fees, provide general price ranges for common services, or explain how pricing works. Transparency about pricing approach helps set expectations and can qualify leads before they call.
