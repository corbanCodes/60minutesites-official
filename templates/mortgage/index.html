Best Practices

What Makes a Great Mortgage Website

Mortgage websites that generate qualified leads share these essential elements.

Loan Calculator

Interactive mortgage calculators help borrowers estimate payments, understand affordability, and engage with your site before contacting you.

Application Forms

Streamlined online application forms capture lead information and pre-qualify borrowers, saving time for both you and your clients.

Rate Information

Display current rates and loan programs clearly. Borrowers compare rates across lenders, so transparency builds trust and attracts serious inquiries.

Credentials Display

Prominently display your NMLS number, state licenses, and lender affiliations. Compliance is required, and credentials build borrower confidence.

Mobile Responsive

Borrowers research mortgages on phones during their home search. Your site must perform flawlessly on every device and screen size.

Client Testimonials

Real reviews from satisfied borrowers provide social proof that helps new clients trust you with their home financing decisions.

Mortgage Website Questions

A mortgage broker website should include a loan calculator, online application or pre-qualification forms, current rate information, NMLS and licensing credentials, loan program comparisons, client testimonials, and easy contact options. These elements help potential borrowers evaluate their options and build trust in your services before reaching out.

Yes. Federal law requires mortgage loan originators to display their NMLS unique identifier on all advertising, including websites. You must also include state license information where applicable. Our mortgage templates include dedicated areas for your NMLS number and licensing information to ensure compliance with SAFE Act requirements.

Absolutely. Loan calculators are one of the most valuable features on a mortgage website. They engage visitors, help them understand their potential monthly payments and buying power, and generate qualified leads. Visitors who use calculators are often further along in the buying process. Include a pre-qualification form that collects essential information like loan amount, property type, credit score range, and contact details. Keep the initial form simple to reduce friction, then follow up for detailed documentation. Our templates integrate with common CRM and loan origination systems to streamline your workflow.
